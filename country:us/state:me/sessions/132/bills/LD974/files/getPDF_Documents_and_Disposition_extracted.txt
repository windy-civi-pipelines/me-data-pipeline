1 Sec. 1. Maine Promise Initiative. Resolved: That the Department of Education
2 shall create a program to be known as the Maine Promise Initiative, referred to in this
3 resolve as "the initiative." The primary goal of the initiative is to have, by 2030, at least
4 95% of the State's secondary school students graduate from high school in either a 4-year
5 or a 6-year time period. The initiative must include additional 2030 graduation rate goals,
6 including increasing graduation rates for identified populations that are below the 2025
7 graduation rates of the general population of secondary school students. As used in this
8 resolve, "identified populations" includes, but is not limited to, students who are unhoused,
9 Native American students, students in the child welfare system and students in the juvenile
10 justice system. The department shall convene a working group of interested stakeholders
11 to develop a strategic plan to implement the initiative and achieve its goals. The department
12 shall report on its progress in developing the strategic plan and implementing the initiative
13 and its recommendations, including any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing
14 Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs by December 3, 2025. The joint standing
15 committee may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
16 Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following
17 appropriations and allocations are made.

20 Initiative: Provides one-time funds to support the creation of the Maine Promise Initiative
21 with a goal of having, by 2030, at least 95% of the State's secondary school students
22 graduate from high school in either a 4-year or a 6-year time period.
