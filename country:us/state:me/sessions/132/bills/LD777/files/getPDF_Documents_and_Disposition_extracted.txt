132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 777
H.P. 502 House of Representatives, March 4, 2025
Received by the Clerk of the House on February 26, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Inland Fisheries and Wildlife pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint
Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative MASON of Lisbon.
Cosponsored by Senator BLACK of Franklin and
Representatives: BOYER of Poland, CAMPBELL of Orrington, COLLAMORE of Pittsfield,
DRINKWATER of Milford, FAULKINGHAM of Winter Harbor, FOSTER of Dexter,
GUERRETTE of Caribou, THORNE of Carmel.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §11404, sub-§1-A, as enacted by PL 2005, c. 280, §2, is amended
3 by amending the first blocked paragraph to read:
4 The commissioner may terminate this open season at any time in an area if, in the
5 commissioner's opinion, an immediate emergency action is necessary due to adverse
6 weather conditions or severe hunting pressure. The length of the special muzzle-loading
7 season is as follows must be the same in all regions of the State and established by rule.
8 Sec. 2. 12 MRSA §11404, sub-§1-A, as enacted by PL 2005, c. 280, §2, is amended
9 by amending the 2nd blocked paragraph to read:
10 The commissioner shall establish by rule the length of the special muzzle-loading season.
11 The commissioner may establish seasons of different lengths in different regions of the
12 State. The season may extend for no more than 12 hunting days in any part of the State.
13 SUMMARY
1144 This bill establishes that the muzzle-loading-only open season on deer must be the same
1155 in all areas of the State.
