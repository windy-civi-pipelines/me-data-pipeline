9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 881, L.D. 1358, “An Act to Reduce
10 Electricity Rates by Removing Limitations on the Ownership of Generation by an Affiliate
11 of an Investor-owned Transmission and Distribution Utility”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, to Direct the Public Utilities Commission to Evaluate Transmission and
14 Distribution Utility Affiliate Interconnection Standards'
15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
16 'Sec. 1. Investor-owned transmission and distribution utility affiliate
17 interconnection standards; evaluation. Resolved: That the Public Utilities
18 Commission shall evaluate standards governing the interconnection of generation or
19 generation-related assets owned by an affiliate of an investor-owned transmission and
20 distribution utility, as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 3204,
21 subsection 11, with facilities owned or operated by that investor-owned transmission and
22 distribution utility. In conducting its evaluation, the commission may develop
23 recommendations for standards that are designed to ensure full and fair competition in the
24 electricity market in the State and protect the interests of electricity ratepayers. In its
25 evaluation, the commission shall consider:
26 1. Standards for ensuring arm's length transactions between an investor-owned
27 transmission and distribution utility and an affiliate of that utility if the affiliate develops
28 and owns generation or generation-related assets capable of interconnecting into the
29 territory of the investor-owned transmission and distribution utility;
30 2. Any other standards applicable to affiliate interconnections based on existing laws
31 of the State, practices in other states and federal and regional rules and procedures;
32 3. Standards or procedures, including consideration of any necessary legislative
33 changes, to ensure that electricity ratepayers are held harmless for costs associated with
34 affiliate interconnection; and

1 4. Recommendations and findings in reports issued as a result of past studies regarding
2 affiliate interconnection, including whether any recommendations in those reports are still
3 advisable.
4 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That, by March 15, 2026, the Public Utilities
5 Commission shall submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and
6 Technology that includes a summary of the commission's evaluation conducted in
7 accordance with section 1, including any findings and recommendations. The committee
8 may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature related to the
9 commission's report.'
10 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
11 number to read consecutively.
12 SUMMARY
1133 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve that requires the Public Utilities
1144 Commission to evaluate standards governing the interconnection of generation or
1155 generation-related assets owned by an affiliate of an investor-owned transmission and
1166 distribution utility with facilities owned or operated by that investor-owned transmission
1177 and distribution utility. In conducting its evaluation, the commission may develop
1188 recommendations for standards that are designed to ensure full and fair competition in the
1199 electricity market in the State and protect the interests of electricity ratepayers. By March
2200 15, 2026, the commission must submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy,
2211 Utilities and Technology that includes a summary of the commission's evaluation,
2222 including any findings and recommendations.
