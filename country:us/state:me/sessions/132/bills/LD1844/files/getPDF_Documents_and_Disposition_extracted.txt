3 15. Reentry services. Within the limits of available resources, the commissioner shall
4 establish and maintain a reentry services program for residents of correctional facilities that
5 makes available, as appropriate, reentry services in the following areas:
6 A. Health and wellness, including, but not limited to, accessing available MaineCare
7 and veterans' benefits and connecting with community providers and other resources
8 for treating and managing substance use disorder and maintaining physical and mental
9 health;
10 B. Financial literacy and planning, including, but not limited to, information about
11 opening a bank account, managing a budget and building and maintaining credit;
12 C. Employment preparation, including, but not limited to, writing a resume, searching
13 for employment, information about obtaining a driver's license, information about
14 obtaining work-related certification or licensure and locating and securing housing;
15 D. Community and family integration, including, but not limited to, voter registration
16 information, information about services offered through the statewide 2-1-1 telephone
17 number designated pursuant to Title 35-A, section 7108 and connecting with
18 community support programs, including programs offering education on conflict
19 resolution, establishing and maintaining relationships and rejection management
20 techniques; and
21 E. Technology training, including, but not limited to, information about computer
22 literacy programs and avoiding Internet scams.
23 SUMMARY
2244 This bill directs the Commissioner of Corrections to establish and maintain reentry
2255 services for residents of correctional facilities. Reentry services must include services
2266 related to health and wellness, financial literacy and planning, employment preparation,
2277 community and family integration and technology training.
