9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 167, L.D. 264, “An Act to Remove the
10 12-month Waiting Period for the Maine Resident Homestead Property Tax Exemption”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Waive the 12-month Waiting Period for the Maine Resident Homestead
13 Property Tax Exemption for Certain Heirs of Property for Which an Exemption Was
14 Established'
15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
16 following:
17 'Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §683, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2017, c. 478, §1, is further
18 amended to read:
19 1. Exemption amount. Except for assessments for special benefits, the just value of
20 $10,000 of the homestead of a permanent resident of this State who has owned a homestead
21 in this State for the preceding 12 months is exempt from taxation. Notwithstanding this
22 subsection, a permanent resident of this State who loses ownership of a homestead in this
23 State due to a tax lien foreclosure and subsequently regains ownership of the homestead
24 from the municipality that foreclosed on the tax lien is deemed to have continuously owned
25 the homestead and may not be determined ineligible for the exemption provided in this
26 section due to the ownership of the homestead by the municipality. In determining the
27 local assessed value of the exemption, the assessor shall multiply the amount of the
28 exemption by the ratio of current just value upon which the assessment is based as furnished
29 in the assessor's annual return pursuant to section 383. If the title to the homestead is held
30 by the applicant jointly or in common with others, the exemption may not exceed $10,000
31 of the just value of the homestead, but may be apportioned among the owners who reside
32 on the property to the extent of their respective interests. A municipality responsible for
33 administering the homestead exemption has no obligation to create separate accounts for
34 each partial interest in a homestead owned jointly or in common.
35 Sec. 2. 36 MRSA §683, sub-§1-C is enacted to read:

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 167, L.D. 264
1 1-C. Exceptions to residency requirement. The following are exceptions to the
2 requirement in subsection 1 that a person own a homestead in this State for the preceding
3 12 months in order to be eligible for the exemption provided in this section.
4 A. Notwithstanding subsection 1, a permanent resident of this State who loses
5 ownership of a homestead in this State due to a tax lien foreclosure and subsequently
6 regains ownership of the homestead from the municipality that foreclosed on the tax
7 lien is deemed to have continuously owned the homestead and may not be determined
8 ineligible for the exemption provided in this section due to the ownership of the
9 homestead by the municipality.
10 B. Notwithstanding subsection 1, a successor in interest who is a surviving heir of a
11 permanent resident of this State who had ownership of a homestead at the time of death
12 is deemed to have continuously owned the homestead and may not be determined
13 ineligible for the exemption provided in this section due to the change in ownership at
14 the death of the prior owner, as long as that heir can establish that the heir has been a
15 permanent resident of the municipality in which the homestead is located for 12 months
16 prior to the death of the prior owner.
17 Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
18 allocations are made.
19 ADMINISTRATIVE AND FINANCIAL SERVICES, DEPARTMENT OF
20 Revenue Services, Bureau of 0002
21 Initiative: Provides funding for increased homestead property tax exemption
22 reimbursement to municipalities.

30 SUMMARY
3311 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. The amendment provides an
3322 exception to the 12-month waiting period for the Maine resident homestead property tax
3333 exemption to a successor in interest of a deceased resident who had ownership of a
3344 homestead at the time of death as long as that successor has been a permanent resident of
3355 the municipality in which the deceased's homestead is located for the 12 months prior to
3366 the death of the prior owner. The amendment also makes a technical change to the statute
3377 establishing the exemption and adds an appropriations and allocations section.
