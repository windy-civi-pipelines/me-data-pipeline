27 SUMMARY
2288 This amendment makes the following changes to the bill.
29 1. It makes a technical correction to continue to include reports filed by ballot question
30 committees in the prohibition on the use, for any commercial purpose, of information
31 concerning contributors contained in that report.
32 2. It makes technical adjustments to references to cable television and broadcast
33 television to include satellite systems in order to reflect the language in the definition of
34 "public communication" contained in the bill.
35 3. It changes the requirements for when the top 3 funders statement is necessary for
36 public communications funded by an entity making an independent expenditure. In current
37 law, for cable television, broadcast television or Internet video communication 30 seconds
38 or less in duration, the audible statement may be modified to include only the single top
39 funder. The amendment increases the communication duration to 60 seconds or less and
40 removes the requirement that any audible statement be included on such communications.
41 A written statement is still required.

5 5. It also clarifies that, when referring to public communications to influence ballot
6 question elections, the criteria for text messages to clearly and conspicuously state the name
7 of the person who made or financed the expenditure also include the transmission of text
8 messages with the assistance of mass distribution technology.
