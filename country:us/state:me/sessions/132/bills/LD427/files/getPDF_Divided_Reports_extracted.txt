2 Date: (Filing No. H- )
4 STATE OF MAINE
5 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
6 132ND LEGISLATURE
7 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
8 HOUSE AMENDMENT “ ” to COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “A” to H.P. 281,
9 L.D. 427, “An Act to Prohibit Mandatory Parking Space Minimums in State and Municipal
10 Building Codes”
11 Amend the amendment in section 1 in §4364-D by striking out all of subsections 1 and
12 2 (page 1, lines 17 to 30 in amendment) and inserting the following:
13 '1. Definition. For purposes of this section, "parking agreement" means a legally
14 binding agreement between a property developer and the owner of an off-site parking
15 facility to provide required parking spaces within 0.25 miles of a development site.'
16 Amend the amendment in section 1 in §4364-D by striking out all of subsection 5 (page
17 2, lines 11 to 15 in amendment) and inserting the following:
18 '5. Rulemaking. The Maine Office of Community Affairs may adopt rules to
19 administer and enforce this section. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine
20 technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.'
21 Amend the amendment in section 1 in §4364-D by renumbering the subsections to read
22 consecutively.
23 Amend the amendment by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
24 section number to read consecutively.
25 SUMMARY
2266 This amendment removes the provision of Committee Amendment "A" that prohibits
2277 a municipality from requiring a developer to provide off-street parking within 0.25 miles
2288 of a transit stop. This amendment also changes the entity responsible for rulemaking to the
2299 Maine Office of Community Affairs.
