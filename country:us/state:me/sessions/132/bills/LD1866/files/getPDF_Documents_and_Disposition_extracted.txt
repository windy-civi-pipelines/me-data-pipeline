Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1866
S.P. 736 In Senate, May 5, 2025
Advocating for Individuals with Serious Mental Illness
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on May 1, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Health and Human Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator CARNEY of Cumberland.
Cosponsored by Representative MEYER of Eliot and
Senators: BENNETT of Oxford, MOORE of Washington, STEWART of Aroostook,
Representatives: DEBRITO of Waterville, GRAHAM of North Yarmouth, HENDERSON of
Rumford, MCCABE of Lewiston, ZAGER of Portland.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 34-B MRSA §3012 is enacted to read:
3 §3012. Advocacy agency for individuals with serious mental illness
4 1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A. "Designated nonstate mental health institution" has the same meaning as in section
7 3801, subsection 1-A.
8 B. "Nonstate mental health institution" has the same meaning as in section 3801,
9 subsection 6.
10 C. "State mental health institute" has the same meaning as in section 3801, subsection
11 9.
12 2. Protection and advocacy agency services. The department shall contract with,
13 and make a good faith effort to obtain appropriations sufficient to fund, the agency
14 designated pursuant to Title 5, section 19502, referred to in this section as "the agency,"
15 for the provision of 6 full-time advocates to provide the following advocacy services on a
16 statewide basis to individuals with serious mental illness who are residing in those
17 individuals' communities or who are hospitalized at a state mental health institute, a
18 nonstate mental health institution or a designated nonstate mental health institution.
19 A. The agency shall receive complaints made by or on behalf of individuals with
20 serious mental illness and represent those individuals' interests in any matter pertaining
21 to those individuals' rights, including complaints regarding timely access to mental
22 health services in those individuals' communities under paragraph G.
23 B. The agency may pursue in its own name or on behalf of an individual with serious
24 mental illness any legal, administrative or other appropriate remedies to ensure the
25 protection of and advocacy for the rights of individuals with serious mental illness who
26 are or may be eligible for services administered, licensed or funded by the department,
27 except that the agency may refuse to take action on any complaint that it considers to
28 be trivial, to be moot or to lack merit or for which resources are insufficient or for
29 which there is clearly another remedy available.
30 C. The agency may refer individuals with serious mental illness to other agencies or
31 entities and collaborate with those agencies or entities for the purpose of advocating
32 for the rights of those individuals.
33 D. The agency shall act as an information source regarding the rights of individuals
34 with serious mental illness and stay informed about the laws, administrative rules and
35 institutional and other policies relating to the rights and dignity of those individuals
36 and about relevant legal decisions and other developments related to mental health,
37 intellectual disabilities and autism spectrum disorder, both in this State and in other
38 parts of the country.
39 E. The agency may make and publish reports necessary to the performance of the
40 duties described in this section. The agency may report its findings to groups outside
41 the department, such as legislative bodies, advisory committees, commissions, law
42 enforcement agencies and the press. At least annually, the agency shall report both in
Page 1 - 132LR1008(01)
413 person and in writing to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having
424 jurisdiction over health and human services matters and the department regarding the
435 performance of the duties described in this section.
4 F. The agency may monitor the delivery of services, supports and other assistance or
5 residential services or treatment provided to individuals with serious mental illness for
6 the purpose of ensuring that services, supports and assistance meet the needs of those
7 individuals and are delivered in conformity with laws, regulations, rules and other
8 standards regarding quality of care.
9 G. The agency shall timely report to the department any complaints regarding
10 infringement of the rights of individuals with serious mental illness and with any other
11 obligations set forth in statute, rule or contract related to providing mental health
12 services to individuals with serious mental illness by other agencies licensed or funded
13 by the department to provide services in those individuals' communities.
14 3. Access to medical records and files. In addition to the agency's authority to access
15 records under Title 5, chapter 511 and 42 United States Code, Chapter 114, the department
16 through a contract shall grant the agency access to medical records and files related to care
17 exclusively for individuals hospitalized in a state mental health institute.
18 4. Confidentiality. The following provisions govern confidentiality.
19 A. Any request by or on behalf of an individual with serious mental illness for action
20 by the agency and all written records or accounts related to the request, including the
21 identity of the individual, are confidential.
22 B. Records maintained by the agency are the property of the agency, and the agency
23 shall protect them from loss, damage, tampering or use by unauthorized individuals
24 and may release them only as provided by law.
25 5. Conflict with federal law. In the case of any conflict between the provisions of
26 the contract under this section and with 42 United States Code, Chapter 114 and regulations
27 promulgated thereunder, the federal statute or regulation controls.
28 SUMMARY
2299 This bill requires in statute that the Department of Health and Human Services contract
3300 with an independent agency to provide advocacy services on a statewide basis to
3311 individuals with serious mental illness who are residing in those individuals' communities
3322 or who are hospitalized.
