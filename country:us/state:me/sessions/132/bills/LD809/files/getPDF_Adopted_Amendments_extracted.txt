8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 516, L.D. 809, “An Act to Allow a
10 Motion to Extend a Protection from Abuse Order After Expiration of the Original Order”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 19-A MRSA §4110, sub-§5, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 647, Pt. A, §3
14 and affected by Pt. B, §65, is amended to read:
15 A. A final protection order issued under this chapter must be for a fixed period not to
16 exceed 2 years, unless extended or reinstated by the court pursuant to section 4111.
17 Sec. 2. 19-A MRSA §4111, sub-§1-A is enacted to read:
18 1-A. Extension after expiration. The court may reinstate and extend a final
19 protection order issued under this chapter after expiration, upon motion of the plaintiff,
20 only if the court finds:
21 A. That the plaintiff has shown good cause for filing the motion to extend a final
22 protection order after expiration of the order, which may include that the filing was not
23 timely due to a dismissal of a prior motion to extend because of an inability to serve
24 the defendant; and
25 B. That the filing under this subsection was made within a reasonable amount of time
26 based upon the underlying circumstances.
27 If the court makes findings for the plaintiff under paragraphs A and B, the court may
28 reinstate the final protection order that has expired until a hearing under section 4109,
29 subsection 1 on the motion to extend and shall apply the standard in subsection 1 to
30 determine whether an extension of the final protection order is necessary to protect the
31 plaintiff or minor child from abuse or conduct specified in section 4103.'
32 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
33 number to read consecutively.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 516, L.D. 809
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment clarifies that the court has discretion to decide whether a plaintiff has
33 demonstrated good cause for filing a motion to extend a final protection from abuse order
44 after the order has expired. It also authorizes the court, in its discretion, to reinstate the
55 expired order pending a hearing on the motion to extend the order.
