16 Amend the bill by striking out all of sections 4 to 9 and inserting the following:
17 'Sec. 4. 17-A MRSA §853, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2023, c. 316, §6, is further
18 amended to read:
19 1. A person is guilty of sex trafficking if:
20 A. The person knowingly promotes prostitution. Violation of this paragraph is a Class
21 D C crime for which, in addition to any other authorized penalties, a fine of $10,000
22 for an individual and $20,000 for an organization must be adjudged; or
23 B. The person violates paragraph A and has 2 or more prior convictions in this State
24 for any combination of the Maine offenses listed in this paragraph or for engaging in
25 substantially similar conduct to that of the Maine offenses listed in this paragraph in
26 another jurisdiction. The Maine offenses are any violation of this section or section
27 852, 853‑B or 855 or attempts to commit any of these crimes. Section 9‑A governs the
28 use of prior convictions when determining a sentence. Violation of this paragraph is a
29 Class C B crime for which, in addition to any other authorized penalties, a fine of
30 $25,000 for an individual and $50,000 for an organization must be adjudged.
31 Sec. 5. Mandated reporter curriculum review and update. The Department
32 of Health and Human Services shall convene a working group to review the department-
33 approved training that mandated reporters must complete at least once every 4 years
34 pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 4011-A, subsection 9 and shall
35 invite representatives of child advocacy centers, community-based agencies with expertise

327 to participate in the working group. The department shall report the working group's

7 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
8 number to read consecutively.
9 SUMMARY
1100 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, removes the provisions
1111 of the bill that establish mandatory minimum jail sentences and mandatory restitution for
1122 persons convicted of sex trafficking or aggravated sex trafficking. The amendment retains
1133 the provisions of the bill that increase from a Class D crime to a Class C crime a first
1144 offense of sex trafficking and increase from a Class C crime to a Class B crime a 3rd or
1155 subsequent sex trafficking offense. The amendment also retains the provisions of the bill
1166 that impose mandatory fines for sex trafficking and aggravated sex trafficking offenses and
1177 that increase the amount a person convicted of sex trafficking or aggravated sex trafficking
1188 must pay to the Victims' Compensation Fund.
19 The amendment further replaces the provision of the bill requiring certain professionals
20 to receive human trafficking awareness training every 2 years and instead directs the
21 Department of Health and Human Services to convene a working group to review the
22 department-approved training that mandated reporters must complete at least once every 4
23 years pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 4011-A, subsection 9. The
24 department must report the working group’s findings and recommendations to the joint
25 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services
26 matters by February 15, 2027. The joint standing committee may report out legislation
27 related to the subject matter of the report to the 133rd Legislature in 2027.
