Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 584
S.P. 267 In Senate, February 25, 2025
Appointed Position Subject to Confirmation by the Legislature
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on February 19, 2025. Referred to the Committee
on Veterans and Legal Affairs pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator HICKMAN of Kennebec.
Cosponsored by Representative SUPICA of Bangor and
Senators: President DAUGHTRY of Cumberland, DUSON of Cumberland, TALBOT ROSS
of Cumberland, TIMBERLAKE of Androscoggin, Representatives: BOYER of Poland,
FAULKINGHAM of Winter Harbor, FREDERICKS of Sanford, RUDNICKI of Fairfield.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 28-B MRSA §104-B, first ¶, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 679, Pt. B, §6, is
3 amended to read:
4 The Governor shall appoint the Director of the Office of Cannabis Policy, referred to
5 in this chapter as "the director," subject to review by the joint standing committee of the
6 Legislature having jurisdiction over cannabis matters and to confirmation by the
7 Legislature. The Director of the Office of Cannabis Policy, referred to in this chapter as
8 "the director," may employ personnel as necessary to implement, administer and enforce
9 this chapter and the rules adopted pursuant to this chapter and the Maine Medical Use of
10 Cannabis Act and the rules adopted pursuant to that Act.
11 Sec. 2. Appointment. The Governor shall appoint the Director of the Office of
12 Cannabis Policy pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 28-B, section 104-B no later
13 than 60 days after the effective date of this Act.
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill provides that the Director of the Office of Cannabis Policy within the
1166 Department of Administrative and Financial Services is appointed by the Governor and
1177 that the appointment is subject to review by the joint standing committee of the Legislature
1188 having jurisdiction over cannabis matters and to confirmation by the Legislature. The bill
1199 also requires the Governor to appoint the Director of the Office of Cannabis Policy no later
2200 than 60 days after the effective date of this legislation.
