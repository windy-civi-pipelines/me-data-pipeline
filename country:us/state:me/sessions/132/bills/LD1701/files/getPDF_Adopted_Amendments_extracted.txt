5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1136, L.D. 1701, “An Act to Support
10 the Implementation of Learning Standards and Results in Public Schools”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, Directing the Department of Education to Establish a Working Group to
13 Study the Maine Learning Results'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
15 'Sec. 1. Department of Education to convene working group to study Maine
16 learning results; membership. Resolved: That the Department of Education shall
17 convene a working group to study the system of learning results established pursuant to the
18 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 6209, referred to in this resolve as "the Maine
19 learning results." Membership of the working group must include, but is not limited to:
20 1. Educators working with grade levels and subject matter included in the Maine
21 learning results;
22 2. Education experts from the cultures, communities and traditions represented in
23 Maine’s classrooms; and
24 3. Representation from a statewide curriculum leaders' organization.
25 Sec. 2. Study requirements. Resolved: That the study performed by the working
26 group pursuant to section 1 must include, but is not limited to:
27 1. A review of the purpose of the Maine learning results and an evaluation of its
28 efficacy in achieving that purpose;
29 2. An examination of the overall structure, organization and accessibility of the Maine
30 learning results, including how its format supports coherence and interdisciplinary
31 learning;
32 3. An assessment of the inclusion, accuracy and representation of diverse cultures,
33 communities and traditions across all content areas, including, but not limited to, Wabanaki

314 studies, African American studies and Asian American, Native Hawaiian and Pacific
325 Islander studies;
3 4. A review of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 6209 to assess whether
4 statutory revisions are necessary; and
5 5. The findings and recommendations of the working group, including any suggested
6 legislation necessary to support the implementation of the working group's
7 recommendations.
8 Sec. 3. Interim report. Resolved: That the Department of Education shall, no
9 later than February 1, 2026, submit an interim report of the working group's activities,
10 progress and initial findings and recommendations to the Joint Standing Committee on
11 Education and Cultural Affairs. The committee may report out a bill related to the interim
12 report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
13 Sec. 4. Final report. Resolved: That the Department of Education shall, no later
14 than February 1, 2027, submit a final report to the joint standing committee of the
15 Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters. The final report must include the
16 working group's findings related to each component of the review described in section 2
17 and any recommendations related to revisions to the Maine learning results, including any
18 recommendations to amend the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 6209. If
19 revisions to the Maine learning results are recommended, the report must include a detailed
20 plan and proposed timeline for implementing those revisions. The joint standing committee
21 may report out a bill related to the final report to the 133rd Legislature in 2027.'
22 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
23 number to read consecutively.
24 SUMMARY
2255 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve. The amendment directs the
2266 Department of Education to convene a working group to study the State's system of
2277 learning results, referred to as the Maine learning results. The study must include, but is
2288 not limited to, a review of the purpose of the Maine learning results and the system's
2299 efficacy in achieving that purpose; an examination of its structure, organization and
3300 accessibility; an assessment of the inclusion, accuracy and representation of diverse
3311 cultures, communities and traditions across all content areas; and a review of the Maine
3322 Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 6209. The department is required to submit an interim
3333 report of the working group's progress and initial findings to the Joint Standing Committee
3344 on Education and Cultural Affairs no later than February 1, 2026. A final report must be
3355 submitted by the department no later than February 1, 2027 to the joint standing committee
3366 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters.
