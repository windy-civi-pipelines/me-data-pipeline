8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 50, L.D. 124, “An Act to Protect the
10 Right to Food”
11 Amend the bill by inserting after section 19 the following:
12 'Sec. 20. 30-A MRSA §7505, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 625, §6, is amended to
13 read:
14 §7505. Ordinances authorized under the Maine Food Sovereignty Act
15 The county commissioners of each county may adopt ordinances regarding traditional
16 foodways and direct producer-to-consumer transactions in accordance with Title 7, chapter
17 8‑F. Ordinances adopted by the county commissioners govern traditional foodways and
18 direct producer-to-consumer transactions in any unorganized territory within the county
19 whose residents have opted, in a manner prescribed by the county commissioners, to have
20 the ordinance apply in that unorganized territory.'
21 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
22 number to read consecutively.
23 SUMMARY
2244 This amendment clarifies that each county may adopt ordinances regarding traditional
2255 foodways in addition to direct producer-to-consumer transactions in accordance with the
2266 Maine Food Sovereignty Act.

2288 a potential state mandate. In order to be a mandate pursuant to the Constitution of Maine,
2299 a provision must require a local unit of government to expand or modify its activities so as
3300 to necessitate additional expenditures from local revenue. The committee finds that the
3311 section of the amendment relating to the Maine Vegetable Garden Protection Act, the
