1 L.D. 1234
2 Date: (Filing No. H- )
3 EDUCATION AND CULTURAL AFFAIRS
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 809, L.D. 1234, “An Act to Ban Cellular
10 Telephones from Public School Classrooms”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act Regarding Cellular Telephones in Public Schools'
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
14 following:
15 'Sec. 1. 20-A MRSA §1001, sub-§23 is enacted to read:
16 23. Cellular telephone policy. By August 1, 2026, a school board shall adopt and
17 implement a policy related to use of personal electronic devices during the school day. The
18 policy must include, but is not limited to, provisions related to student use of cellular
19 telephones and wearable electronic devices with Internet or cellular network connectivity
20 capabilities.'
21 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
22 number to read consecutively.
23 SUMMARY
2244 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. The amendment provides that,
2255 by August 1, 2026, a school board must adopt a policy related to use of personal electronic
2266 devices during the school day. The policy must include, but is not limited to, provisions
2277 related to student use of cellular telephones and wearable electronic devices.
