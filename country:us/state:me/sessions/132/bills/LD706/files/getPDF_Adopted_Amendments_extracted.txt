24 except for compensation paid to sole proprietors of or partners in the client company.

26 under its state employer identification number, using its contribution rate. The
27 employee leasing company shall keep separate records and submit separate quarterly
28 wage reports to the Bureau of Unemployment Compensation for each of its client
29 companies.'
30 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
31 number to read consecutively.
32 SUMMARY
3333 This amendment makes the following changes to the bill.
34 1. It removes the authorization of the Department of Labor, Bureau of Unemployment
35 Compensation to use facial recognition software for identity verification purposes.
36 2. It provides that, in addition to an individual who is a member of a bona fide labor
37 union, an individual who is represented by a bona fide labor union or who is otherwise
38 authorized to use the services of a bona fide union hiring hall, who maintains contact with
39 that union and who uses and complies with the placement services of the union hiring hall
40 in seeking work is considered to be actively seeking work for the purposes of the Maine
41 Revised Statutes, Title 26, section 1192-A.
Page 2 - 132LR0140(02)

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 445, L.D. 706
1 3. It removes the requirement that an individual enrolled in a training program under
2 the Governor’s Jobs Initiative Program, established in Title 26, section 2031, or the
3 Competitive Skills Scholarship Program, established in Title 26, section 2033, qualifies for
4 a waiver of eligibility requirements to collect unemployment benefits as long as the training
5 is in accordance with rules adopted by the Commissioner of Labor.
6 4. It also provides that the bureau may not withhold payment for claimants receiving
7 benefits due to the claimant's failure to adequately respond to a written request for
8 documentation to verify the claimant's identity unless the bureau has credible evidence that
9 substantiates a reasonable basis for establishing an issue of potential fraud and withholding
10 payment. If there is evidence, the bureau must issue timely notice to the claimant informing
11 the claimant that benefit payments are withheld and instructions to the claimant on how the
12 claimant may satisfy identity verification requirements and where to submit that
13 documentation. The notice must include a reasonable time frame and the consequences for
14 failing to provide that documentation. The bureau must allow individuals a reasonable time
15 frame to submit documents that comply with an identity verification request. The bureau
16 must also provide a list of acceptable documents and allow multiple methods to submit
17 documents for identity verification. If the claimant fails to comply with the request for
18 documents in the time frame provided by the bureau or if the documents are insufficient or
19 determined to be fraudulent, the bureau must render a timely decision denying further
20 benefits.
21 5. It also provides that the changes made to the provision of law governing employee
22 leasing company reports and contributions begin January 1, 2026. It also makes a technical
23 change in Title 32 referencing employee leasing companies.
