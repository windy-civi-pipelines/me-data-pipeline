5 STATE OF MAINE

7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 262, L.D. 408, “An Act to Allow
10 Unaccompanied and Emancipated Minors to Access Their Vital Records At No Cost”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §2706, sub-§5-B is enacted to read:
14 5-B. Disclosure of records regarding certain minors. Certified or noncertified
15 copies of vital records of an unaccompanied minor or emancipated minor must be made
16 available to the minor and other individuals in accordance with the requirements of
17 subsection 5 or, with the written permission of the minor, to the director of an emergency
18 shelter program, runaway or homeless youth services organization or continuum of care
19 agency at which the minor is a client, or the director's designee, or to a social worker, school
20 administrator or teacher providing services to the minor. The state registrar shall make
21 available the vital records of an unaccompanied minor or emancipated minor to the minor
22 at no cost. For the purposes of this subsection, "unaccompanied minor" means a person
23 who has not attained 18 years of age who is not accompanied by a parent or guardian at the
24 time that the minor makes the request to obtain the minor's vital records or gives written
25 permission for a person authorized under this subsection to receive the records on the
26 minor's behalf. For the purposes of this subsection, "emancipated minor" means an
27 individual ordered emancipated in accordance with Title 15, section 3506-A.'
28 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
29 number to read consecutively.
30 SUMMARY
3311 This amendment replaces the bill. It requires that the State Registrar of Vital Statistics,
3322 with the written permission of the minor, make certified or noncertified copies of vital
3333 records of an unaccompanied or emancipated minor available at any reasonable time upon
3344 the request of the director of an emergency shelter program, runaway or homeless youth
3355 services organization or continuum of care agency at which the minor is a client, or the
