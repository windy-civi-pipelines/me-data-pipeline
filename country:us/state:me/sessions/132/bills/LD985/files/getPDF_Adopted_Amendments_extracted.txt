1 L.D. 985
2 Date: (Filing No. S- )
4 Reproduced and distributed under the direction of the Secretary of the Senate.
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 416, L.D. 985, “An Act to Impose a
10 Moratorium on the Ownership or Operation of Hospitals in the State by Private Equity
11 Companies or Real Estate Investment Trusts”
12 Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the following:
13 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
14 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
15 Whereas, this legislation establishes a one-year moratorium on the ownership or
16 operation of hospitals in the State by private equity companies or real estate investment
17 trusts; and
18 Whereas, this legislation must take effect as soon as possible so that any transactions
19 involving the State's hospitals and private equity companies or real estate investment trusts
20 may not move forward for one year; and
21 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
22 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
23 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
24 therefore,'
25 Amend the bill in section 1 in §1730-A in subsection 3 in the first line (page 1, line 24
26 in L.D.) by striking out the following: "2029" and inserting the following: '2026'
27 Amend the bill by inserting after section 1 the following:
28 'Sec. 2. Application. This Act does not apply to any transaction for which an
29 application for a certificate of need under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 329
30 has been filed on or before June 1, 2025.'
31 Amend the bill by adding before the summary the following:
32 'Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
33 takes effect when approved.'

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 416, L.D. 985
1 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
2 number to read consecutively.
3 SUMMARY
44 This amendment reduces the moratorium established in the bill from 5 years to one
55 year and clarifies that the moratorium does not apply to any transaction for which an
66 application for a certificate of need under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 329
77 has been filed on or before June 1, 2025. The amendment also adds an emergency preamble
88 and emergency clause.
