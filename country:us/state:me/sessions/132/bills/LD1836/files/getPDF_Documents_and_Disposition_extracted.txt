Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1836
S.P. 718 In Senate, April 30, 2025
Management and Risk Assessment Advisory Commission Regarding
Tier II Offenses
(AFTER DEADLINE)
(EMERGENCY)
Reported by Senator BEEBE-CENTER of Knox for the Sex Offender Management and
Risk Assessment Advisory Commission and approved for introduction by a majority of the
Legislative Council pursuant to Joint Rule 205.
Reference to the Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety suggested and ordered
printed pursuant to Joint Rule 218.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Printed on recycled paper
1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, this Act corrects logical inconsistencies in the Sex Offender Registration
4 and Notification Act of 2013; and
5 Whereas, the timely correction of these inconsistencies is necessary to maintain a
6 consistent system and statutory structure; and
7 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
8 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
9 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
10 therefore,
11 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
12 Sec. 1. 34-A MRSA §11273, sub-§15, ¶A, as amended by PL 2017, c. 377, §4, is
13 further amended by enacting a new subparagraph (3-B) to read:
14 (3-B) Title 17-A, section 255-A, subsection 1, paragraph D, regardless of the age
15 of the victim, if the crime is committed on or after October 1, 2025;
16 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
17 takes effect when approved.
18 SUMMARY
1199 This bill designates unlawful sexual contact based on lack of consent by an unconscious
2200 person to sexual contact that includes penetration as a Tier II offense under the Sex
2211 Offender Registration and Notification Act of 2013.
