9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 416, L.D. 648, “An Act to Expand the
10 Supervised Community Confinement Program”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 34-A MRSA §3036-A, sub-§10-A is enacted to read:
14 10-A. Prisoner committed crime prior to attaining 26 years of age. With the
15 consent of the prisoner, the commissioner may transfer a prisoner committed to the
16 department from a correctional facility to supervised community confinement without
17 meeting the eligibility requirements of subsection 2, paragraphs B, C and D if:
18 A. The prisoner has served at least 2/3 or 20 years of the term of imprisonment
19 imposed, whichever occurs first;
20 B. The crime or crimes for which the prisoner is serving the term of imprisonment
21 were committed before the prisoner attained 26 years of age; and
22 C. The prisoner has continuously maintained a custody classification level of
23 minimum for the 5 years preceding the prisoner's consideration for transfer to the
24 supervised community confinement program, or the prisoner would otherwise be
25 classified as a minimum custody prisoner if not for the sole reason that the prisoner is
26 not within 5 years of the prisoner's release date at the time of the prisoner's
27 consideration for transfer to the supervised community confinement program.
28 Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
29 allocations are made.
30 CORRECTIONS, DEPARTMENT OF
31 Adult Community Corrections 0124
32 Initiative: Provides funding for 16 Probation Officers, 2 Public Service Manager I positions
33 and related costs to accept additional people into the supervised community confinement
34 program.

10 SUMMARY
1111 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill. The
1122 amendment establishes an exception to the standard eligibility criteria of the Department
1133 of Corrections' supervised community confinement program by allowing a prisoner to be
1144 eligible for transfer to the program if the prisoner has served either 2/3 or 20 years of the
1155 prisoner's term of imprisonment, whichever occurs first; the crime or crimes for which the
1166 prisoner is serving the term of imprisonment were committed before the prisoner attained
1177 26 years of age; and the prisoner maintained a minimum custody classification for the 5
1188 years preceding the prisoner's consideration for the supervised community confinement
1199 program, or the prisoner would otherwise be classified as a minimum custody prisoner if
2200 not for the sole reason that the prisoner is not within 5 years of the prisoner's release date.
