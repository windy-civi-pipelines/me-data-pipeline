9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1325, L.D. 1982, “An Act to Ensure
10 Uniformity in the Regulation of PFAS”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Exempt Pesticides from the Law Regulating Perfluoroalkyl and
13 Polyfluoroalkyl Substances in Products'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
15 following:
16 'Sec. 1. 7 MRSA §606, sub-§1, ¶G, as amended by PL 2021, c. 673, §4, is further
17 amended to read:
18 G. Beginning January 1, 2022, a pesticide containing chlorpyrifos as an active
19 ingredient; or
20 Sec. 2. 7 MRSA §606, sub-§1, ¶H, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 673, §4, is amended
21 to read:
22 H. A pesticide that has been contaminated by perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl
23 substances; or.
24 Sec. 3. 7 MRSA §606, sub-§1, ¶I, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 673, §4, is repealed.
25 Sec. 4. 38 MRSA §1614, sub-§4, ¶D-1 is enacted to read:
26 D-1. A pesticide, as defined in Title 7, section 604, subsection 25, regulated under
27 Title 7, chapter 103, subchapter 2-A;'
28 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
29 number to read consecutively.
30 SUMMARY
3311 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
3322 changes the title. It amends the law regulating perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl
