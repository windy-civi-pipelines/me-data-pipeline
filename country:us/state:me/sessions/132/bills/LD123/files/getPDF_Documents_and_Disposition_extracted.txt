132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 123
S.P. 44 In Senate, January 8, 2025
Persons
Submitted by the Department of Public Safety pursuant to Joint Rule 204.
Reference to the Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety suggested and ordered
printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator CYRWAY of Kennebec.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §85, sub-§3, ¶C, as amended by PL 2011, c. 271, §8, is further
3 amended to read:
4 C. The person must have successfully completed a state cognitive test for basic
5 emergency medical treatment and a board-approved practical evaluation assessment of
6 emergency medical treatment skills.
7 Sec. 2. 32 MRSA §85, sub-§4, ¶B, as amended by PL 2011, c. 271, §9, is further
8 amended to read:
9 B. The person must have satisfactorily demonstrated competence in the skills required
10 for the license level. Skill competence may be satisfied by a combination of run report
11 reviews and continuing education training programs conducted in accordance with the
12 rules or by satisfactorily completing the state cognitive test and a board-approved
13 practical evaluation assessment of emergency medical treatment skills.
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill amends the law governing the minimum requirements for licensing and
1166 relicensing of emergency medical services persons by removing the requirement that a
1177 person seeking licensing or relicensing must have successfully completed the practical
1188 evaluation of emergency medical treatment skills approved by the Emergency Medical
1199 Services' Board and replacing it with a requirement that the person must have successfully
2200 completed an assessment of emergency medical treatment skills approved by the
2211 Emergency Medical Services' Board.
