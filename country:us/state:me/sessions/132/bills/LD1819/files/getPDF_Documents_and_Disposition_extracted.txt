Presented by Representative ANKELES of Brunswick.
Cosponsored by President DAUGHTRY of Cumberland.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §13083-H, sub-§10 is enacted to read:
3 10. Remote employee. "Remote employee" means an employee who performs
4 employment duties outside of a traditional office environment, including from the
5 employee's home or other location that is not on the premises of the employer.
6 Sec. 2. 5 MRSA §13083-S-1, sub-§3, ¶E, as enacted by PL 2009, c. 641, §9, is
7 amended to read:
8 E. Payments made to the fund must be allocated as follows:
9 (1) For payments transferred to the fund in 2011 and 2012, 100% must be allocated
10 to the college;
11 (2) For payments made to the fund in 2013, 75% must be allocated to the college
12 and 25% must be allocated to the authority; and
13 (3) For payments made to the fund in 2014 and after, 50% must be allocated to the
14 college and 50% must be allocated to the authority.; and
15 (4) For payments made to the fund in 2026 and after, 25% must be allocated to the
16 college and 75% must be allocated to the authority.
17 Sec. 3. 5 MRSA §13083-S-1, sub-§3, ¶F, as enacted by PL 2009, c. 641, §9, is
18 amended to read:
19 F. Payments to the fund are not allowed for calendar years beginning on or after
20 January 1, 2031 2051. If at least 5,000 8,000 net new jobs are created in the base area
21 prior to 2031 2051, the services funded under paragraphs A and B must be reviewed
22 by the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over economic
23 development matters in order to determine whether continuance of the fund is
24 necessary.
25 Sec. 4. 5 MRSA §13083-S-1, sub-§4, as amended by PL 2019, c. 659, Pt. E, §1,
26 is further amended to read:
27 4. Certification by authority. By February 15th of each year, beginning in 2011, the
28 authority shall provide a report identifying each employer located at the base area to the
29 commissioner. By April 15th of each year, beginning in 2021, each employer located at
30 the base area shall report to the commissioner the number of employees employed at the
31 base area, including remote employees, during the immediately preceding calendar year,
32 the state income taxes withheld for each of those employees and any further information
33 the commissioner may reasonably require.
34 The commissioner shall certify annually to the assessor on or before June 1st of each year
35 the following information:
36 A. Employment, payroll and state withholding data necessary to calculate the base
37 level of employment;
38 B. The total number of employees added during the previous year within the base area
39 above the base level of employment, including additional associated payroll and
40 withholding data necessary to calculate the job tax increment and establish the
41 appropriate payment to the fund;
Page 1 - 132LR0094(01)
1 C. A listing of all employers within the base area that pay withholding taxes, the
2 locations of those employers and the number of employees at each location;
3 D. A listing of all affiliated businesses, data regarding current employment, payroll
4 and Maine income tax withholding for each affiliated business within the base area;
5 and
6 E. Any information required by the assessor to determine the job tax increment
7 pursuant to this section and the employment tax increment revenues pursuant to Title
8 36, chapter 917.
9 Sec. 5. 5 MRSA §13083-S-1, sub-§5, as amended by PL 2023, c. 613, Pt. C, §3,
10 is further amended to read:
11 5. Procedure for payment of revenue to the fund. On or before July 15th of each
12 year, the assessor shall review the information required by subsection 4 and calculate the
13 job tax increment for the preceding calendar year. The assessor shall also calculate the
14 amount of the benefit base in the base area eligible for reimbursement to qualified Maine
15 Employment Tax Increment Financing Program businesses pursuant to Title 36, chapter
16 917. Between July 1st and July 15th of each year, the assessor shall certify to the State
17 Controller the total remaining job tax increment as a result of the limitation in subsection
18 3, paragraph D and the remaining benefit base after reimbursements have been made to
19 qualified Maine Employment Tax Increment Financing Program businesses pursuant to
20 Title 36, chapter 917. On or before July 31st of each year, the State Controller shall transfer
21 50% 100% of the remaining job tax increment and 50% 100% of the remaining benefit
22 base to the state job tax increment contingent account established, maintained and
23 administered by the State Controller from General Fund undedicated revenue within the
24 withholding tax category. On or before July 31st of each year, the State Controller shall
25 deposit this revenue into the fund and distribute the payments pursuant to subsection 3.
26 Sec. 6. 5 MRSA §13083-S-2 is enacted to read:
27 §13083-S-2. Authority roads are public ways
28 1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the
29 following terms have the following meanings.
30 A. "Private way" has the same meaning as in Title 23, section 1903, subsection 10-A.
31 B. "Public way" has the same meaning as in Title 23, section 1903, subsection 11.
32 2. Public ways. A road or street under the jurisdiction of the authority that is not a
33 private way is a public way.
34 SUMMARY
3355 This bill includes remote workers of businesses in the Midcoast Regional
3366 Redevelopment Authority, which owns and administers the former Brunswick Naval Air
3377 Station, in the definition of "employee" for purposes of calculating disbursements by the
3388 State into the Brunswick Naval Air Station Job Increment Financing Fund. The bill amends
3399 disbursements into the fund by removing the state 50% share of the remaining tax
4400 increment share and base benefit share so that 100% goes into the fund. The bill changes
4411 the allocation of reimbursement from the fund from 50% to the Midcoast Regional
4422 Redevelopment Authority and 50% to the Southern Maine Community College in the
Page 2 - 132LR0094(01)
413 Maine Community College System to 75% to the authority and 25% to the college. The
424 bill also clarifies that streets and roads under the jurisdiction of the Midcoast Regional
435 Development Authority that are not private ways are public ways.
