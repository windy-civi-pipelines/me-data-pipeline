7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 487, L.D. 1198, “Resolve, Directing the
10 Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to Assess Its Current Meat and
11 Poultry Inspection Programs”
12 Amend the resolve by inserting before section 1 the following:
13 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
14 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
15 Whereas, Maine's meat and poultry inspection programs exist to protect the health
16 and welfare of consumers by ensuring that livestock and poultry used in the production of
17 meat and poultry products, respectively, are humanely handled and disease-free and the
18 resulting products are wholesome, not adulterated and correctly labeled and packaged; and
19 Whereas, this legislation must take effect before the expiration of the 90-day period
20 to allow the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to begin its review of
21 Maine's meat and poultry inspection programs as soon as possible; and
22 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
23 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
24 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
25 therefore, be it'
26 Amend the resolve in section 1 in the first line (page 1, line 1 in L.D.) by inserting after
27 the following: "That" the following: ', within existing budgeted resources,'
28 Amend the resolve in section 1 in the 11th line (page 1, line 11 in L.D.) by inserting
29 after the following: "training." the following: 'As part of the review under this section, the
30 department shall conduct a survey of meat and poultry producers and processors to obtain
31 feedback on rules, regulations, policies and practices affecting the regulated community.'
32 Amend the resolve in section 1 in the last line (page 1, line 14 in L.D.) by inserting
33 after the following: "requirements." the following: 'The department shall develop strategies
34 to ensure uniform interpretation of the United States Department of Agriculture's
35 regulations and exemptions.'

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 487, L.D. 1198
1 Amend the resolve in section 2 in the 3rd line (page 1, line 17 in L.D.) by inserting
2 after the following: "1" the following: 'describing strategies, findings and
3 recommendations'
4 Amend the resolve in section 2 in the 3rd line (page 1, line 17 in L.D.) by striking out
5 the following: "recommended state or federal" and inserting the following: 'suggested'
6 Amend the resolve by adding before the summary the following:
7 'Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
8 takes effect when approved.'
9 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
10 section number to read consecutively.
11 SUMMARY
1122 This amendment clarifies that the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and
1133 Forestry is required to conduct its review of programs and processes related to meat and
1144 poultry inspections within existing budgeted resources. The amendment also specifies that
1155 the department, as part of the review, is required to conduct a survey of meat and poultry
1166 producers and processors to obtain feedback on rules, regulations, policies and practices
1177 affecting the regulated community and to develop strategies to ensure uniform
1188 interpretation of the United States Department of Agriculture's regulations and exemptions.
1199 The amendment also requires the department's report to include strategies, findings,
2200 recommendations and suggested legislation and adds an emergency preamble and
2211 emergency clause to the bill.
