9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 357, L.D. 830, “An Act to Protect
10 Maine's Scenic Beauty by Requiring Solar Panel Fields to Be Hidden from View”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Protect Maine's Scenic Beauty Through the Regulation of Solar Energy
13 Developments Under the Site Location of Development Laws'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
15 following:
16 'Sec. 1. 38 MRSA §484-E is enacted to read:
17 §484-E. Additional standards for solar energy developments
18 In addition to other standards required by or pursuant to this article, the department
19 shall evaluate a proposed solar energy development in accordance with this section.
20 1. Evaluation criteria. The department shall evaluate the effects of the proposed solar
21 energy development on scenic character and existing uses related to scenic character. The
22 evaluation must include, but is not limited to, consideration of:
23 A. The significance of the scenic character that may potentially be affected by the
24 proposed solar energy development;
25 B. The existing scenic character and existing uses related to scenic character in the
26 area surrounding the proposed solar energy development; and
27 C. The expectations of the typical viewer in viewing the proposed solar energy
28 development.
29 2. Measures required. If, following the evaluation under subsection 1, the department
30 finds that the proposed solar energy development will have an adverse effect on scenic
31 character and existing uses related to scenic character, the department, after consultation
32 with the local land use authority, shall require the developer to adopt measures designed to
33 protect scenic character and existing uses, which may include, but are not limited to, visual
34 screening measures such as the construction of a fence surrounding the solar energy

3 As used in this section, "solar energy development" means a development that uses a
4 solar energy system consisting of ground-mounted solar panels to convert solar energy to
5 electrical energy and includes generating facilities and associated facilities.'
6 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
7 number to read consecutively.
8 SUMMARY
99 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, changes the title of
1100 and replaces the bill. It amends the site location of development laws to provide that, in
1111 reviewing a proposed solar energy development, the Department of Environmental
1122 Protection is required to evaluate the effects of the development on scenic character and
1133 existing uses related to scenic character. The amendment requires that, if the department
1144 finds that the proposed development will have an adverse effect on scenic character and
1155 existing uses related to scenic character, the department, after consultation with the local
1166 land use authority, must require the developer to adopt measures designed to protect scenic
1177 character and existing uses.
