5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1311, L.D. 1967, “Resolve, to Establish
10 the Maine Home Energy Navigator and Coaching Pilot Program”
11 Amend the resolve by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, to Design a Maine Home Energy Navigator and Coaching Pilot Program'
13 Amend the resolve by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
14 'Sec. 1. Maine home energy navigator and coaching pilot program.
15 Resolved: That the Governor's Energy Office and the Office of Policy Innovation and the
16 Future, referred to in this resolve as "the offices," in consultation with the Efficiency Maine
17 Trust, the Maine State Housing Authority and the Maine Office of Community Affairs,
18 shall design a Maine home energy navigator and coaching pilot program, referred to in this
19 resolve as "the pilot program." The pilot program must be designed to:
20 1. Provide community-based energy coaching services to residential consumers;
21 2. Provide guidance to residential consumers, particularly those in low-income and
22 underserved communities, to understand and navigate energy efficiency and clean energy
23 investment options to affordably meet their home energy needs;
24 3. Advise residential consumers on accessing available grants, rebates, financing and
25 other assistance programs and incentives to meet their home energy needs;
26 4. Assist residential consumers in prioritizing identified energy-saving opportunities,
27 including through the integration of weatherization strategies to reduce heating and cooling
28 loads that could minimize the need for the installation of new equipment and lower future
29 electric demands on the grid;
30 5. Help residential consumers review and analyze contractor recommendations
31 regarding cost, payment and other relevant factors;
32 6. Minimize potential liability risks for the pilot program; and
33 7. Use grant funds and private partnerships to support pilot program implementation.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1311, L.D. 1967
1 For the purposes of this resolve, "residential consumers" includes homeowners and
2 renters.
3 The offices shall consult with community-based organizations and tribal governments
4 regarding pilot program design and implementation considerations.
5 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That the offices shall submit a report on the pilot program
6 design to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology by February
7 1, 2026. The report must include a description of the design of the pilot program, a
8 description of the technical assistance and educational materials to be developed as part of
9 the pilot program, an estimate of pilot program costs, a target number of residential
10 consumers to be served by the pilot program and a proposed timeline for implementation
11 of the pilot program. The committee may report out a bill based on the report to the Second
12 Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.'
13 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
14 section number to read consecutively.
15 SUMMARY
1166 This amendment replaces the resolve and changes the title. The amendment directs the
1177 Governor's Energy Office and the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future, in
1188 consultation with the Efficiency Maine Trust, the Maine State Housing Authority and the
1199 Maine Office of Community Affairs, to design a Maine home energy navigator and
2200 coaching pilot program. The pilot program must be designed to provide community-based
2211 energy coaching services to residential consumers, including homeowners and renters, and
2222 to provide guidance to residential consumers regarding energy efficiency and clean energy
2233 investment options and financial considerations. The Governor's Energy Office and the
2244 Office of Policy Innovation and the Future must submit a report on the pilot program design
2255 to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology by February 1, 2026,
2266 including a description of the design of the pilot program, a description of the technical
2277 assistance and educational materials to be developed as part of the pilot program, an
2288 estimate of pilot program costs, a target number of residential consumers to be served by
2299 the pilot program and a proposed timeline for implementation of the pilot program. The
3300 committee may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature
3311 based on the report.
