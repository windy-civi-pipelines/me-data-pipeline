Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 603
An Act to Ensure That the Exemption of Certain Agricultural
Buildings from the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code
Includes Maple Sugarhouses
Received by the Clerk of the House on February 20, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Housing and Economic Development pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed
pursuant to Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative COOPER of Windham.
Cosponsored by Senator BRADSTREET of Kennebec and
Representatives: BAGSHAW of Windham, CIMINO of Bridgton, COLLINS of Sidney,
CRAY of Palmyra, GUERRETTE of Caribou, HALL of Wilton, JACKSON of Oxford,
OLSEN of Raymond.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 10 MRSA §9722, sub-§6, ¶B-1, as amended by PL 2023, c. 527, §1, is
3 further amended by amending subparagraph (3) to read:
4 (3) That building materials from local sawmills, including but not limited to
5 nongraded lumber, are permissible under the Maine Uniform Building and Energy
6 Code; and
7 Sec. 2. 10 MRSA §9722, sub-§6, ¶B-1, as amended by PL 2023, c. 527, §1, is
8 further amended by amending subparagraph (4) to read:
9 (4) That buildings used to house livestock, store harvested crops or cultivate crops
10 are not subject to the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code. The exemption
11 in this subparagraph does not include the cultivation of cannabis regulated under
12 Title 22 or 28‑B.; and
13 Sec. 3. 10 MRSA §9722, sub-§6, ¶B-1, as amended by PL 2023, c. 527, §1, is
14 further amended by enacting a new subparagraph (5) to read:
15 (5) That buildings used primarily to process or store maple sap or syrup are not
16 subject to the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code;
17 SUMMARY
1188 This bill exempts buildings used primarily to process or store maple sap or syrup from
1199 the provisions of the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code.
