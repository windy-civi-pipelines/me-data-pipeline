13 'Sec. 1. 10 MRSA c. 239 is enacted to read:
14 CHAPTER 239
15 ENFORCEMENT OF PARKING VIOLATIONS ON LOTS ACCESSIBLE TO
16 THE PUBLIC
17 §1500-Z. Enforcement of parking violations on property accessible to the public
18 A person or entity that owns, operates or manages property accessible to the public for
19 the parking of motor vehicles, or that provides collection or enforcement services with
20 respect to the parking of motor vehicles, may not impose, assess or seek to enforce or
21 collect any fine, fee or charge to any person for the violation of any parking rule applicable
22 to that property except in compliance with this section.
23 1. Fine, fee or charge amount must be reasonable and conspicuously posted. The
24 amount of any fine, fee or charge to any person for the violation of any parking rule
25 applicable to property accessible to the public for the parking of motor vehicles must be
26 reasonable and must be conspicuously and prominently posted within the property so that
27 it is visible at any entrance and exit of a parking lot or parking structure within the property
28 by a person entering and exiting in a motor vehicle.
29 2. Written notice of violation required. Written notice of a violation of any parking
30 rule applicable to property accessible to the public for the parking of motor vehicles,
31 including the failure to pay an amount of money to park a motor vehicle, must be provided
32 to the registered owner of the motor vehicle. At a minimum, the written notice must include
33 the following information:

1 A. The date and time of the violation;
2 B. The nature of the violation, including the parking rule violated;
3 C. The amount of the fine, fee or charge for the violation and the payment due date for
4 the fine, fee or charge for the violation, which must be at least 10 business days after
5 the date of the written notice of the violation;
6 D. Payment instructions; and
7 E. Contact information for the person or entity responsible for collecting the fine, fee
8 or charge for the violation.
9 3. Use of automated license plate recognition system prohibited. The provisions of
10 Title 29-A, section 2117-A prohibit the use of an automated license plate recognition
11 system at a property accessible to the public for the parking of motor vehicles, subject to
12 the exceptions enumerated in that section.
13 4. Violation; remedies. A violation of this section is a violation of the Maine Unfair
14 Trade Practices Act. In addition to any remedies available under the Maine Unfair Trade
15 Practices Act, a claimant may obtain an award of damages equal to 3 times the amount of
16 a fine, fee or charge assessed, imposed or sought to be enforced in violation of this section.'
17 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
18 number to read consecutively.
19 SUMMARY
2200 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment requires that the amount of any fine,
2211 fee or charge to any person for the violation of any parking rule applicable to property
2222 accessible to the public must be reasonable and be conspicuously and prominently posted
2233 within the property so that it is visible at any entrance and exit of a parking lot or parking
2244 structure within the property by a person entering and exiting in a motor vehicle. The
2255 amendment requires that written notice of a parking violation be provided to the registered
2266 owner of the motor vehicle and specifies the minimum information that must be included
2277 in the notice. The amendment also prohibits the use of an automated license plate
2288 recognition system at a property accessible to the public for the parking of motor vehicles
2299 except as specifically provided under current law. A violation of these provisions is a
3300 violation of the Maine Unfair Trade Practices Act.
