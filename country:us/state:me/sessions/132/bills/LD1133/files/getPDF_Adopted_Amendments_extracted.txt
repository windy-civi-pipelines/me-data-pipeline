9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 457, L.D. 1133, “An Act to Allow
10 Electric Vehicle Charging Stations by Condominium and Residential Associations”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 33 MRSA §576-A is enacted to read:
14 §576-A. Electric vehicle charging stations allowed
15 1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the
16 following terms have the following meanings.
17 A. "Bylaw" means the instrument, however denominated, that contains the procedures
18 for conduct of the affairs of the association of unit owners regardless of the form in
19 which the association of unit owners is organized, including any amendments to the
20 instrument.
21 B. "Common interest community" has the same meaning as in Title 38, section 3002,
22 subsection 3.
23 C. "Electric vehicle charging station" means an electric component assembly or cluster
24 of component assemblies designed specifically to charge batteries within electric
25 vehicles by permitting the transfer of electric energy to a battery or other storage device
26 in an electric vehicle.
27 D. "Executive board" means the body, regardless of name, designated in the declaration
28 to act on behalf of the association of unit owners.
29 E. "Limited common element" has the same meaning as in section 1601-103,
30 subsection (16).
31 F. "Purchaser" means a person, other than a declarant or a dealer, that by means of a
32 voluntary transfer acquires a legal or equitable interest in a unit, other than: a leasehold
33 interest, including renewal options, of less than 20 years; or as security for an
34 obligation.

1 G. "Rule" means a policy, guideline, restriction, procedure or regulation of an
2 association of unit owners, however denominated, that is adopted by an association of
3 unit owners, that is not set forth in the declaration or bylaws and that regulates conduct
4 occurring within the common interest community for the use, maintenance, repair,
5 replacement, modification or appearance of the common interest community of an
6 association of unit owners.
7 H. "Unreasonable restriction" means a restriction that significantly increases the cost
8 of the electric vehicle charging station or significantly decreases its efficiency or
9 specified performance.
10 2. Unenforceable provisions. Beginning January 1, 2026, any provision of a
11 declaration or bylaw that either prohibits or places an unreasonable restriction on the
12 installation or use of an electric vehicle charging station in a unit parking space or limited
13 common element parking space or is otherwise in conflict with the provisions of this section
14 is void and unenforceable.
15 3. Application to install. A unit owner may submit an application to the executive
16 board to install an electric vehicle charging station in a unit parking space or in a limited
17 common element parking space with the written approval of the unit owner of each unit to
18 which use of the limited common element parking space is reserved. The executive board
19 shall acknowledge, in writing, the receipt of an application not later than 30 days after
20 receipt of the application and process the application in the same manner as an application
21 for an addition, alteration or improvement pursuant to the declaration or bylaws. The
22 approval or denial of the application must be in writing and must be issued not later than
23 60 days after the date of receipt of the application. Unless the executive board reasonably
24 requests additional information not later than 60 days from the date of receipt of the
25 application, the application is deemed approved if a denial in writing has not been received
26 within 60 days of the date the application was received.
27 4. Installation requirements. A unit owner may not install an electric vehicle charging
28 station in a unit parking space or limited common element parking space unless the unit
29 owner complies with the requirements of this subsection.
30 A. The unit owner shall obtain approval from the executive board to install the electric
31 vehicle charging station. The executive board shall approve the application for the
32 installation if the unit owner agrees in writing to:
33 (1) Comply with the provisions of the declaration or bylaws regarding an addition,
34 alteration or improvement;
35 (2) Provide a certificate of insurance, within 14 days of approval, that demonstrates
36 insurance coverage in amounts determined sufficient by the executive board;
37 (3) Pay for the costs associated with the installation of the electric vehicle charging
38 station, including, but not limited to, increased master insurance policy premiums,
39 attorney's fees incurred by the association of unit owners, engineering fees,
40 professional fees, permits and applicable zoning compliance costs; and
41 (4) Pay the electricity usage costs associated with the electric vehicle charging
42 station.
43 B. The unit owner, and each successive unit owner, of the electric vehicle charging
44 station is responsible for:

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 457, L.D. 1133
1 (1) The cost of any damage to the electric vehicle charging station, common
2 elements or units resulting from the installation, use, maintenance, repair, removal
3 or replacement of the electric vehicle charging station;
4 (2) The cost of any maintenance, repair and replacement of the electric vehicle
5 charging station until it has been removed;
6 (3) The cost of any restoration of the physical space where the electric vehicle
7 charging station was installed after it is removed;
8 (4) The cost of electricity associated with the electric vehicle charging station;
9 (5) The common expenses as a result of uninsured losses pursuant to any master
10 insurance policy held by the association of unit owners; and
11 (6) Making disclosures to prospective purchasers regarding:
12 (a) The existence of the electric vehicle charging station;
13 (b) The associated responsibilities of the unit owner under this section; and
14 (c) The requirement that the purchaser accept the electric vehicle charging
15 station unless it is removed prior to the transfer of the unit.
16 An electric vehicle charging station installed pursuant to this section must meet all
17 applicable health and safety standards and requirements under any state or federal law or
18 municipal ordinance, except that a unit owner is not required to maintain a liability
19 coverage policy for an existing National Electrical Manufacturers Association or successor
20 organization standard alternating current power plug.
21 5. Association authority. An association of unit owners may:
22 A. Install an electric vehicle charging station in the common elements for the use of all
23 unit owners and develop appropriate rules for such use;
24 B. Create a new parking space where one did not previously exist to facilitate the
25 installation of an electric vehicle charging station;
26 C. Require a unit owner to remove the unit owner's electric vehicle charging station
27 prior to the unit owner's sale of the property unless the purchaser of the property agrees
28 to take ownership of the electric vehicle charging station; and
29 D. Assess the unit owner for any uninsured portion of a loss associated with an electric
30 vehicle charging station, whether resulting from a deductible or otherwise, regardless
31 of whether the association of unit owners submits an insurance claim.
32 6. Attorney's fees. In any action seeking to enforce compliance with this section, the
33 prevailing party must be awarded reasonable attorney's fees.'
34 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
35 number to read consecutively.
36 SUMMARY
3377 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment makes any provision of bylaws or
3388 the declaration of an association of unit owners that places an unreasonable restriction on
3399 the installation or use of an electric vehicle charging station void and unenforceable
4400 beginning January 1, 2026. The amendment also allows a unit owner to apply to the

411 executive board of an association of unit owners to gain approval to install an electric
422 vehicle charging station in a unit parking space or limited common element parking space.
433 The amendment requires the unit owner to comply with any bylaws, provide a certificate
444 of insurance and pay for any costs associated with, and electricity usage related to, the
455 electric vehicle charging station. A prevailing party in an action to seek compliance with
466 these provisions must be awarded reasonable attorney's fees.
