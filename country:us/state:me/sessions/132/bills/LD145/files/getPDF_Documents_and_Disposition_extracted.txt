Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 145
S.P. 81 In Senate, January 14, 2025
Medical Equipment, Breast Pumps and Mobility-enhancing
Equipment
Reference to the Committee on Taxation suggested and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator ROTUNDO of Androscoggin.
Cosponsored by Representative HASENFUS of Readfield, Representative CLOUTIER of
Lewiston and
Senator: LIBBY of Cumberland, Representative: ABDI of Lewiston.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§1-K is enacted to read:
3 1-K. Breast pump. "Breast pump" means an electronically or manually controlled
4 pump device used to express milk from a human breast during lactation, including any
5 external power supply unit packaged and sold with the pump device at the time of sale to
6 power the pump device. "Breast pump" includes breast pump replacement parts, breast
7 pump collection and storage supplies and breast pump kits. For the purposes of this
8 subsection, "breast pump collection and storage supplies" means tangible personal property
9 to be used in conjunction with a breast pump to collect milk expressed from a human breast
10 and to store that collected milk until it is ready for consumption.
11 Sec. 2. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§2-F is enacted to read:
12 2-F. Durable medical equipment. "Durable medical equipment" means equipment,
13 including repair and replacement parts for such equipment, that:
14 A. Can withstand repeated use;
15 B. Is primarily and customarily used to serve a medical purpose;
16 C. Is not generally useful to a person in the absence of illness or injury; and
17 D. Is not worn in or on the body.
18 "Durable medical equipment" does not include mobility-enhancing equipment.
19 Sec. 3. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§6-J is enacted to read:
20 6-J. Mobility-enhancing equipment. "Mobility-enhancing equipment" means
21 equipment, including repair and replacement parts for such equipment, that:
22 A. Is primarily and customarily used to provide or increase the ability to move from
23 one place to another and that is appropriate for use either in a home or a motor vehicle;
24 B. Is not generally used by persons without impaired mobility; and
25 C. Does not include a motor vehicle or equipment on a motor vehicle routinely
26 provided by a motor vehicle manufacturer.
27 "Mobility-enhancing equipment" does not include durable medical equipment.
28 Sec. 4. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§7-F, as enacted by PL 2019, c. 401, Pt. B, §2, is
29 repealed.
30 Sec. 5. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§8-C, as enacted by PL 2011, c. 655, Pt. PP, §1 and
31 affected by §4, is repealed.
32 Sec. 6. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§11, ¶B, as amended by PL 2023, c. 643, Pt. H, §§5
33 to 11 and affected by §29 and amended by c. 673, §§5 to 11 and affected by §28, is further
34 amended by repealing subparagraph (15) and enacting the following in its place:
35 (15) The sale of durable medical equipment or breast pumps for lease for home
36 use to a person engaged in the business of leasing durable medical equipment or
37 breast pumps or the sale of mobility-enhancing equipment for lease for use in a
38 home or motor vehicle to a person engaged in the business of leasing mobility-
39 enhancing equipment;
Page 1 - 132LR0538(01)
1 Sec. 7. 36 MRSA §1760, sub-§94, as amended by PL 2019, c. 401, Pt. B, §15, is
2 repealed.
3 Sec. 8. 36 MRSA §1760, sub-§116 is enacted to read:
4 116. Durable medical equipment; breast pumps; mobility-enhancing equipment.
5 Beginning January 1, 2026, sales of:
6 A. Durable medical equipment that is sold or leased for home use;
7 B. Breast pumps sold or leased for home use; and
8 C. Mobility-enhancing equipment that is sold or leased for use in a home or motor
9 vehicle.
10 Sec. 9. Effective date; application. This Act takes effect January 1, 2026 and
11 applies to sales occurring on or after January 1, 2026.
12 SUMMARY
1133 This bill provides a sales and use tax exemption on the sale of durable medical
1144 equipment and breast pumps for home use and on the sale of mobility-enhancing equipment
1155 for use in a home or motor vehicle. The provisions in the bill apply to sales occurring on
1166 or after January 1, 2026.
