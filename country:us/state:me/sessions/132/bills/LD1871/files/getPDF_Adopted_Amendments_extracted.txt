15 A. A conviction for a current or former Class E crime, except a conviction for a current

18 amended by amending subparagraph (5) to read:
19 (5) Unlawful possession of a scheduled drug under Title 17‑A, former section
20 1107 when that drug was marijuana and the underlying crime was a Class D crime.;
21 and

23 C. For a motion under section 2262-B, a criminal conviction for any current or former
24 crime.'

26 'Sec. 6. 15 MRSA §2262, first ¶, as amended by PL 2023, c. 409, §1, is further
27 amended to read:
28 Except as provided in section sections 2262‑A and 2262-B, criminal history record
29 information relating to a specific criminal conviction may be sealed under this chapter only
30 if:'
31 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 7 and inserting the following:
32 'Sec. 7. 15 MRSA §2262-A, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 409, §2, is repealed.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 741, L.D. 1871
1 Sec. 8. 15 MRSA §2262-A, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 409, §2, is amended
2 to read:
3 2. Time since sentence fully satisfied. At least one year has passed since the person
4 has fully satisfied each of the sentencing alternatives imposed under Title 17‑A, section
5 1502, subsection 2 for the eligible a criminal conviction; and'
6 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
7 number to read consecutively.
8 SUMMARY
99 This amendment removes from the bill changes made to the definition of "eligible
1100 criminal conviction" and modifies the definition that currently exists in statute to provide
1111 that, for the purposes of a motion to seal criminal history record information of victims of
1122 sex trafficking or sexual exploitation, an eligible criminal conviction means a criminal
1133 conviction for any current or former crime. The amendment also eliminates the
1144 requirement that sealing criminal history record information related to engaging in
1155 prostitution be only for an eligible conviction.
