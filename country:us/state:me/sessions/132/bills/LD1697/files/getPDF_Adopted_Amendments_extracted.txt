9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1132, L.D. 1697, “An Act to Increase
10 Penalties to Deter Violations of the Laws Regarding Improper Pesticide Use”
11 Amend the bill in section 1 in paragraph A in the 3rd line (page 1, line 6 in L.D.) by
12 inserting after the following: "chapter 258-A." the following: 'This paragraph does not
13 apply to a private applicator as defined in Title 22, section 1471-C, subsection 22 or a
14 private applicator of general use pesticides as defined in Title 22, section 1471-C,
15 subsection 22-A.'
16 Amend the bill in section 1 in paragraph A in subparagraph (1) in division (a) in the
17 first line (page 1, line 10 in L.D.) by striking out the following: "$25,000" and inserting the
18 following: '$10,000'
19 Amend the bill in section 2 in paragraph B in the first line (page 1, line 31 in L.D.) by
20 inserting after the following: "1471-C," the following: 'subsection 22, and a private
21 applicator of general use pesticides, as defined in Title 22, section 1471-C, subsection 22-
22 A,'
23 Amend the bill in section 2 in paragraph B in the first line (page 1, line 31 in L.D.) by
24 inserting after the following: "violate" the following: 'this subchapter or a rule adopted
25 pursuant to this subchapter or Title 22, chapter 258-A or a rule adopted pursuant to Title
26 22, chapter 258-A or'
27 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
28 number to read consecutively.
29 SUMMARY
3300 This amendment, which is the majority report, decreases the maximum fine that may
3311 be adjudged for a violation of the laws and rules governing pesticides in the bill from
3322 $25,000 to $10,000, except in a case for an unauthorized pesticide application in which the
3333 preponderance of the evidence demonstrates that the violator benefited substantially from
3344 the violation, in which case the maximum fine is $50,000. The amendment clarifies that
3355 this fine does not apply to a private applicator or a private applicator of general use
