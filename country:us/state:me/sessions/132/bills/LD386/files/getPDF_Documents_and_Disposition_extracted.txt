Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 386
S.P. 167 In Senate, February 4, 2025
Enforcement Entities with a School Superintendent or a Principal
Reference to the Committee on Judiciary suggested and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator ROTUNDO of Androscoggin.
Cosponsored by Senators: CARNEY of Cumberland, RAFFERTY of York, Representatives:
CLOUTIER of Lewiston, MCCABE of Lewiston.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 15 MRSA §3301-A, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2003, c. 190, §1, is amended
3 to read:
4 1. Sharing information. Nothing in this This Part precludes does not preclude a law
5 enforcement officer or criminal justice agency from sharing information with a school
6 superintendent or principal, whether or not the information is contained in records,
7 pertaining to a juvenile when the information is credible and indicates a substantial danger
8 with the use of a firearm as defined in Title 17-A, section 2, subsection 12-A or dangerous
9 weapon as defined in Title 17-A, section 2, subsection 9 or an imminent danger to the safety
10 of students or school personnel on school grounds or at a school function. The
11 superintendent or principal may disseminate this information only to the extent necessary
12 to protect students and school personnel and as governed by subsection 2.
13 SUMMARY
1144 This bill allows a law enforcement officer or criminal justice agency to share
1155 information with a school superintendent or principal pertaining to a juvenile when the
1166 information is credible and indicates a substantial danger with the use of a firearm or
1177 dangerous weapon.
