132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1945
H.P. 1304 House of Representatives, May 7, 2025
Reference to the Committee on Health and Human Services suggested and ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative RANA of Bangor.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §265, first ¶, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 129, §1, is amended to
3 read:
4 Beginning October 1, 2026, a health care facility, other than a pharmacy licensed
5 pursuant to Title 32, chapter 117, shall ensure that data related to sexual orientation and
6 gender identity is collected from an individual as part of the health care information
7 collected upon intake at a health care facility and at any other time who is 18 years of age
8 or older when demographic information is collected, unless the individual declines to
9 answer questions regarding the individual's sexual orientation or gender identity. This data
10 is part of the individual's electronic health record. For the purposes of this section, "health
11 care facility" has the same meaning as in section 1711‑C, subsection 1, paragraph D,
12 "health care information" has the same meaning as in section 1711‑C, subsection 1,
13 paragraph E and "individual" has the same meaning as in section 1711‑C, subsection 1,
14 paragraph G, except that in this section it is limited to an individual who is 18 years of age
15 or older. Data collected pursuant to this section is health care information that is subject to
16 the confidentiality requirements of section 1711‑C. The sexual orientation and gender
17 identity data required to be collected pursuant to this section is the same as the sexual and
18 gender identity data elements added by the federal Department of Health and Human
19 Services, Health Resources and Services Administration to the uniform data system
20 administered by the Health Resources and Services Administration in 2016 or included in
21 any subsequent standards.
22 SUMMARY
2233 This bill provides that sexual orientation and gender identity data required to be
2244 collected by a health care facility is limited to an individual who is 18 years of age or older
2255 and that this data is collected when other demographic information is collected from such
2266 an individual. It removes language that describes the data required to be collected.
