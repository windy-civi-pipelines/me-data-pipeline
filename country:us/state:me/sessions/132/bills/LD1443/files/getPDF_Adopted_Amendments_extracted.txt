5 STATE OF MAINE

7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 584, L.D. 1443, “An Act to Ensure the
10 Financial Stability of Behavioral Health Service Providers and Housing Assistance
11 Providers”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the
13 emergency clause and inserting the following:
14 'Sec. 1. 22-A MRSA §214, sub-§1, ¶A-1 is enacted to read:
15 A-1. "In good standing" means that a service provider has successfully complied with
16 the terms and conditions of its most recent contract with the department and has no
17 unresolved or outstanding compliance concerns.
18 Sec. 2. 22-A MRSA §214, sub-§1, ¶C is enacted to read:
19 C. "Previous contract rate" means the payment rate or structure established under the
20 most recent contract between a service provider and the department.
21 Sec. 3. 22-A MRSA §214, sub-§1, ¶D is enacted to read:
22 D. "Service provider" means an entity, including, but not limited to, a private or
23 nonprofit behavioral health agency, housing assistance provider or other nonprofit
24 organization that contracts with the department to provide services.
25 Sec. 4. 22-A MRSA §214, sub-§5 is enacted to read:
26 5. Contract extensions. The department shall include language in its contracts with
27 service providers providing that, in the event of a delay in the finalization of a
28 noncompetitively procured contract renewal, as long as the delays are solely the result of
29 department or state administrative procurement processes, the value of the renewal contract
30 meets the review threshold of the State Procurement Review Committee as set forth in Title
31 5, section 1824-B and the service provider remains in good standing with the department,
32 the department must offer the service provider the option of extending the contract for up
33 to 6 months at the previous contract rate and terms until the renewal of the contract is
34 finalized.
