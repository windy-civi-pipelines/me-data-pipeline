9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 807, L.D. 1232, “An Act to Require
10 Radon Testing for Certain Commercial and Residential Construction”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Require Reporting on Radon Testing and Identification of Radon
13 Prevention Measures for New Commercial and Residential Construction'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
15 following:
16 'Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §778, as amended by PL 2009, c. 278, §2, is further amended to
17 read:
18 §778. Reports
19 A person registered under section 774 or 775 shall, within 45 days of the date the
20 services are provided, notify the department in writing of the street address and zip code of
21 the client and the results of any tests performed. The notice must also identify whether the
22 services were provided for new construction or as part of a real estate transfer and whether
23 the new construction or real estate transfer was for a residential or commercial building.
24 The department may, by rule, specify an alternative notification procedure and notification
25 period and any additional data required in the report.
26 Sec. 2. Department of Health and Human Services, Maine Center for
27 Disease Control and Prevention and Department of Public Safety, Office of the
28 State Fire Marshal, Technical Building Codes and Standards Board to adopt
29 radon prevention standards. The Department of Health and Human Services, Maine
30 Center for Disease Control and Prevention and Department of Public Safety, Office of the
31 State Fire Marshal, Technical Building Codes and Standards Board established in the
32 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 12004-G, subsection 5-A shall work together and
33 consult with a nonprofit organization in this State dedicated to promoting healthy and
34 sustainable indoor air quality to identify appropriate radon prevention measures to be
35 required for all new commercial and residential construction in the State. The Technical

8 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
9 number to read consecutively.
10 SUMMARY
1111 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
1122 changes the title. It requires a person registered to provide radon testing or radon mitigation
1133 services pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, chapter 165 to provide additional
1144 information in the report that the person is required to submit to the Department of Health
1155 and Human Services. The additional information includes whether the testing or mitigation
1166 services were provided for new residential or commercial construction or as part of a real
1177 estate transfer. The amendment also requires the Department of Health and Human
1188 Services, Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention and Department of Public
1199 Safety, Office of the State Fire Marshal, Technical Building Codes and Standards Board to
2200 work with the Maine Indoor Air Quality Council to identify appropriate radon prevention
2211 measures to be required for all new commercial and residential construction in the State
2222 and any necessary technical building code adoptions or amendments. The Technical
2233 Building Codes and Standards Board is required to submit a report to the Joint Standing
2244 Committee on Housing and Economic Development by January 1, 2026 describing the
2255 results of the collaborative work and any codes or standards established or adopted. The
2266 committee may report out legislation based on the report to the Second Regular Session of
2277 the 132nd Legislature.
