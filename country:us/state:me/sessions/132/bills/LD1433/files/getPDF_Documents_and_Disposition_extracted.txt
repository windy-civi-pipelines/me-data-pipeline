132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1433
H.P. 942 House of Representatives, April 3, 2025
Engineer Corps Pilot Program and to Conduct a Feasibility Study of
a Higher Education Service Corps Program
Reference to the Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs suggested and ordered
printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative RIELLY of Westbrook.
Cosponsored by Representatives: BRENNAN of Portland, GERE of Kennebunkport,
MCCABE of Lewiston, MURPHY of Scarborough, STOVER of Boothbay, Senators:
BENNETT of Oxford, RAFFERTY of York.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Sec. 1. Working Waterfront Infrastructure Engineer Corps Pilot Program
2 established. Resolved: That the Working Waterfront Infrastructure Engineer Corps
3 Pilot Program, referred to in this resolve as "the program," is established. The Maine Sea
4 Grant at the University of Maine shall oversee the program and consult with Volunteer
5 Maine and the Maine College of Engineering and Computing at the University of Maine.
6 The purpose of the program is to provide expertise in engineering, planning and permitting
7 to restore and protect the State's working waterfront and other vulnerable infrastructure.
8 Sec. 2. Length of program; number of program participants; program
9 service activity and member development. Resolved: That the program established
10 under section 1 begins in the 2026-2027 academic year and lasts through the 2027-2028
11 academic year. The program must include 5 students each academic year for a total of 10
12 students for the duration of the program. Program participants may be undergraduate or
13 graduate students. Program participants shall conduct vulnerability assessments and other
14 analyses of the working waterfront and other essential working waterfront infrastructure
15 threatened by extreme weather. Program participants shall complete projects that may be
16 semester service-learning or summer term projects. Program participants shall engage with
17 municipalities or other partners in the projects. Program participants must receive
18 education about permitting requirements and funding opportunities to better understand
19 how projects progress from proposal to implementation. Program participants must be
20 exposed to careers in municipalities.
21 Sec. 3. Maine Sea Grant at University of Maine to issue interim report;
22 final report. Resolved: That the Maine Sea Grant at the University of Maine shall
23 provide an interim report on the program by December 1, 2027 detailing the first year of
24 the program to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over
25 education matters and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction
26 over state and local government matters. The Maine Sea Grant at the University of Maine
27 shall provide a final report by November 1, 2028 detailing the final year of the program to
28 the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters
29 and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over state and local
30 government matters. Each report submitted by the Maine Sea Grant at the University of
31 Maine must include recommendations, including suggested legislation. Each joint standing
32 committee may report out legislation related to the contents of each report in the session of
33 the Legislature following submission of each report.
34 Sec. 4. Maine Commission for Community Service to study feasibility of
35 developing higher education service corps program. Resolved: That the Maine
36 Commission for Community Service, established in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5,
37 section 7501, the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System and
38 the Maine Maritime Academy shall collaborate to conduct a feasibility study to examine
39 the expansion of existing service programs and the creation of a higher education service
40 corps program that focuses on:
41 1. Coastal issues;
42 2. Transportation;
43 3. Energy;
44 4. Housing;
Page 1 - 132LR1344(01)
1 5. Land and freshwater preservation;
2 6. Community resilience;
3 7. Education; and
4 8. Public health.
5 The feasibility study must also examine economic development and consider whether
6 the higher education service corps program will operate out of each campus at the
7 University of Maine, described in Title 20-A, section 10901-A, each campus of the Maine
8 Community College System, described in Title 20-A, section 10901-A, and the Maine
9 Maritime Academy. The Maine Commission for Community Service shall submit a report
10 on the feasibility study to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs
11 and the Joint Standing Committee on State and Local Government by December 3, 2025.
12 Each joint standing committee may report out legislation related to the content of the report
13 to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill establishes the Working Waterfront Infrastructure Engineer Corps Pilot
1166 Program. The Maine Sea Grant at the University of Maine is directed to oversee the
1177 program and consult with Volunteer Maine and the Maine College of Engineering and
1188 Computing at the University of Maine. Program participants may be graduate or
1199 undergraduate students. Program participants are required to conduct vulnerability
2200 assessments and other analyses of the State's working waterfronts. Program participants are
2211 required to engage with municipalities and be exposed to careers in municipal government.
2222 The bill requires the Maine Sea Grant to report on the program's status to the Legislature.
2233 The bill also directs the Maine Commission for Community Service to study the feasibility
2244 of developing a higher education service corps program. The feasibility study is due to the
2255 Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs and the Joint Standing
2266 Committee on State and Local Government by December 3, 2025. Each joint standing
2277 committee may report out legislation related to the contents of the report.
