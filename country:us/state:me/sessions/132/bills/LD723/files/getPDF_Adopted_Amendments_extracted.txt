5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 462, L.D. 723, “An Act Requiring the
10 Maine Criminal Justice Academy to Develop a Nonresidential Basic Law Enforcement
11 Training Program”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, to Direct the Maine Criminal Justice Academy to Study the Feasibility of
14 Establishing a Nonresidential Law Enforcement Training Program'
15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
16 'Sec. 1. Maine Criminal Justice Academy study; nonresidential training
17 program. Resolved: That the Board of Trustees of the Maine Criminal Justice Academy
18 shall convene a working group to study the feasibility of establishing a nonresidential law
19 enforcement training program within the Maine Criminal Justice Academy.
20 Sec. 2. Membership. Resolved: That the working group under section 1 consists
21 of the chair of the Board of Trustees of the Maine Criminal Justice Academy, or the chair's
22 designee, and the following members appointed by the Board of Trustees of the Maine
23 Criminal Justice Academy:
24 1. A representative from a statewide association representing municipalities;
25 2. A representative from a statewide association of county commissioners;
26 3. A representative from a statewide association of chiefs of police; and
27 4. A representative from a statewide association of sheriffs.
28 Sec. 3. Report. Resolved: That, by January 1, 2026, the Board of Trustees of the
29 Maine Criminal Justice Academy shall submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on
30 Criminal Justice and Public Safety that includes the findings and recommendations from
31 the working group convened pursuant to section 1, including suggested legislation. The
32 committee may report out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of
33 the 132nd Legislature.'

3 SUMMARY
44 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill with
55 a resolve. The amendment requires the Board of Trustees of the Maine Criminal Justice
66 Academy to conduct a study on the feasibility of establishing a nonresidential law
77 enforcement training program. The board is required to submit a report based on the study
88 by January 1, 2026 to the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety,
99 which may report out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the
1100 132nd Legislature.
