5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 828, L.D. 1253, “An Act to Authorize
10 the Commissioner of Marine Resources to Add Limited-access Area Fishing Days During
11 the Scallop Season”
12 Amend the bill by striking out all of the emergency preamble.
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
14 following:
15 'Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §6171, sub-§3, as corrected by RR 2021, c. 2, Pt. B, §35, is
16 amended to read:
17 3. Emergency rules. The commissioner may adopt or amend rules under the
18 emergency procedures provided in Title 5, chapter 375 in the following circumstances:
19 A. Immediate action is necessary to protect or conserve any marine organism from
20 unusual damage or imminent depletion;
21 B. An unusually large concentration of persons who fish might deplete the supply of
22 any marine organism;
23 C. Immediate action is necessary to comply with changes to federal or interstate
24 fisheries management plans; or
25 D. Immediate action is necessary pursuant to section 6302‑B, subsection 4 to prohibit
26 elver fishing.; or
27 E. Immediate action is necessary to add up to a total of 5 limited-access area days
28 during the scallop season under section 6722 if the commissioner determines that
29 additional days are necessary to counteract the inability to fish for or take scallops on
30 certain days due to inclement weather and that the scallop population is sufficient to
31 allow additional fishing for or taking of scallops in those limited-access areas.'
32 Amend the bill by striking out all of the emergency clause.
33 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
34 number to read consecutively.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 828, L.D. 1253
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment replaces the bill and removes the emergency preamble and emergency
33 clause. The amendment allows the Commissioner of Marine Resources to extend the
44 scallop fishing season by up to a total of 5 limited-access area days during the scallop
55 season through emergency rulemaking if the commissioner determines that additional days
66 are necessary to counteract the inability to fish for scallops on certain days due to inclement
77 weather and that the scallop population is sufficient to allow additional scallop fishing in
88 those areas.
