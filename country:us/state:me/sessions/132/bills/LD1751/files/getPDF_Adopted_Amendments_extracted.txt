14 1. Affordable housing. "Affordable housing" means a decent, safe and sanitary
15 dwelling as follows:

23 Department of Housing and Urban Development under the United States Housing Act
24 of 1937, Public Law 75-412, 50 Stat. 888, Section 8, as amended, can afford without

41 address local challenges, including, but not limited to, seeking input through digital and
42 paper surveys, questionnaires, visual preference surveys and other means;

20 30-A, chapter 187, subchapter 2 shall partner with at least 3 communities that use place

3388 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, makes the following
3399 changes to the bill.
40 1. It amends the definitions of "affordable housing," "cluster development," "place
41 type" and "rural area" in the laws governing planning and land use regulation.

1 2. It requires that rules adopted on the inventory, analysis and needs assessment
2 components of developing a comprehensive plan allow for flexibility in the timing and
3 quantity of data collected and include a map of existing conditions that identifies areas in
4 the municipality where development would be inconsistent with the plan and areas that
5 may be considered suitable for development and includes data related to the environment,
6 the natural resource-based economy, local or regional water supplies and natural hazards.
7 3. It requires the state agency responsible for the administration of the growth
8 management program under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, chapter 187,
9 subchapter 2 to define the term "downtown" in rule and to convene a stakeholder group to
10 solicit input on the rules to be adopted to implement this legislation.
11 4. It requires the state agency responsible for the administration of the growth
12 management program under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, chapter 187,
13 subchapter 2 to develop guidance materials on public participation in the comprehensive
14 planning process, to prepare a guide to help municipalities choose among the various
15 approaches to comprehensive planning, to prepare technical assistance materials on place
16 types and to partner with at least 3 communities to acquire information on how place types
17 and similar planning tools are used. The state agency is also required to submit an interim
18 progress report on the implementation of this legislation to the Joint Standing Committee
19 on Housing and Economic Development, which is authorized to report out legislation based
20 on the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
21 5. It adjusts terminology and makes technical changes.
