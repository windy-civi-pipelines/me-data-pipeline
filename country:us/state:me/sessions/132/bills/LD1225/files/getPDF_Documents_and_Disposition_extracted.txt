Cosponsored by Senator RAFFERTY of York and

CURRY of Waldo.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 10 MRSA §1019, sub-§2, ¶C, as enacted by PL 2005, c. 417, §1, is
3 amended to read:
4 C. Possess an outstanding education loan relating to the an associate or bachelor's
5 degree of any kind or a master's or doctoral nursing degree; and
6 Sec. 2. 10 MRSA §1019, sub-§4, as amended by PL 2023, c. 412, Pt. CC, §2, is
7 further amended to read:
8 4. Administration. The nursing education loan repayment program and the nursing
9 education loan repayment fund are administered by the authority. The authority shall repay
10 the loan of an applicant who meets the criteria in subsection 2 in the amount of up to
11 $20,000 for a an associate, bachelor's or master's degree and up to $40,000 for a doctoral
12 degree for full-time nursing faculty and in the amount of up to $10,000 for a an associate,
13 bachelor's or master's degree and up to $20,000 for a doctoral degree for part-time nursing
14 faculty. The authority may adopt rules to carry out the purposes of this subchapter. Rules
15 adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules pursuant to Title 5, chapter
16 375, subchapter 2‑A.
17 SUMMARY
1188 This bill modifies the nursing education loan repayment program by amending the
1199 criteria to add an outstanding education loan relating to an associate or bachelor's degree
2200 of any kind to the list of debts that qualify for the program, whether or not the otherwise
2211 qualifying nurse has outstanding debts related to a master's or doctoral nursing degree.
2222 Under the bill, the Finance Authority of Maine must repay the loan for an associate or
2233 bachelor's degree up to $20,000 for a qualifying full-time nurse and up to $10,000 for a
2244 qualifying part-time nurse.
