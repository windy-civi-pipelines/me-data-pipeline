9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1108, L.D. 1673, “Resolve, to Establish
10 a Pilot Project to Facilitate the Inclusion of Economic Impact Statements in the Legislative
11 Process”
12 Amend the resolve by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
13 'Sec. 1. Economic impact statement pilot project established. Resolved:
14 That, by the start of the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature, the Legislative
15 Council shall establish and oversee a pilot project, referred to in this resolve as "the pilot
16 project," to develop and implement an economic impact statement to be used to evaluate
17 the economic impact of proposed legislation. The purpose of the pilot project is to assess
18 the methodology, feasibility and effectiveness of using an economic impact statement to
19 provide Legislators with credible, nonpartisan, timely and accessible information regarding
20 the economic impact of legislation in order to enhance legislative decision making and to
21 ascertain how legislation relates to the State's prosperity, productivity and competitiveness.
22 Sec. 2. Request for proposals. Resolved: That, by December 1, 2025, the
23 Legislative Council shall issue a request for proposals to solicit applications from public
24 agencies, private firms, nonprofit organizations and academic institutions to develop an
25 economic impact statement to be used to evaluate the economic impact of legislation as
26 part of the pilot project. The Legislative Council may select one or more entities to
27 participate in the pilot project.
28 Sec. 3. Selection of bills and economic impact analysis. Resolved: That the
29 Legislative Council, in consultation with the selected entity or entities under section 2, shall
30 identify 3 bills during the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature to serve as the
31 subject of economic impact analysis, focusing on legislation that enacts or amends statutes
32 relating to employment, labor or taxation laws; creates a new workforce program; or
33 significantly affects employers, employees or the State's economy. The economic impact
34 statement may address the direct and indirect or secondary economic and demographic
35 effects related to each bill, including the effect on talent attraction, jobs, investment,
36 income, gross domestic product and taxes, and may prioritize and report on the factors most

317 relevant to each bill. The economic impact statement must be available prior to the work
328 session on the legislation.
3 Sec. 4. Oversight and coordination. Resolved: That the Legislative Council shall
4 oversee the pilot project, including the selection of proposals, coordination with
5 participating entities and review of draft and final economic impact statements. The
6 Legislative Council may designate an advisory group or existing staff to assist with
7 oversight and implementation of the pilot project.
8 Sec. 5. Funding. Resolved: That the Legislative Council may receive funds from
9 any public, private, nonprofit or academic source for the purposes of the pilot project.
10 Sec. 6. Evaluation and report. Resolved: That, no later than December 15, 2026,
11 the Legislative Council, in coordination with any entity that was a part of the pilot project,
12 shall submit a written report detailing findings and recommendations, including whether to
13 expand the use of economic impact statements in the legislative process, to the Legislative
14 Council of the 133rd Legislature.'
15 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
16 section number to read consecutively.
17 SUMMARY
1188 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the resolve.
1199 It establishes a pilot project to facilitate the inclusion of economic impact statements in the
2200 legislative process. The amendment also removes the emergency preamble and emergency
2211 clause.
