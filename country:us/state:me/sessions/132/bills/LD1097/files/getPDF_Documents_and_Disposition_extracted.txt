1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 20-A MRSA §6558 is enacted to read:
3 §6558. De-escalation and behavior intervention training
4 1. De-escalation and behavior intervention training. Beginning in the 2026-2027
5 school year and every 3rd school year thereafter, a school administrative unit shall provide
6 at least 4 hours of training in the following topics to each of its administrators, teachers and
7 education technicians:
8 A. Positive behavior interventions;
9 B. Communicative intent of behaviors;
10 C. Alternatives to restrictive procedures, including techniques to identify events and
11 environmental factors that may escalate behavior;
12 D. De-escalation methods;
13 E. Standards for using restraint and seclusion procedures;
14 F. Obtaining emergency medical assistance;
15 G. The physiological and psychological effect of restraint and seclusion;
16 H. Monitoring and responding to a child's physical signs of distress;
17 I. The school administrative unit's policies and procedures for timely reporting and
18 documenting of each incident involving the dangerous behavior of a student and the
19 law governing dangerous behavior prevention and intervention under section 6555; and
20 J. Positive behavior strategies.
21 2. Training list. The department shall maintain a list of trainings under subsection 1
22 and a list of experts who can help school administrative units provide training pursuant to
23 this section.
24 3. New hires. Beginning in the 2027-2028 school year, a school administrative unit
25 shall offer training pursuant to this section to all new school administrators, teachers and
26 education technicians within 60 days of hiring.
27 Sec. 2. De-escalation and behavior intervention training best practices. The
28 Department of Education shall convene teachers and other educators and experts in the
29 subject matter of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 6558 to develop best
30 practices for trainings given pursuant to section 6558 and distribute the best practices to all
31 school administrative units in the State no later than September 1, 2026.
32 SUMMARY
3333 This bill directs a school administrative unit to provide, beginning in the 2026-2027
3344 school year and every 3rd year thereafter, at least 4 hours of training to each of its school
3355 administrators, teachers and education technicians in topics related to de-escalation and
3366 behavior intervention. It directs the Department of Education to maintain a list of trainings
3377 and a list of experts who can help school administrative units provide training. It requires
3388 school administrative units to offer, beginning in the 2027-2028 school year, training to all
3399 new school administrators, teachers and education technicians within 60 days of hiring. It
