Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 514
S.P. 233 In Senate, February 11, 2025
Detoxification Services for Substance Use Disorder Treatment
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on February 7, 2025. Referred to the Committee
on Health and Human Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BALDACCI of Penobscot.
Cosponsored by Representative SUPICA of Bangor and
Senators: FARRIN of Somerset, RAFFERTY of York, Representatives: RANA of Bangor,
STOVER of Boothbay.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Sec. 1. Department of Health and Human Services to increase MaineCare
2 reimbursement for detoxification services. Resolved: That, no later than January 1,
3 2026, the Department of Health and Human Services shall amend its rule Chapter 101:
4 MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 97, Private Non-Medical Institution
5 Services, Appendix B, Principles of Reimbursement for Substance Use Treatment
6 Facilities, to reimburse for non-hospital-based medically supervised withdrawal services at
7 no less than $594.38 per day.
8 SUMMARY
99 This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to amend its rule
1100 Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 97, Private Non-Medical
1111 Institution Services, Appendix B to reimburse for non-hospital-based medically supervised
1122 withdrawal services at no less than $594.38 per day. The department must amend the rule
1133 no later than January 1, 2026.
