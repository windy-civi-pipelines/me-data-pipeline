3 JUDICIARY
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 748, L.D. 1129, “An Act to Clarify
10 Standards for Defendants' Post-judgment Motions for Relief from Protection from Abuse
11 Orders”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
13 following:
14 'Sec. 1. 19-A MRSA §4111, sub-§4 is enacted to read:
15 4. Action by defendant. A defendant may not file a motion to extinguish a final order
16 issued under this chapter. The court may dismiss a motion to extinguish filed by a
17 defendant without a hearing and before opposition is filed.'
18 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
19 number to read consecutively.
20 SUMMARY
2211 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment provides that a defendant seeking
2222 relief from a protection from abuse order may not file a motion to extinguish a final
2233 protection from abuse order and that a court may dismiss such a motion without a hearing
2244 and before opposition is filed.
