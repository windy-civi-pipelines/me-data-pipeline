Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 960
H.P. 619 House of Representatives, March 11, 2025
Nursing Facilities
Received by the Clerk of the House on March 6, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Health and Human Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint
Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative JAVNER of Chester.
Cosponsored by Senator BERNARD of Aroostook and
Representatives: DAIGLE of Fort Kent, MCINTYRE of Lowell.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 18-C MRSA §5-312, sub-§1, ¶A, as amended by PL 2021, c. 463, §1, is
3 further amended by amending the first blocked paragraph to read:
4 For purposes of this paragraph, the delay of discharge of a patient in a hospital until
5 the appointment of a guardian constitutes substantial harm. Identification of a
6 placement in a nursing facility or residential care facility for a patient delayed from
7 discharge from a hospital is not required to file a petition for emergency guardianship
8 under this section;
9 Sec. 2. Nursing facility location requirement. The Department of Health and
10 Human Services shall amend its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter
11 II, Section 67, Nursing Facility Services, to remove the requirement for a placement to be
12 within a 60-mile radius from a MaineCare member's home when the member is being
13 discharged from a hospital to a nursing facility placement.
14 Sec. 3. MaineCare application processing standard. The Department of
15 Health and Human Services shall amend its rules in Chapter 332: MaineCare Eligibility
16 Manual to establish a 30-day processing standard for MaineCare applications for
17 hospitalized patients in need of MaineCare approval for nursing facility placement.
18 SUMMARY
1199 This bill makes the following changes when a hospitalized patient is awaiting discharge
2200 to a long-term care facility:
21 1. Identification of a placement in a nursing facility or residential care facility is not
22 required to file a petition for emergency guardianship for a patient delayed from discharge
23 from a hospital;
24 2. The requirement for a MaineCare member to be placed in a nursing facility within
25 60 miles of the member's home when the member is being discharged from a hospital is
26 removed; and
27 3. The processing standard to process a MaineCare application for a hospitalized
28 patient awaiting a placement to a nursing facility is changed from 45 days to 30 days.
