132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE

in Maine

ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative COLLAMORE of Pittsfield.
Cosponsored by Senator BENNETT of Oxford and
Representatives: CROCKETT of Portland, DILL of Old Town, Speaker FECTEAU of
Biddeford, HAGGAN of Hampden, Senator: HAGGAN of Penobscot.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §3737, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2021, c. 138, §1, is further
3 amended to read:
4 3. Quality differential. To the extent permitted by federal law, the department shall
5 pay a differential rate for child care services that meet or that make substantial progress
6 toward meeting nationally recognized quality standards, such as those standards required
7 by the Head Start program or required for accreditation by the National Association for the
8 Education of Young Children, and shall do so from the Child Care Development Fund 25%
9 Quality Set-aside funds or by other acceptable federal practices. Rules adopted pursuant
10 to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter
11 2‑A. The rules must establish a child care quality rating system with a minimum of 3 steps
12 and must provide for graduated quality differential rates for steps that demonstrate that a
13 child care provider meets or makes substantial progress toward meeting nationally
14 recognized quality standards. The rules must provide that child care providers accredited
15 by the National Association for the Education of Young Children, the National Association
16 for Family Child Care or the Council on Accreditation, or any of their successor
17 organizations, or a Head Start program that meets national Head Start standards, receive
18 the highest rating under the child care quality rating system.
19 Nothing in this subsection requires This subsection does not require the department to pay
20 a quality differential rate for child care services provided through the Temporary
21 Assistance for Needy Families block grant.
22 SUMMARY
2233 Current law requires the Department of Health and Human Services to establish a child
2244 care quality rating system as part of the department's quality differential rate payments.
2255 This bill specifies that child care providers accredited by the National Association for the
2266 Education of Young Children, the National Association for Family Child Care or the
2277 Council on Accreditation or a Head Start program that meets national Head Start standards
2288 must receive the highest rating under the child care quality rating system.
