9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 591, L.D. 1450, “An Act Regarding the
10 Voluntary Municipal Farm Support Program”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 7 MRSA §60, sub-§3, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2007, c. 301, §1, is amended
14 to read:
15 B. Is limited to a term of not less than 20 10 years.
16 Sec. 2. 7 MRSA §60-A, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2007, c. 693, §2, is further
17 amended to read:
18 1. Program. In The Voluntary Municipal Farm Support Program is established in
19 order to protect and support local farms, preserve farmland and reduce the potential tax
20 burdens from new development fiscal burden for participating municipalities that results
21 from new development in rural areas. Under this program, a municipality may enter into
22 farm support arrangements with the owners of qualified farmland.
23 A. A farm support arrangement must be approved by majority vote of the
24 municipality’s legislative body or municipal officers or by a municipal employee
25 appointed by the municipal officers and designated to review and approve farm support
26 arrangements.
27 B. Unless approved by a 2/3 vote of the municipality’s legislative body, the
28 municipality may not enter into farm support arrangements:
29 (1) Affecting more than 3% of the total annual valuation of taxable land in the
30 municipality; and or
31 (2) In any calendar year, affecting more than 1% of the total annual valuation of
32 taxable land in the municipality.
33 Sec. 3. 7 MRSA §60-A, sub-§4, as enacted by PL 2007, c. 301, §1, is amended to
34 read:

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 591, L.D. 1450
1 4. Rules. The department shall adopt rules governing farm support arrangements.
2 Rules adopted under this subsection are major substantive routine technical rules as defined
3 in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.'
4 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
5 number to read consecutively.
6 SUMMARY
77 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment retains the allocation of the
88 Voluntary Municipal Farm Support Program in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 7, which
99 contains laws governing agriculture, rather than reallocating the program to Title 36, which
1100 contains laws governing taxation.
11 The amendment retains certain provisions proposed in the bill. First, the amendment
12 reduces the minimum term of a qualified easement from 20 years to 10 years. Secondly,
13 the amendment allows the municipal officers or a municipal employee appointed by the
14 municipal officers to review and approve farm support arrangements.
15 The amendment provides that rules adopted by the Department of Agriculture,
16 Conservation and Forestry under the Voluntary Municipal Farm Support Program statutes
17 are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A, rather than
18 major substantive rules as provided in current law.
