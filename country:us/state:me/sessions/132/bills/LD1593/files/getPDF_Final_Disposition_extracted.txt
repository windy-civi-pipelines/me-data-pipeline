5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1051, L.D. 1593, “An Act to Require
10 Certain Public Entities to Define Their Use of the Term "Equity"”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §4635 is enacted to read:
14 §4635. Explanation of equity-based decisions
15 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, when a public entity states that
16 it is taking an action, including, without limitation, the adoption of a policy, awarding of a
17 contract or conducting its hiring practices, to advance equity, that public entity shall specify
18 and make available on its publicly accessible website the definition of "equity" that the
19 public entity is using to make that decision and the metrics being used to measure equity.
20 For the purposes of this section, "public entity" does not include a public school as
21 defined in Title 20-A, section 1, subsection 24, a municipality or a county.'
22 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
23 number to read consecutively.
24 SUMMARY
2255 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, changes the bill by
2266 removing educational institutions from the requirement to provide a definition of "equity"
2277 and adding a provision to the bill clarifying that "public entity" does not include a public
2288 school as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 1, subsection 24, a
2299 municipality or a county.
