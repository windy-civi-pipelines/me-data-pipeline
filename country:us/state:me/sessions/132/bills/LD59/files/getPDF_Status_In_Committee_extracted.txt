9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 23, L.D. 59, “An Act to Ban the Drug
10 Tianeptine”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, Directing the Maine Drug Enforcement Agency to Study Whether the Drug
13 Tianeptine Should Be Added to the List of Scheduled Drugs'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
15 'Sec. 1. Maine Drug Enforcement Agency to convene stakeholder group
16 regarding tianeptine. Resolved: That the Department of Public Safety, Maine Drug
17 Enforcement Agency shall convene a stakeholder group to examine whether the drug
18 tianeptine should be added to the list of scheduled drugs under the Maine Revised Statutes,
19 Title 17-A, section 1102 and, if so, at which level the drug should be scheduled. The Maine
20 Drug Enforcement Agency shall ensure that the stakeholder group includes representatives
21 from a broad spectrum of stakeholders, including, but not limited to, a representative from
22 the Department of Health and Human Services, a representative from the judicial branch,
23 a representative of a statewide association representing prosecutors, a representative of a
24 statewide association representing criminal defense attorneys, a representative from a
25 substance use disorder recovery program and a representative from the Office of Policy
26 Innovation and the Future with expertise in opioid response.
27 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That, no later than December 3, 2025, the Maine Drug
28 Enforcement Agency shall submit and report the findings and recommendations of the
29 stakeholder group convened under section 1, including any recommended legislation, to
30 the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety. The committee may
31 report out a bill related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
32 Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following
33 appropriations and allocations are made.

11 SUMMARY
1122 This amendment is a minority report of the committee. The amendment replaces the
1133 bill with a resolve that does the following.
14 1. It directs the Department of Public Safety, Maine Drug Enforcement Agency to
15 form a stakeholder group to study whether the drug tianeptine should be added to the list
16 of scheduled drugs under the Maine Criminal Code and, if so, at which level the drug should
17 be scheduled.
18 2. It requires members of the stakeholder group to include representatives of the
19 Department of Health and Human Services, the judicial branch, a statewide association
20 representing prosecutors, a statewide association representing criminal defense attorneys
21 and others.
22 3. It directs the Maine Drug Enforcement Agency to report the findings and
23 recommendations of the stakeholder group, including any recommended legislation, to the
24 Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety by December 3, 2025. The
25 committee is authorized to report out a bill related to the report to the Second Regular
26 Session of the 132nd Legislature.
2277 4. It adds an appropriations and allocations section.
