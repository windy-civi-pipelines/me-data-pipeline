132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1150
H.P. 755 House of Representatives, March 20, 2025
Children
Reference to the Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety suggested and ordered
printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative MASTRACCIO of Sanford.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 25 MRSA §2157 is enacted to read:
3 §2157. Sheltering or aiding missing child
4 1. Duty to inform law enforcement officer. If a person knowingly shelters or aids
5 in obtaining shelter for a missing child for more than 24 hours without the consent of the
6 missing child's parent, guardian or legal custodian, the person shall inform a law
7 enforcement officer as to the missing child's location and condition if the law enforcement
8 officer contacts that person as part of the investigation of the missing child.
9 2. Violation. A person who violates subsection 1 is subject to a civil penalty not to
10 exceed $250.
11 SUMMARY
1122 This bill requires that if a person knowingly shelters or aids in obtaining shelter for a
1133 missing child for more than 24 hours without the consent of the missing child's parent,
1144 guardian or legal custodian, the person must provide information regarding the missing
1155 child's location and condition to a law enforcement officer who has contacted that person.
1166 A person who violates this provision is subject to a civil penalty not to exceed $250.
