3 MARINE RESOURCES
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 549, L.D. 1319, “An Act to Amend the
10 Law Regarding the Suspension of Licenses Issued by the Department of Marine Resources
11 for Failure to Comply with Reporting Requirements”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
13 following:
14 'Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §6412, sub-§1-B is enacted to read:
15 1-B. Process for suspension for failing to comply with daily reporting by
16 harvesters of marine organisms that have daily reporting requirements. If the
17 commissioner determines that an individual licensed or certified to fish for marine
18 organisms under this Part has failed to comply with a daily reporting requirement
19 established in rule adopted by the department pursuant to section 6173, the commissioner
20 may notify the individual by e-mail at the e-mail address provided on the application for
21 the license or certificate. If the individual has not complied with the reporting requirements
22 within 2 days of the requirement to submit the report, the commissioner may mail a notice
23 to the individual. The notice is deemed received 3 days after the mailing. The notice must:
24 A. Describe the information that the individual is required to provide that the
25 department has not received; and
26 B. State that, unless all the information described in paragraph A is provided to the
27 department or the individual requests a hearing, the license or certificate will be
28 suspended 3 business days after the individual's receipt of the notice.
29 Notwithstanding subsection 4, if the individual has not complied with the reporting
30 requirements or requested a hearing within 3 business days after receipt of the notice, the
31 commissioner shall suspend the license or certificate.'
32 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
33 number to read consecutively.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 549, L.D. 1319
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment makes the following changes to the process for license or certificate
33 suspension for failing to comply with daily reporting requirements by harvesters of marine
44 organisms established by the bill.
5 1. It changes the period of time a license or certificate holder has to comply with the
6 reporting requirements before notice of suspension is mailed from 24 hours to 2 days.
7 2. It removes language that authorized the Commissioner of Marine Resources to serve
8 the notice of suspension in hand.
9 3. It clarifies that the notice of suspension must state that a license or certificate will be
10 suspended 3 business days after the license or certificate holder's receipt of the notice.
11 4. It changes from one calendar day to 3 business days the period of time a license or
12 certificate holder has upon receipt of the notice of suspension to comply with the reporting
13 requirements or to request a hearing before the license or certificate is suspended.
