132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1319
S.P. 549 In Senate, March 27, 2025
Issued by the Department of Marine Resources for Failure to
Comply with Reporting Requirements
Submitted by the Department of Marine Resources pursuant to Joint Rule 204.
Reference to the Committee on Marine Resources suggested and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator RENY of Lincoln.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA ยง6412, sub-ยง1-B is enacted to read:
3 1-B. Process for suspension for failing to comply with daily reporting by
4 harvesters of marine organisms. If the commissioner determines that an individual
5 licensed to fish for marine organisms under this Part has failed to comply with a daily
6 reporting requirement established in rule adopted by the department, the commissioner may
7 notify the individual by e-mail. If the license holder has not complied with the reporting
8 requirements within 24 hours of the requirement to submit the report, the commissioner
9 may serve a notice of suspension in hand to the license holder or mail the notice to the
10 license holder. If the notice is mailed to the license holder, the notice is deemed received
11 3 days after the mailing. The notice must:
12 A. Describe the information that the license holder is required to provide that the
13 department has not received; and
14 B. State that, unless all the information described in paragraph A is provided to the
15 department or the license holder requests a hearing, the license is suspended upon
16 receipt of the notice, or 3 days from the date of the notice, whichever is earlier.
17 Notwithstanding subsection 4, if the license holder has not complied with the reporting
18 requirements or requested a hearing within one calendar day after receipt of the notice, the
19 commissioner shall suspend the license.
20 SUMMARY
2211 This bill creates a new license suspension process for marine organism harvesting
2222 licenses when the license holder fails to comply with daily reporting requirements.
