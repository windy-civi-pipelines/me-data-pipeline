6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1069, L.D. 1615, “An Act to Expand
10 Access to Oral Health Care by Creating a New Path for Obtaining a License to Practice
11 Dentistry”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, to Expand Access to Oral Health Care by Studying Alternative Pathways
14 for Obtaining a License to Practice Dentistry'
15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
16 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
17 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
18 Whereas, this resolve establishes a commission to study alternative pathways for
19 obtaining a license to practice dentistry; and
20 Whereas, it is important to conduct this evaluation to address workforce shortages
21 that impact the availability of oral health care services to Maine residents; and
22 Whereas, this legislation must take effect as soon as possible in order to provide
23 adequate time for the commission to complete its work in a timely manner before
24 submitting its report; and
25 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
26 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
27 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
28 therefore, be it
29 Sec. 1. Commission to Expand Access to Oral Health Care by Studying
30 Alternative Pathways for Obtaining a License to Practice Dentistry
31 established. Resolved: That the Commission to Expand Access to Oral Health Care by
32 Studying Alternative Pathways for Obtaining a License to Practice Dentistry, referred to in
33 this resolve as "the commission," is established.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1069, L.D. 1615
1 Sec. 2. Commission membership. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule
2 353, the commission consists of 11 members appointed as follows:
3 1. Two members of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate, at least one
4 of whom must be a member of the Joint Standing Committee on Health Coverage,
5 Insurance and Financial Services;
6 2. Two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the
7 House of Representatives, at least one of whom must be a member of the Joint Standing
8 Committee on Health and Human Services;
9 3. One member who is a member of the Board of Dental Practice, appointed by the
10 Board of Dental Practice;
11 4. One member who is a representative of the Maine Dental Association, appointed by
12 the President of the Senate;
13 5. One member who is a representative of federally qualified health centers that provide
14 dental services, appointed by the President of the Senate;
15 6. One member who is a dentist licensed to practice in the State, appointed by the
16 President of the Senate;
17 7. One member who is a foreign-trained dentist, appointed by the Speaker of the House
18 of Representatives;
19 8. One member who is a representative of the Maine Dental Hygienists' Association,
20 appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives; and
21 9. One member representing the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation,
22 Office of Professional and Occupational Regulation, appointed by the Governor.
23 Sec. 3. Chairs. Resolved: That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair
24 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the
25 commission.
26 Sec. 4. Appointments; convening of commission. Resolved: That all
27 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this
28 resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative
29 Council once all appointments have been completed. After appointment of all members,
30 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission. If 30 days or more
31 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been
32 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for
33 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
34 Sec. 5. Duties. Resolved: That the commission shall study integrating foreign-
35 trained dentists and out-of-state dentists into the dental care workforce in a way that best
36 reflects their level of skills and training and reducing barriers to licensing for foreign-
37 trained dentists and dentists from other states. The commission shall explore a wide range
38 of options for how to help enable foreign-trained dentists and out-of-state dentists who wish
39 to live and practice in the State to address potential workforce shortages. The commission
40 shall make recommendations on:
41 1. Strategies to integrate foreign-trained dentists and dentists from other states into the
42 State's dental care workforce;

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1069, L.D. 1615
1 2. Changes to state laws and rules that may pose unnecessary barriers to practice for
2 foreign-trained dentists and dentists from other states;
3 3. Necessary supports for foreign-trained dentists and out-of-state dentists moving
4 through the different steps in the licensing process prior to involvement with the Board of
5 Dental Practice;
6 4. Opportunities to advocate for corresponding changes to national licensing
7 requirements; and
8 5. Any other matters pertaining to foreign-trained dentists and dentists from other states
9 considered necessary by the commission.
10 The commission shall review and identify best practices from similar efforts in other
11 states. The commission may hold hearings and invite testimony from experts and the public
12 to gather information. The commission may develop guidelines for full licensure and
13 conditional licensure of foreign-trained dentists and dentists from other states and
14 recommendations for the types of strategies, programs and support that would benefit
15 foreign-trained dentists and dentists from other states to use the fullest extent of their
16 training and experience.
17 Sec. 6. Staff assistance. Resolved: That the Legislative Council shall provide
18 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support
19 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
20 Sec. 7. Stakeholder participation. Resolved: That the commission may invite
21 the participation of stakeholders to participate in meetings or subcommittee meetings of
22 the commission to ensure the commission has the information and expertise necessary to
23 fulfill its duties, including, but not limited to, representatives of the University of New
24 England College of Dental Medicine and the Maine Public Health Association.
25 Sec. 8. Report. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than
26 January 15, 2026, the commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and
27 recommendations, including suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on
28 Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services. The joint standing committee may
29 report out legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature based on the
30 report.
31 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
32 takes effect when approved.'
33 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
34 number to read consecutively.
35 SUMMARY
3366 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve. The amendment establishes the
3377 Commission to Expand Access to Oral Health Care by Studying Alternative Pathways for
3388 Obtaining a License to Practice Dentistry. To address potential workforce shortages, the
3399 commission is directed to study integrating foreign-trained dentists and out-of-state dentists
4400 into the dental care workforce in a way that best reflects their level of skills and training
4411 and reducing barriers to licensing for foreign-trained dentists and dentists from other states.
