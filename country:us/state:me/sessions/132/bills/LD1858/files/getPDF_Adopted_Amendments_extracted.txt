10 Local Seafood in Schools”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 20-A MRSA §6602, sub-§12, as amended by PL 2023, c. 101, §1, is further
14 amended to read:
15 12. Local Foods Fund. The Local Foods Fund is established within the department.
16 The fund is authorized to receive revenue from public and private sources. The fund must
17 be held separate and apart from all other money, funds and accounts. Any balance
18 remaining in the fund at the end of the fiscal year must be carried forward to the next fiscal
19 year. The fund must be used to match $1 for every $3 $2 a school administrative unit pays
20 for produce, value-added dairy, protein, including seafood, or processed foods purchased
21 directly from a farmer, person who fishes commercially, aquaculture farmer, farmers'
22 cooperative, aquaculture farmers' cooperative, local food hub, local food processor or food
23 service distributor in the State, to a maximum state contribution of $5,000 $10,000 per
24 school administrative unit in fiscal year 2021‑22 2025-26 and subsequent years or $5,500
25 $11,000 per school administrative unit if funding is received and the school administrative
26 unit sends a food service employee to local foods training administered by the department
27 under subsection 13. All foods purchased using the fund must be grown, caught or
28 produced in the State, with the exception of processed and value-added food products
29 produced in the State, which must meet standards set by the department. The department
30 shall create standards for allowable processed and value‑added food products produced in
31 the State and provide guidance to school administrative units regarding which of those
32 products are allowable for reimbursement under this subsection and subsection 12‑A. At
33 the end of the fiscal year, the school administrative unit may provide the department with
34 receipts documenting purchases pursuant to this subsection during that year.
35 Reimbursement or partial reimbursement to school administrative units may only be made
36 up to the amount available in the fund. Failure to reimburse does not constitute an
37 obligation on behalf of the State to a school administrative unit. The department shall apply

8 Sec. 2. 20-A MRSA §6602, sub-§12-A, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 426, §2, is
9 amended to read:
10 12-A. Local Foods Fund reimbursement. Reimbursement or partial reimbursement
11 to school administrative units may be made only up to the amount appropriated to support
12 the provisions of the Local Foods Fund as established in subsection 12. Funds appropriated
13 for this purpose do not lapse but must be carried forward to the next fiscal year to be used
14 for the same purpose or for training and materials related to the Local Foods Fund
15 programs.
16 Sec. 3. 20-A MRSA §6602, sub-§13, as enacted by PL 2015, c. 267, Pt. OOO, §2,
17 is amended by enacting at the end a new last blocked paragraph to read:
18 Funds received for the purposes of this subsection may be used to contract with 3rd-party
19 entities to provide training.
20 Sec. 4. 20-A MRSA §6602, sub-§13-A is enacted to read:
21 13-A. Data collection; annual report to Legislature. The department shall collect
22 data on the usage of the Local Foods Fund under subsection 12 and the local foods training
23 program under subsection 13 by school administrative units, including the procurement of
24 seafood. Beginning January 1, 2026 and annually thereafter, the department shall report to
25 the joint standing committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters
26 and marine resources matters on program participation, seafood purchases made by school
27 administrative units and recommendations for program improvement. Each of the joint
28 standing committees may report out legislation related to the content of the report.'
29 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
30 number to read consecutively.
31 SUMMARY
3322 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment amends the Local Foods Fund
3333 within the Department of Education to require the fund to be used to match $1 for every $2
3344 a school administrative unit pays for food, including seafood, from a farmer, person who
3355 fishes commercially, aquaculture farmer, farmers' cooperative, aquaculture farmers'
3366 cooperative, local food hub, local food processor or food service distributor in the State. It
3377 changes the maximum state contribution from $5,000 to $10,000 per school administrative
3388 unit in fiscal year 2025-26 and subsequent years and from $5,500 to $11,000 per school
3399 administrative unit if funding is received and the school administrative unit sends a food
4400 service employee to a local foods training administered by the department. The amendment
4411 also allows for unexpended balances remaining at the end of a fiscal year to be used for
4422 training and materials related to the Local Foods Fund programs. The amendment also
4433 allows for the local foods training program funds to be used to contract with a 3rd-party

414 entity to provide training. The amendment also requires the department to collect data on
425 the usage of the Local Foods Fund and the local foods training program and to report
436 annually to the Legislature beginning January 1, 2026.
