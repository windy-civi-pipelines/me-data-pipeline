Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 326
An Act to Increase the Property Tax Exemption Provided to
Individuals Who Are Legally Blind
Received by the Clerk of the House on January 30, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Taxation pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative BRIDGEO of Augusta.
Cosponsored by Senator BRENNER of Cumberland and
Representatives: ARATA of New Gloucester, BRENNAN of Portland, LAJOIE of Lewiston,
LEE of Auburn, ROLLINS of Augusta.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §654-A, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2019, c. 401, Pt. A, §8, is
3 further amended to read:
4 1. Exemption. The residential real estate up to the just value of $4,000 $10,000,
5 having a taxable situs in the place of residence, of inhabitants of the State who are legally
6 blind as determined by a properly licensed Doctor of Medicine, Doctor of Osteopathy or
7 Doctor of Optometry is exempt from taxation.
8 Sec. 2. Application. This Act applies to property tax years beginning on or after
9 April 1, 2026.
10 SUMMARY
1111 This bill increases, for property tax years beginning on or after April 1, 2026, the
1122 maximum value of residential real estate that is exempt from property taxation for
1133 inhabitants of the State who are legally blind from $4,000 to $10,000.
