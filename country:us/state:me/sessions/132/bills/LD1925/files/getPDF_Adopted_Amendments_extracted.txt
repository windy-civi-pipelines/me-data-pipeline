3 AGRICULTURE, CONSERVATION AND FORESTRY
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1286, L.D. 1925, “An Act to Improve
10 Access to Grant Funding for the Maine Farms for the Future Program”
11 Amend the bill in section 1 in §318 in subsection 3 in the 3rd and 4th lines (page 1,
12 lines 20 and 21 in L.D.) by striking out the following: "These services must include:" and
13 inserting the following: 'These services must include: The services package must include
14 services provided by outside experts, such as the analysis of production practices and
15 markets or the development of financial data. These services may include instruction or
16 classroom training in economics and business planning as available and as required by the
17 department for the owner or operator of the farm business.'
18 Amend the bill in section 1 in §318 in subsection 3 by striking out all of paragraphs A
19 and B (page 1, lines 22 to 25 in L.D.) and inserting the following:
20 'A. Outside experts to provide services such as analyzing production practices and
21 markets or developing financial data; and
22 B. Department-approved instruction or classroom training in economics and
23 business planning for the owner or operator of the farm.'
24 Amend the bill by striking out all of sections 3 and 4.
25 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
26 number to read consecutively.
27 SUMMARY
2288 Current law provides that once an applicant is selected to participate in the Maine
2299 Farms for the Future Program, the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry
3300 is required to assist the selected farm business in assembling a services package to develop
3311 a business plan within 18 months of the selection.
32 This amendment clarifies that services may include instruction or classroom training
33 in economics and business planning as available and as required by the department for the
