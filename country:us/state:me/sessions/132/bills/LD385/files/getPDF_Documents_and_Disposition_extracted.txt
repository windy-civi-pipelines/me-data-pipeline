132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 385
S.P. 154 In Senate, February 4, 2025
Sexual Assault, Sexual Harassment, Sexual Misconduct,
Cyberbullying or Discrimination from Litigation
Reference to the Committee on Judiciary suggested and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BRENNER of Cumberland.
Cosponsored by Representative SATO of Gorham and
Senator: CARNEY of Cumberland, Representatives: DHALAC of South Portland, DOUDERA
of Camden.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 14 MRSA §732, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 626, §2 and affected by §§3, 4
3 and 7, is amended to read:
4 §732. Definitions
5 As used in this subchapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms
6 have the following meanings.
7 1. Governmental unit. "Governmental unit" means a public corporation or
8 government or governmental subdivision, agency or instrumentality.
9 1-A. Cyberbullying. "Cyberbullying" has the same meaning as in Title 20-A, section
10 6554, subsection 2, paragraph C.
11 1-B. Discrimination. "Discrimination" has the same meaning as in Title 5, section
12 4553, subsection 2, and includes:
13 A. Subjecting to harassment;
14 B. Failing to prevent an act of discrimination;
15 C. Aiding, abetting, inciting, compelling or coercing an act of discrimination; or
16 D. Retaliating against a person for reporting an act of discrimination.
17 2. Person. "Person" means an individual, estate, trust, partnership, business or
18 nonprofit entity, governmental unit or other legal entity.
19 3. Sexual assault. "Sexual assault" means conduct described under Title 17-A,
20 chapters 11 and 12.
21 4. Sexual harassment. "Sexual harassment" means verbal or physical conduct of a
22 sexual nature directed at a specific person, including:
23 A. Unwelcome sexual advances;
24 B. Sexually suggestive remarks or actions;
25 C. Unwanted hugs, touches or kisses;
26 D. Requests for sexual favors; or
27 E. Retaliation for communicating about or filing a complaint of sexual harassment.
28 5. Sexual misconduct. "Sexual misconduct" means conduct described in Title 17-A,
29 chapter 35.
30 Sec. 2. 14 MRSA §733, sub-§2, ¶C, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 626, §2 and affected
31 by §§3, 4 and 7, is amended to read:
32 C. Exercise of the right of freedom of speech or of the press, the right to assemble or
33 petition or the right of association, guaranteed by the United States Constitution or by
34 the Constitution of Maine, on a matter of public concern; or
35 Sec. 3. 14 MRSA §733, sub-§2, ¶D, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 626, §2 and affected
36 by §§3, 4 and 7, is amended to read:
37 D. Written or oral statement made in connection with a discrimination complaint
38 pursuant to the Maine Human Rights Act or any written or oral statement made in
Page 1 - 132LR0634(01)
319 connection with a complaint pursuant to Title 20‑A, chapter 445 or the so-called Title
420 IX provisions of the federal Education Amendments of 1972, Public Law 92-318.; or
3 Sec. 4. 14 MRSA §733, sub-§2, ¶E is enacted to read:
4 E. Communication, made without malice, regarding an incident of sexual assault,
5 sexual harassment, sexual misconduct, cyberbullying or discrimination experienced by
6 the person, who has or at any time had a reasonable basis to file a complaint concerning
7 the sexual assault, sexual harassment, sexual misconduct, cyberbullying or
8 discrimination, whether the complaint was filed or not. For purposes of this paragraph,
9 discrimination may include any conduct prohibited by Title 5, chapter 337, subchapters
10 3, 4 and 5-B.
11 SUMMARY
1122 This bill amends the Uniform Public Expression Protection Act by protecting from
1133 causes of action a communication made by a person without malice, regarding an incident
1144 of sexual assault, sexual harassment, sexual misconduct, cyberbullying or discrimination
1155 experienced by the person, who has or at any time had a reasonable basis to file a complaint
1166 concerning the sexual assault, sexual harassment, sexual misconduct, cyberbullying or
1177 discrimination, whether the complaint was filed or not. For purposes of this bill,
1188 "discrimination" includes subjecting to harassment; failing to prevent an act of
1199 discrimination; aiding, abetting, inciting, compelling or coercing an act of discrimination;
2200 or retaliating against a person for reporting an act of discrimination.
