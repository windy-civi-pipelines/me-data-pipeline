3 AGRICULTURE, CONSERVATION AND FORESTRY
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 142, L.D. 356, “An Act to Require
10 Notification of Certain Outdoor Pesticide Applications”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, Directing the Board of Pesticides Control to Prohibit the Use of Rodenticides
13 in Outdoor Residential Settings'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
15 'Sec. 1. Board of Pesticides Control to prohibit use of rodenticides.
16 Resolved: That the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Board of
17 Pesticides Control shall prohibit the use of rodenticides, including rodenticidal baits, in
18 outdoor residential settings. A certified applicator as defined under the Maine Revised
19 Statutes, Title 22, section 1471-C, subsection 4 is exempt from the prohibition under this
20 section. The board shall submit a report with an update on the prohibition under this section
21 to the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry no later than
22 January 15, 2026. The joint standing committee may submit a bill to the Second Regular
23 Session of the 132nd Legislature relating to the subject matter of the report.'
24 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
25 number to read consecutively.
26 SUMMARY
2277 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve and removes the emergency preamble
2288 and emergency clause. The resolve requires the Department of Agriculture, Conservation
2299 and Forestry, Board of Pesticides Control to prohibit the use of rodenticides, including
3300 rodenticidal baits, in outdoor residential settings. A person who is certified by the board
3311 pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 1471-D and authorized to use or
3322 supervise the use of any pesticides is exempt from the prohibition on the use of rodenticides
3333 in outdoor residential settings. The amendment requires the board to submit a report with
3344 an update on the prohibition on the use of rodenticides in outdoor residential settings to the
