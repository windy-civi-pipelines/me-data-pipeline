132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE

Legislative Document No. 1355
H.P. 878 House of Representatives, April 1, 2025
Renewable Energy Infrastructure
Received by the Clerk of the House on March 28, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Taxation pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative HASENFUS of Readfield.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Sec. 1. Office of Tax Policy to examine and evaluate system of taxation of
2 renewable energy infrastructure and possible adoption of uniform capacity tax
3 for renewable energy infrastructure. Resolved: That the Department of
4 Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Revenue Services, Office of Tax Policy
5 shall examine and evaluate the current system of taxation of renewable energy
6 infrastructure and possible adoption of a uniform capacity tax for renewable energy
7 infrastructure. The study must include an examination and evaluation of:
8 1. The current statutory framework for the application of personal and real property
9 taxes on renewable energy infrastructure, including, but not limited to, solar photovoltaic
10 systems, wind energy development and battery energy storage systems;
11 2. The history of municipal and county taxation of renewable energy infrastructure in
12 the State, as well as in other states;
13 3. How tax revenue from new renewable energy infrastructure projects may affect
14 state-municipal revenue-sharing formulas, including the effect of renewable energy
15 infrastructure value depreciation;
16 4. Whether renewable energy infrastructure presents special difficulties with
17 identifying just value;
18 5. What tax amount per megawatt of electric generation capacity of renewable energy
19 infrastructure projects, if any, would fairly compensate municipalities without making such
20 projects unviable;
21 6. The current use, complexity, cost and appropriateness of programs such as tax
22 increment financing and credit enhancement agreements for renewable energy
23 infrastructure and whether an alternative program may benefit municipalities, counties and
24 renewable energy infrastructure development in the State; and
25 7. Whether a uniform taxation of renewable energy infrastructure and exclusion of
26 such revenue from state-municipal revenue-sharing formulas may encourage renewable
27 energy infrastructure development, advance the State's clean energy goals and benefit
28 municipalities and counties in the State.
29 The Office of Tax Policy shall consult with the Governor's Energy Office,
30 representatives from the renewable energy industry and other experts in the State and
31 municipal and county officials with relevant experience, and solicit public comments on
32 the study. No later than December 3, 2025, the Office of Tax Policy shall submit a report,
33 including findings, recommendations and any suggested legislation, based on the study to
34 the Joint Standing Committee on Taxation. The joint standing committee may report out
35 legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
36 SUMMARY
