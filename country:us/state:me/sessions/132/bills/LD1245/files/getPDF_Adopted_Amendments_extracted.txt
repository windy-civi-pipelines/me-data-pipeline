11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Establish a Fund and Council to Support Working Waterfronts'
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
14 following:
15 'Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §3207 is enacted to read:
16 §3207. Working Waterfront Information and Technical Assistance Fund
17 The Working Waterfront Information and Technical Assistance Fund, referred to in
18 this section as "the fund," is established. Balances in the fund may not lapse and must be
19 carried forward and used for the purposes of this section. The fund is administered by the
20 office, and the office may accept and deposit in the fund money from private and public
21 sources. In carrying out the purposes of the fund, the office shall work with the Department
22 of Economic and Community Development, Office of Tourism; the Department of Marine
23 Resources; coastal municipal and regional governments; and organizations and coalitions
24 that have experience advocating for developing and protecting working waterfronts in the
25 State and experience working with persons who use working waterfronts in the State. In
26 collaboration with the entities listed in this section, the office shall use the fund in the
27 production of working waterfront information campaigns and the provision of technical
28 assistance to encourage protection and development of working waterfronts. As used in
29 this section, "working waterfront" means a parcel of land abutting water subject to tidal
30 influence or land located in the intertidal zone that is used primarily or predominantly to
31 provide access to or support the conduct of commercial fishing and marine activities.
32 Sec. 2. 5 MRSA §3208 is enacted to read:
33 §3208. Working Waterfront Advisory Council

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 820, L.D. 1245
1 The Working Waterfront Advisory Council, established by section 12004-I, subsection
2 57-I and referred to in this section as "the council," is established to address issues facing
3 the State's working waterfronts.
4 1. Membership. The director shall appoint up to 15 members to the council who, to
5 the greatest extent possible, must represent federally recognized Indian nations, tribes or
6 bands in the State, municipalities, nonprofit organizations, trade organizations, educational
7 institutions, individuals and industries that represent or use working waterfronts in the
8 State. To the greatest extent possible, the membership of the council must reflect a
9 geographic distribution along the coast.
10 In making appointments, the director shall consider and appoint residents of the State who
11 have knowledge of problems facing working waterfronts in the State, have experience in
12 advocacy relating to working waterfront issues or provide leadership in programs or
13 activities that create and improve opportunities or spread awareness related to working
14 waterfronts and persons that use them.
15 2. Term. A member's term is for 3 years. If a member is unable to complete the term,
16 the director shall appoint a member pursuant to subsection 1 to serve out the unexpired
17 portion of the term.
18 3. Officers; staff. The officers of the council are the chair and the vice-chair. The
19 term of the officers is one year. The council shall elect a member of the council for each
20 officer position at the first regular meeting of each year. The council is staffed by the
21 office.
22 4. Meetings. The council shall meet at least 4 times per year. It may meet at other
23 times at the call of the chair or the director.
24 5. Council report. By February 1, 2026 and annually thereafter, the council shall
25 submit a report to the joint standing committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction over
26 economic and community development matters and marine resources matters on the status
27 of the condition of, the current and future challenges concerning and recommendations
28 related to working waterfronts in the State, including, but not limited to:
29 A. How to best use state agencies, municipal governments, working waterfront
30 industries and citizen groups to protect and improve access to working waterfronts in
31 the State;
32 B. Matters of interest to working waterfront industries and communities in the State,
33 including, but not limited to, geographic information systems mapping and inventory,
34 climate and resilience, preservation and access, affordable workforce housing, climate-
35 driven relocation, water quality and ecosystems, workforce development, health care
36 access, the marine resources economy and economic development, including public-
37 private partnerships, decarbonization and electrification and developing new working
38 waterfronts; and
39 C. Information on relevant existing federal, state, municipal, educational organization
40 and nonprofit organization data, plans, recommendations and studies that relate to
41 working waterfronts in the State and updates on progress made in state plans that
42 include references to working waterfronts.
43 After reviewing the report, either joint standing committee may report out legislation to
44 implement any recommendations contained in the report.
