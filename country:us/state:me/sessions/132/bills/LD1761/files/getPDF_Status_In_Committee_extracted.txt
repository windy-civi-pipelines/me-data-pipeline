8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1179, L.D. 1761, “An Act to Prohibit
10 Indemnification Agreements”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Prohibit the Transfer of Liability Relating to a Party's Own Negligence or
13 Liability in Contracts'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
15 following:
16 'Sec. 1. 10 MRSA c. 201-B is enacted to read:
17 CHAPTER 201-B
18 TRANSFER OF LIABILITY PROVISIONS IN CONTRACTS PROHIBITED
19 §1120-E. Transfer of liability provisions in contracts prohibited
20 1. Transfer of liability provisions void and unenforceable. A provision or
21 agreement in a contract that purports to transfer or has the effect of transferring to another
22 party liability for negligence arising out of a party's own negligence or liability for a party's
23 own liability arising out of an intentional act or omission is void and unenforceable.
24 2. Additional insured not prohibited. This section does not prohibit an agreement
25 from including any party as an additional insured in an insurance policy.
26 3. Exception. This section does not affect the validity of:
27 A. An insurance policy for workers' compensation or any other insurance policy or
28 agreement issued by an insurer authorized to engage in the business of insurance in this
29 State;
30 B. A surety bond issued by an entity authorized to engage in the business of acting as
31 a surety in this State; or

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1179, L.D. 1761
1 C. A release or waiver of liability agreement or other similar agreement related to a
2 party's own assumption of risk or indemnification.
3 Sec. 2. Application. This Act applies to contracts entered into or renewed on or after
4 January 1, 2026.'
5 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
6 number to read consecutively.
7 SUMMARY
88 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. The amendment prohibits the
99 inclusion of transfer of liability provisions in contracts in which a party transfers liability
1100 for negligence arising out of a party's own negligence or liability for a party's own liability
1111 arising out of an intentional act or omission. The amendment does not affect the validity of
1122 workers' compensation or other insurance policies, the validity of surety bonds or the
1133 validity of any release or waiver of liability agreements or similar agreements related to a
1144 party's own assumption of risk or indemnification. The amendment applies to contracts
1155 entered into or renewed on or after January 1, 2026.
