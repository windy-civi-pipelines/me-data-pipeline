Legislative Document No. 1554

Restaurant Employees
Reference to the Committee on Labor suggested and ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative SATO of Gorham.
Cosponsored by Senator DUSON of Cumberland and
Representatives: BECK of South Portland, CLOUTIER of Lewiston, SALISBURY of
Westbrook, SAYRE of Kennebunk, STOVER of Boothbay, SUPICA of Bangor, Senators:
BENNETT of Oxford, President DAUGHTRY of Cumberland.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §2504 is enacted to read:
3 §2504. Required sexual harassment prevention training for restaurant employees
4 1. Required sexual harassment prevention training for restaurant employees.
5 Every employee of a restaurant is required to receive training approved by the department
6 regarding sexual harassment prevention. The restaurant shall complete a compliance
7 checklist under Title 26, section 807, subsection 4. For purposes of this section,
8 "restaurant" has the same meaning as in Title 28-A, section 2, subsection 15, paragraph Q
9 and "sexual harassment" has the same meaning as in Title 14, section 6000, subsection 2-A.
10 2. Violation. If a restaurant is not in compliance with this section upon the expiration
11 of the restaurant's license under section 2492, the restaurant has 45 days after expiration to
12 comply with subsection 1. Upon expiration of that 45 days, if the restaurant is not in
13 compliance with subsection 1, in addition to any other sanction under section 2498, the
14 department shall deny the renewal of the restaurant's license under Title 22, section 2495.
15 SUMMARY
1166 This bill requires every restaurant employee to receive sexual harassment prevention
1177 training. If the restaurant is not in compliance upon license expiration, the restaurant has
1188 45 days to come into compliance or the Department of Health and Human Services is
1199 required to deny the renewal of the restaurant's license.
