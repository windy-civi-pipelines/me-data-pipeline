132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1106
H.P. 725 House of Representatives, March 18, 2025
Nursing Home in Hancock County into Affordable Senior Housing
(EMERGENCY)
Reference to the Committee on Housing and Economic Development suggested and
ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative EATON of Deer Isle.
Cosponsored by Senator GROHOSKI of Hancock and
Representatives: FRIEDMANN of Bar Harbor, MILLIKEN of Blue Hill, RAY of Lincolnville.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, the Island Nursing Home in Hancock County has ceased operations,
4 resulting in the loss of a critical resource for the district; and
5 Whereas, the region faces an urgent and ongoing shortage of affordable housing for
6 senior citizens; and
7 Whereas, the Board of Directors of the Island Nursing Home has proposed a
8 redevelopment plan to convert the facility into 24 affordable apartments for senior citizens,
9 which will address this critical need; and
10 Whereas, immediate funding is necessary to initiate the renovation project, which has
11 an estimated cost of $1.2 million to $1.5 million; and
12 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
15 therefore,
16 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
17 Sec. 1. Accountability and reporting. The Maine State Housing Authority shall
18 submit a detailed report regarding the conversion of the Island Nursing Home in Hancock
19 County to affordable apartments for senior citizens to the Joint Standing Committee on
20 Appropriations and Financial Affairs within 6 months of receipt of funding provided
21 pursuant to section 2. The report must include:
22 1. A breakdown of how the funds have been used;
23 2. A progress update; and
24 3. A financial summary of additional funds raised and any additional funding needs.
25 Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
26 allocations are made.
27 HOUSING AUTHORITY, MAINE STATE
28 Housing Authority - State 0442
29 Initiative: Provides one-time funds to support the conversion of the Island Nursing Home
30 in Hancock County into 24 affordable apartments for senior citizens.
