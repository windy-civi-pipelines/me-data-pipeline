1 1. Condition of schools receiving state reimbursement for school lunch and school
2 breakfast. A public school or a private school approved for tuition purposes, as defined in
3 section 1, subsection 23, that receives state funding under section 6602, subsection 1,
4 paragraph B, D, H or I, shall maximize the amount of federal reimbursement funds that the
5 school receives in order to receive state funding equal to the difference between the federal
6 reimbursement for a free regular breakfast and the federal reimbursement for a reduced-
7 price regular breakfast and equal to the difference between the federal reimbursement for
8 a free lunch and the federal reimbursement for a reduced-price lunch, and funding equal to
9 the difference between the federal reimbursement for a free breakfast or lunch and the
10 federal reimbursement for a full-price breakfast or lunch for each publicly funded student
11 receiving breakfast or lunch. The public school or private school approved for tuition
12 purposes, if eligible for any provisional school food service program, including, but not
13 limited to, the community eligibility provision under the federal Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids
14 Act of 2010, Public Law 111-296, Section 104(a), shall maximize participation in that
15 provisional school food service program. The department shall determine whether the
16 school is maximizing federal reimbursement funds.
17 2. Department to communicate options to public schools and private schools to
18 maximize federal funding. The department shall annually communicate school food
19 service program options available to a public school or a private school approved for tuition
20 purposes, as defined in section 1, subsection 23, to the school for the school to maximize
21 federal funding in the school food service program. The department may provide technical
22 assistance to a public school or a private school approved for tuition purposes and may
23 contract with a 3rd-party entity.
24 3. School not eligible for state funding if school does not participate in National
25 School Lunch Program or provisional school food service program if eligible. A public
26 school or a private school approved for tuition purposes, as defined in section 1, subsection
27 23, that does not participate in the National School Lunch Program in accordance with 7
28 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 210 (2007) and does not participate in any provisional
29 school food service program, if eligible, or that chooses a provisional school food service
30 program option other than a school food service program option communicated to the
31 public school or private school approved for tuition purposes under subsection 2 by the
32 department may not receive state funding equal to the difference between the federal
33 reimbursement for a free breakfast and the federal reimbursement for a reduced-price
34 breakfast and equal to the difference between the federal reimbursement for a free lunch
35 and the federal reimbursement for a reduced-price lunch, and funding equal to the
36 difference between the federal reimbursement for a free breakfast or lunch and the federal
37 reimbursement for a full-price breakfast or lunch for each publicly funded student receiving
38 breakfast or lunch.

10 SUMMARY
1111 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. The amendment provides that
1122 a public school or private school approved for tuition purposes that enrolls at least 60%
1133 publicly funded students and that participates in the National School Lunch Program in
1144 accordance with 7 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 210 (2007) that serves breakfast and
1155 lunch must maximize the amount of federal reimbursement the school receives in order to
1166 receive state funding. The amendment requires the Department of Education to annually
1177 communicate school food service program options to participating schools to maximize
1188 federal funding in the school food service program. A school that does not enroll in a school
1199 food service program option provided by the department may not receive state funding.
2200 The amendment also provides state funding for eligible schools.
