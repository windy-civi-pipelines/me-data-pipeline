3 EDUCATION AND CULTURAL AFFAIRS
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 48, L.D. 31, “An Act Regarding School
10 Enrollment for Military-connected Students”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 20-A MRSA §5205, sub-§12, ¶A-1 is enacted to read:
14 A-1. If the parent of a military-connected student is transferred or pending transfer
15 within the State, a school administrative unit shall:
16 (1) Allow the student to continue attending the student's current school;
17 (2) Allow the student to enroll in the student's prospective school prior to
18 establishing residence; or
19 (3) Allow the student to enroll at a school located where the student will reside
20 with a noncustodial guardian.
21 Student transfers under this paragraph that take place during a student's final year of
22 high school must conform with section 20107.'
23 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
24 number to read consecutively.
25 SUMMARY
2266 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment provides that if the parent of a
2277 military-connected student is transferred or pending transfer within the State, a school
2288 administrative unit must allow the student to continue attending the student's current
2299 school, allow the student to enroll in the student's prospective school prior to establishing
3300 residence or allow the student to enroll at a school located where the student will reside
3311 with a noncustodial guardian. The amendment also provides that if a transfer occurs during
3322 the student's final year of high school, the transfer must conform with the requirements
3333 described in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 20107.
