Evidence Collection Kits
Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
Sec. 1. 25 MRSA ยง2915-A is enacted to read:

Section 2:
ยง2915-A. Sale of self-administered sexual assault forensic evidence collection kit
prohibited

Section 3:
1. Definitions. As used in this section, the following terms have the following
meanings.
A. "Person" has the same meaning as in Title 5, section 206, subsection 2.
B. "Self-administered sexual assault forensic evidence collection kit" means materials
advertised or marketed as a means for a person other than a licensed hospital or licensed
health care practitioner to collect physical evidence of a sexual assault.
C. "Sexual assault" has the same meaning as in section 2915, subsection 3-A.

Section 4:
2. Prohibition. A person may not sell or offer for sale a self-administered sexual
assault forensic evidence collection kit.

Section 5:
3. Admissibility. This section does not affect the admissibility in a court proceeding
of evidence collected using a self-administered sexual assault forensic evidence collection
kit.

Section 6:
4. Violation. There is a rebuttable presumption that a violation of this section is a
violation of the Maine Unfair Trade Practices Act.
