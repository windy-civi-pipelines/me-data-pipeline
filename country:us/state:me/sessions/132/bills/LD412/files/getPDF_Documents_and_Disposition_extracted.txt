Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 412
An Act to Prohibit the Sale or Provision of Self-administered Sexual
Assault Forensic Evidence Collection Kits
Reference to the Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety suggested and ordered
printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative SATO of Gorham.
Cosponsored by President DAUGHTRY of Cumberland and
Representatives: BABIN of Fort Fairfield, GRAMLICH of Old Orchard Beach, LAJOIE of
Lewiston, MILLIKEN of Blue Hill, STOVER of Boothbay, Senators: CYRWAY of Kennebec,
DUSON of Cumberland, LAWRENCE of York.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 25 MRSA §2915-A is enacted to read:
3 §2915-A. Sale or provision of self-administered sexual assault forensic evidence
4 collection kit prohibited
5 1. Definitions. As used in this section, the following terms have the following
6 meanings.
7 A. "Covered evidence kit" means a self-administered sexual assault forensic evidence
8 collection kit to collect evidence relating to an alleged sexual assault.
9 B. "Person" has the same meaning as in Title 5, section 206, subsection 2.
10 C. "Sexual assault" has the same meaning as in section 2915, subsection 3-A.
11 2. Prohibition. A person may not sell, offer for sale or otherwise provide a covered
12 evidence kit to an individual.
13 3. Admissibility. This section does not apply to the admissibility of a covered
14 evidence kit as evidence in a court proceeding.
15 4. Violation. A violation of this section is a violation of the Maine Unfair Trade
16 Practices Act.
17 SUMMARY
1188 This bill prohibits and makes a violation of the Maine Unfair Trade Practices Act the
1199 sale, offering for sale or otherwise providing to an individual a self-administered sexual
2200 assault forensic evidence collection kit. This bill does not apply to the admissibility of a
2211 self-administered sexual assault forensic evidence collection kit as evidence in a court
2222 proceeding.
