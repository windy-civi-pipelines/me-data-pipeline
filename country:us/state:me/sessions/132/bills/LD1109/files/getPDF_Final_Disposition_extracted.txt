9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 728, L.D. 1109, “An Act to Reduce Gun
10 Violence Casualties in Maine by Prohibiting the Possession of Large-capacity Ammunition
11 Feeding Devices”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
13 following:
14 'Sec. 1. 17-A MRSA §1060 is enacted to read:
15 §1060. Large-capacity ammunition feeding devices
16 1. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms
17 have the following meanings.
18 A. "Federally licensed firearm dealer" means a person or firm that is licensed or is
19 required to be licensed as a dealer under 18 United States Code, Section 923(a).
20 B. "Large-capacity ammunition feeding device" means a magazine, belt, drum, box,
21 tube, feed strip or similar device that has a capacity of, or that can be readily restored
22 or converted to accept, more than 10 rounds of ammunition for a rifle or 15 rounds of
23 ammunition for a handgun.
24 2. A person is guilty of possession of a large-capacity ammunition feeding device if
25 that person intentionally or knowingly manufactures, imports, purchases, sells, offers to
26 transfer or transfers ownership of a large-capacity ammunition feeding device.
27 3. A person who violates subsection 2 commits a Class D crime.
28 4. This section does not apply to:
29 A. An active duty member of the Armed Forces of the United States or the National
30 Guard who is authorized to possess and carry a large-capacity ammunition feeding
31 device;
32 B. An employed federal or state law enforcement officer who, whether on or off duty,
33 is authorized to possess and carry a large-capacity ammunition feeding device;

1 C. A large-capacity ammunition feeding device that is manufactured or sold solely for
2 use with a firearm that is determined to be a curio or relic, or antique, as those terms
3 are defined in 27 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 478.11;
4 D. A federally licensed firearm dealer temporarily transporting a large-capacity
5 ammunition feeding device through the State to be sold or transferred to a person
6 outside of the State; or
7 E. A large-capacity ammunition feeding device lawfully possessed by a federally
8 licensed firearm dealer prior to the effective date of this section.'
9 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
10 number to read consecutively.
11 SUMMARY
1122 This amendment provides that the culpable state of mind required for someone to be
1133 guilty of possession of a large-capacity ammunition feeding device is intentionally or
1144 knowingly. The amendment also clarifies that a device is a large-capacity ammunition
1155 feeding device if it accepts more than 10 rounds of ammunition for a rifle and 15 rounds of
1166 ammunition for a handgun. Finally, this amendment clarifies that the prohibitions
1177 regarding large-capacity ammunition feeding devices do not apply to a device lawfully
1188 possessed by a federally licensed firearm dealer prior to the effective date of the legislation.
