9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 789, L.D. 1184, “An Act to Require
10 Municipal Reporting on Residential Building Permits, Dwelling Units Permitted and
11 Demolished and Certificates of Occupancy Issued”
12 Amend the bill by inserting before section 1 the following:
13 'Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §13056-J, sub-§2, ¶D is enacted to read:
14 D. The department shall assist a municipality with 4,000 or more residents in meeting
15 the requirements of subsection 5. The assistance must include reimbursement of 90%
16 of actual expenses incurred by a municipality if collection or reporting of data as
17 required by subsection 5 is a modification or expansion of municipal activities so as to
18 necessitate additional expenditures from local revenues.'
19 Amend the bill in section 1 in subsection 5 in the first line (page 1, line 3 in L.D.) by
20 striking out the following: "A" and inserting the following: 'By January 31st of each year,
21 a'
22 Amend the bill in section 1 in subsection 5 in the first line (page 1, line 3 in L.D.) by
23 inserting after the following: "municipality" the following: 'with 4,000 or more residents'
24 Amend the bill by inserting after section 1 the following:
25 'Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
26 allocations are made.
27 ECONOMIC AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT, DEPARTMENT OF
28 Reimbursement - Municipal Housing Data Reporting N533
29 Initiative: Provides ongoing funds to reimburse municipalities with 4,000 or more residents
30 for 90% of the cost of preparing an annual report on certain housing data, including data
31 on residential building permits, dwelling units permitted and demolished, certificates of
32 occupancy or other approvals of housing units issued, as well as certain affordability data.

24 SUMMARY
2255 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, sets a due date of
2266 January 31st of each year for the report required in the bill. It limits the report requirement
2277 to municipalities with 4,000 residents or more. It requires the Department of Economic
2288 and Community Development to reimburse 90% of the actual costs to collect and report
2299 the data as required in the Constitution of Maine, Article IX, Section 21. The amendment
3300 adds an appropriations and allocations section.
