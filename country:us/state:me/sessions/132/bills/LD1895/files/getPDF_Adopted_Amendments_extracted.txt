1 L.D. 1895
2 Date: (Filing No. H- )
3 TAXATION
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1266, L.D. 1895, “An Act to Require
10 the Removal from a Property Tax Lien the Name of a Previous Owner Who Paid Prorated
11 Property Taxes”
12 Amend the bill in section 1 in the indented paragraph in the 2nd line (page 1, line 5 in
13 L.D.) by inserting after the following: "party's" the following: 'own'
14 Amend the bill in section 1 in the indented paragraph in the 3rd line (page 1, line 6 in
15 L.D.) by striking out the following: "assignee" and inserting the following: 'the treasurer's
16 designee'
17 Amend the bill in section 1 in the indented paragraph in the last line (page 1, line 9 in
18 L.D.) by inserting after the following: "seller." the following: 'The discharge of the tax lien
19 must include a statement that the assignee, following the release of the property, did not
20 owe property taxes as to the released property. The assignee of the discharge is responsible
21 for the cost of recording the discharge. The assignee of the discharge must be limited to
22 the seller of the property that is the subject of the tax lien mortgage.'
23 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
24 number to read consecutively.
25 SUMMARY
2266 This amendment requires that the discharge of the tax lien mortgage contain a statement
2277 that the seller of the property did not owe any taxes on the property following its
2288 conveyance, limits the discharge of the tax lien to the seller of the property and requires the
2299 seller to pay the cost of recording the discharge.
