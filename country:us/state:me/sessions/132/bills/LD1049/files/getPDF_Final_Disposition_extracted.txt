9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 678, L.D. 1049, “An Act to Eliminate
10 the Duty to Inform a Law Enforcement Officer When Carrying a Concealed Handgun
11 Without a Permit”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'An Act to Require a Person to Inform a Law Enforcement Officer upon Request
14 When Carrying a Concealed Handgun Without a Permit'
15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
16 following:
17 'Sec. 1. 25 MRSA §2003-A, as enacted by PL 2015, c. 327, §4, is amended to read:
18 §2003-A. Duty to inform law enforcement upon request
19 When an individual who is carrying a concealed handgun pursuant to the authority of
20 this chapter and who does not have a valid permit to carry a concealed handgun that has
21 been issued as provided in this chapter first comes into contact with any law enforcement
22 officer of this State or its political subdivisions or a federal law enforcement officer during
23 the course of any arrest, detainment or routine traffic stop, that individual shall, upon
24 request of the law enforcement officer, immediately inform that law enforcement officer
25 of the fact that the individual is carrying a concealed handgun.
26 Sec. 2. 25 MRSA §2004, sub-§5, as enacted by PL 2015, c. 327, §5, is amended to
27 read:
28 5. Failure to inform law enforcement upon request. A person who fails to comply
29 with section 2003‑A commits a civil violation for which a fine of not more than $100 $250
30 may be adjudged.'
31 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
32 number to read consecutively.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 678, L.D. 1049
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
33 changes the title. The amendment requires a person who is lawfully carrying a concealed
44 handgun to immediately inform a law enforcement officer of that fact upon request of that
55 law enforcement officer. The amendment also increases the fine from $100 to $250 for a
66 failure to inform a law enforcement officer upon request of the fact that a person is lawfully
77 carrying a concealed handgun.
