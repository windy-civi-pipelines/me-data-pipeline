7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 261, L.D. 580, “An Act to Protect Maine
10 Consumers by Prohibiting Fees Charged for Receiving Paper Statements from Financial
11 Institutions and Credit Card Issuers”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
13 following:
14 'Sec. 1. 10 MRSA §9420, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2011, c. 226, §1, is repealed.'
15 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
16 number to read consecutively.
17 SUMMARY
1188 This amendment replaces the bill and is the majority report of the committee. Under
1199 current law, a customer may not be penalized by a business for opting out of receiving from
2200 the business a billing statement by electronic record rather than in paper form, except that
2211 the provision does not apply to a depository institution. The amendment removes the
2222 exemption that applies to a depository institution.
