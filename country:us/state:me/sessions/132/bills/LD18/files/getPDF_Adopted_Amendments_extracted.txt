9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 30, L.D. 18, “An Act to Allow the
10 Department of Corrections to Increase Health Care Fees and Use the Proceeds from Those
11 Fees to Offset the Costs of Client Medical Care Support Workers”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'An Act to Offset the Costs of Client Medical Care Support Workers at Department
14 of Corrections Facilities and Study Correctional Service Fees and Collections'
15 Amend the bill in section 1 in subsection 2 in the 5th line (page 1, line 8 in L.D.) by
16 striking out the following: "$5 $25" and inserting the following: '$5'
17 Amend the bill in section 1 in subsection 2 in the 6th line (page 1, line 9 in L.D.) by
18 striking out the following: "$5 $25" and inserting the following: '$5'
19 Amend the bill in section 1 in subsection 2 in the 13th, 14th and 15th lines (page 1,
20 lines 16 to 18 in L.D.) by striking out the following: ". After first being used to defray this
21 cost, money is retained by the facility to offset the cost of medical and dental services,
22 prescriptions, medication and prosthetic devices" and inserting the following: 'medical and
23 dental services, prescriptions, medication and prosthetic devices'
24 Amend the bill by inserting after section 1 the following:
25 'Sec. 2. Department of Corrections to convene working group. The
26 Department of Corrections shall convene a working group to study all collections and fees
27 assessed to correctional facility clients and review the effect those fees have on a client's
28 ability to pay for facility services. The working group must consist of the following
29 members:
30 1. The Commissioner of Corrections or the commissioner's designee;
31 2. A client from the Maine State Prison;
32 3. A client from the Maine Correctional Center;
33 4. A client from either the Downeast Correctional Facility or the Mountain View
34 Correctional Facility;

4 7. A representative from the Office of the Attorney General that works with the crime

6 8. A representative from a prisoner's advocacy organization.

8 the working group for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice
9 and Public Safety. The committee may report out legislation based on the report to the
10 Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.

26 SUMMARY
2277 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, removes the increased
2288 fees for medical and dental services and requires that those fees go only toward payment
2299 of medical care support workers. The amendment requires the Department of Corrections
3300 to convene a working group to study and review all financial assessments made on
3311 correctional facility clients and to submit a report by January 1, 2026 to the Joint Standing
3322 Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety, which may report out legislation to the
3333 Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature. The amendment also adds an
3344 appropriations and allocations section.
