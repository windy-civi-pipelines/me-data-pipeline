26 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
27 therefore,'

1 F. In conducting a solicitation and selecting eligible Class IA resources or combined
2 projects for contracts under this section, the commission shall:
3 (1) Consider the expected effect of eligible Class IA resources on other renewable
4 resources, as defined in section 3210, subsection 2, paragraph C, due to congestion

6 (2) Select only those eligible Class IA resources or combined projects for contracts
7 that will benefit ratepayers, as determined by the commission in accordance with
8 subsection 2-A; and

10 ratepayers, give preference to eligible Class IA resources or combined projects as
11 follows:
12 (a) Primary preference to those eligible Class IA resources or combined
13 projects that are located on contaminated land; and
14 (b) Secondary preference to those eligible Class IA resources or combined
15 projects that minimize use of farmland that is not contaminated land and
16 minimize use of forested land.
17 G. In conducting a solicitation and selecting combined projects for contracts under this
18 section, the commission shall:
19 (1) Require 2 separate bid proposals, one with the energy storage system and one
20 without; and
21 (2) Require an energy storage system selected for a contract to remain stationary
22 and under the same ownership throughout the contract term.
23 H. The commission may establish a process by rule to allow an energy storage system
24 to apply to the commission to be paired with and added to a contract awarded to an
25 eligible Class IA resource after that resource has been awarded a contract.

1 The commission shall direct investor-owned transmission and distribution utilities to
2 enter into one or more contracts for energy or renewable energy credits from eligible Class
3 IA resources in accordance with this section.
4 1. Definition. As used in this section, "eligible Class IA resource" means a Class IA
5 resource as defined in section 3210, subsection 2, paragraph A-3 that began commercial
6 operation on or before June 30, 2019.
7 2. Competitive procurement. The commission shall conduct one competitive
8 solicitation in order to select eligible Class IA resources for contracts under this section.
9 The commission shall initiate the competitive solicitation by December 31, 2025.
10 A. Through a competitive solicitation under this section, the commission shall procure
11 an amount of energy or renewable energy credits or a combination of energy and
12 renewable energy credits from eligible Class IA resources that is equal to 5% of the
13 retail electricity sales in this State for the period from January 1, 2023 to December 31,
14 2023, as determined by the commission.
15 B. In conducting the solicitation and selecting eligible Class IA resources for contracts
16 under this section, the commission shall consider the benefits to ratepayers and to the
17 economy as set forth in section 3210-G, subsection 1, paragraph C, subparagraph (2)
18 and must determine that the contract award is more likely than not to produce benefits
19 to ratepayers that exceed costs to ratepayers in the State.
20 C. If the commission concludes that the solicitation is not competitive or, following a
21 review of bids received, that approval of a contract or contracts is not in the public
22 interest, the commission must make a determination not to award a contract.
23 3. Contract requirements. A contract entered into pursuant to this section must:
24 A. Be for a term of 10 years unless the commission finds a contract for a longer term
25 to be prudent; and
26 B. Require that payments to the bidder under the contract be reduced by the stranded
27 costs arising from energy produced during negative price intervals.
28 Sec. 8. Commission procurement; timing. The Public Utilities Commission
29 shall initiate the competitive solicitation required by the Maine Revised Statutes, Title
30 35-A, section 3210-J, subsection 2, paragraph C-1 within 30 days of the effective date of
31 this Act.'
32 Amend the bill by adding before the summary the following:
33 'Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
34 takes effect when approved.'
35 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
36 number to read consecutively.

3388 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft, changes the title and adds
3399 an emergency preamble and emergency clause. The amendment does the following.
40 1. It amends the section of law requiring the Public Utilities Commission to conduct a
41 renewable energy procurement to direct the commission to conduct a competitive

423 legislation. The commission may initiate an additional competitive solicitation within 12
434 months of the first solicitation. The amendment also clarifies that, to select a contract that
445 will benefit ratepayers, the commission must determine that the contract is more likely than
456 not to produce benefits to ratepayers that exceed the costs to ratepayers associated with the
467 contract.
7 2. It requires the commission to conduct one competitive solicitation to procure energy
8 or renewable energy credits from eligible Class IA resources as defined in the amendment
9 in an amount equal to 5% of the retail electricity sales in this State for the period from
10 January 1, 2023 to December 31, 2023, as determined by the commission. If the
11 commission concludes that the solicitation is not competitive or, following a review of bids
12 received, that approval of a contract or contracts is not in the public interest, the
13 commission must decide not to award a contract.
14 3. It requires that an energy contract resulting from a competitive solicitation conducted
15 by the commission include a provision that requires payments under the contract to the
16 bidder be reduced by the stranded costs arising from energy produced during negative price
17 intervals. The commission may grant an exception from the inclusion of such a negative
18 price provision if the bidder demonstrates good cause and the commission finds that the
19 contract is cost-effective, includes other price protections for ratepayers and will avoid to
20 the maximum extent possible that curtailment of existing renewable resource generators.
