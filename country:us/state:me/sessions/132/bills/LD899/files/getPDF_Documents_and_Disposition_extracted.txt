Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 899
S.P. 388 In Senate, March 11, 2025
Coverage
Submitted by the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation pursuant to Joint
Rule 204.
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on March 5, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered
printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BAILEY of York.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 24-A MRSA §2910-A, sub-§4, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2019, c. 182, §1, is
3 amended to read:
4 B. Medical payments coverage in a casualty insurance policy is assignable only by
5 written agreement between the insured and the casualty insurer. Benefits under
6 medical payments coverage must be applied as directed by the insured.
7 Sec. 2. 24-A MRSA §2910-A, sub-§5 is enacted to read:
8 5. Payments to health care providers. A carrier may not issue a payment under
9 medical payments coverage directly to a health care provider unless the carrier receives a
10 bill from that health care provider that shows the health insurer has paid the health care
11 provider for care related to the claim under which the medical payment is made and that
12 identifies the patient's responsibility for cost sharing, including, but not limited to, a
13 copayment, deductible or coinsurance.
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill adds a requirement regarding medical payments coverage that prohibits
1166 casualty insurers from issuing a payment directly to a provider until the casualty insurer
1177 receives a bill that shows the health insurer has paid the health care provider and that
1188 identifies the patient's responsibility for cost sharing. The bill also requires the assignment
1199 of medical payments coverage to be in writing.
