5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 751, L.D. 1910, “An Act to Strengthen
10 Housing Stability Services by Increasing Support and Outreach”
11 Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the following:
12 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
13 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
14 Whereas, the State continues to face unprecedented homelessness; and
15 Whereas, Public Law 2023, chapter 412, Part AAAA established the Housing First
16 Program to facilitate the delivery of support and stabilization services to persons
17 experiencing chronic homelessness in the State; and
18 Whereas, among the services allowed through the Housing First Program were
19 housing stability services that provide outreach to persons experiencing chronic
20 homelessness for purposes of establishing connections and providing support that may
21 result in securing stable housing; and
22 Whereas, the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 20-A, subsection 4, paragraph
23 A requires the Department of Health and Human Services to annually deposit $1,000,000
24 in the fund to support the Housing First Program to provide housing stability services, and
25 the department has not yet contracted for those services to be provided; and
26 Whereas, continued delays in delivery of help to persons experiencing chronic
27 homelessness creates unnecessary hardships for not only the person but the person's
28 community; and
29 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
30 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
31 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
32 therefore,'
33 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 4 and inserting the following:

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 751, L.D. 1910
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, adds an emergency
33 preamble and emergency clause to the bill. It adds a requirement that the Department of
44 Health and Human Services submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Housing
55 and Economic Development by September 1, 2025, with details on the delivery of housing
66 stability support services required by the Housing First Program and the requests for
77 proposals process required by this legislation. The committee may report out legislation
88 based on the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature. The
99 amendment also replaces the appropriations and allocations section to correct a program
1100 name and number.
