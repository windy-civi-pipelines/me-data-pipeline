7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 193, L.D. 293, “An Act to Eliminate the
10 Debt Limit of the Boothbay Region Water District”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Increase the Debt Limit of the Boothbay Region Water District'
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
14 following:
15 'Sec. 1. P&SL 2001, c. 15, §6 is amended to read:
16 Sec. 6. Borrowing. Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section
17 6412, subsections 1 and 2 and section 6413, the district, in order to accomplish the purpose
18 of this Act, by vote of its board of trustees and without district vote, is authorized to borrow
19 money, including temporary borrowing, for the purposes of acquisition and renovation of
20 the Boothbay Harbor Water System and the East Boothbay Water District system, of
21 renewing and refunding the indebtedness of water-related notes or bonds of the Town of
22 Boothbay Harbor and the East Boothbay Water District, of paying necessary expenses and
23 liabilities incurred under this Act, and of acquiring properties; paying damages; laying
24 pipes, mains, sewers, drains and conduits; purchasing, constructing, maintaining and
25 operating a water system; and making renewals, additions, extensions and improvements
26 to that system, and is authorized to issue, from time to time, bonds, notes or other evidences
27 of indebtedness of the district in such amount or amounts, bearing interest at such rate or
28 rates and having such terms and provisions as the trustees determine. In the case of a vote
29 by the trustees after acquisition and renovation of the Boothbay Harbor Water System and
30 the East Boothbay Water District system to authorize bonds or notes to pay for the
31 acquisition of property, the cost of a water system or part of a water system, renewals or
32 additions or for other improvements in the nature of capital costs, the estimated cost of
33 which singly or in the aggregate included in any one financing is $500,000 or more, but
34 not for renewing or refunding existing indebtedness or to pay for maintenance, repairs or
35 current expenses, notice of the proposed debt and of the general purpose or purposes for
36 which it was authorized must be given by the district by publication at least once in a

317 newspaper having a general circulation in the towns of Boothbay Harbor and Boothbay.
328 The total indebtedness outstanding of the district may not exceed the sum of $12,000,000
339 $19,000,000 at any one time or such other amount as may be established pursuant to this
440 section.
5 Notwithstanding the limitation on total indebtedness established in this section, the
6 trustees of the district may propose a different debt limit and submit that debt limit for
7 districtwide approval in a referendum held in accordance with this section. The referendum
8 must be called, advertised and conducted according to the law relating to municipal
9 elections, except that the registrar of voters is not required to prepare nor the clerk to post
10 a new list of voters. For the purpose of registering voters, the registrar of voters must be in
11 session on the regular workday preceding the election. The question presented must
12 conform to the following form:
13 "Do you favor changing the debt limit of the Boothbay Region Water District from
14 (insert current debt limit) to (insert proposed debt limit)?"
15 The voters shall indicate by a cross or check mark placed against the word "Yes" or
16 "No" their opinion on the question.
17 The results must be declared by the trustees and entered upon the district's records. Due
18 certificate of the results must be filed by the clerk with the Secretary of State.
19 A debt limit proposal becomes effective upon its acceptance by a majority of the legal
20 voters within the district voting at the referendum. Failure of approval by the majority of
21 voters voting at the referendum does not prevent subsequent referenda from being held for
22 the same purpose. The costs of referenda are borne by the district.
23 The total indebtedness of the district at any one time outstanding may not exceed the
24 sum approved by referendum.'
25 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
26 number to read consecutively.
27 SUMMARY
2288 This amendment replaces the bill. It increases the maximum debt limit for the
2299 Boothbay Region Water District from $12,000,000 to $19,000,000.
