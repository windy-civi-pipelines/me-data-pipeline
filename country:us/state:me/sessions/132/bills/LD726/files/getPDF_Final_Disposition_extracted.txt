9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 465, L.D. 726, “An Act to Protect
10 Municipalities' Investment in Law Enforcement Officers”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 25 MRSA §2808, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2023, c. 242, §1, is further
14 amended to read:
15 3. Reimbursement for training costs. Whenever a full-time law enforcement officer,
16 trained at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy or the Indian police academy at the federal
17 law enforcement training center at the expense of a particular governmental entity, is
18 subsequently hired by another governmental entity as a full-time law enforcement officer
19 within 5 years of graduation from the academy, the governmental entity shall reimburse
20 the first governmental entity for the training costs according to the following formula,
21 unless a mutual agreement is reached.
22 A. If the officer is hired by the other governmental entity during the first year 2 years
23 after graduation, that governmental entity shall reimburse the first governmental entity
24 the full cost of the training costs.
25 B. If the officer is hired by the other governmental entity during the 2nd year after
26 graduation, that governmental entity shall reimburse the first governmental entity 80%
27 of the training costs.
28 C. If the officer is hired by the other governmental entity during the 3rd year after
29 graduation, that governmental entity shall reimburse the first governmental entity 60%
30 80% of the training costs.
31 D. If the officer is hired by the other governmental entity during the 4th year after
32 graduation, that governmental entity shall reimburse the first governmental entity 40%
33 60% of the training costs.

8 graduation from the academy, each of those governmental entities is liable to the
9 governmental employer immediately preceding it for the training costs paid by that
10 governmental entity under this subsection. The extent of financial liability must be
11 determined according to the formula established by this subsection.
12 The board shall, as necessary, incorporate the Indian police academy at the federal law

14 rates.
