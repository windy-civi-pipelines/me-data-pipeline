9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1097, L.D. 1656, “An Act to Facilitate
10 Compliance with Federal Immigration Law by State and Local Government Entities”
11 Amend the bill in section 1 in Pt. 32 in c. 641 in §26002 in subsection 6 in the last line
12 (page 2, line 10 in L.D.) by inserting after the following: "Safety." the following: '"Law
13 enforcement agency" does not include any person designated by the State Court
14 Administrator pursuant to Title 4, section 17, subsection 15 to provide court security
15 services.'
16 Amend the bill in section 1 in Pt. 32 in c. 641 in §26002 in subsection 10 in the last
17 line (page 2, line 20 in L.D.) by inserting after the following: "State." the following: '"State
18 government entity" does not include the judicial branch.'
19 Amend the bill in section 1 in Pt. 32 in c. 641 in §26004 in the headnote in the first line
20 (page 3, line 20 in L.D.) by striking out the following: "; appeals"
21 Amend the bill in section 1 in Pt. 32 in c. 641 in §26004 by striking out all of subsection
22 2 (page 3, lines 27 to 32 in L.D.) and inserting the following:
23 '2. Attorney General investigation; enforcement action. The Attorney General shall
24 investigate each complaint and request received under subsection 1. If the Attorney
25 General believes that a violation of section 26003 has occurred, the Attorney General shall
26 bring an action in Superior Court to enjoin the state government entity, local government
27 entity or law enforcement agency from violating section 26003. A person that violates
28 the terms of an injunction issued under this subsection is subject to a civil penalty of $500
29 for each day that a violation occurs.'
30 Amend the bill in section 1 in Pt. 32 in c. 641 in §26004 by striking out all of subsection
31 3 (page 3, lines 33 to 43 in L.D.).
32 Amend the bill in section 1 in Pt. 32 in c. 641 in §26005 in the indented paragraph in
33 the last 2 lines (page 4, lines 5 and 6 in L.D.) by striking out the following: "the federal
34 Whistleblower Protection Act of 1989." and inserting the following: 'the Whistleblowers'
35 Protection Act.'

16 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section

18 SUMMARY
1199 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, makes the following
2200 changes to the bill.
21 1. It excludes the judicial branch and court security services from the scope of the bill.
22 2. It provides that a state government entity official, local government entity official,
23 law enforcement agency official or person holding public office who reports a violation to
24 the Attorney General is protected by the state Whistleblowers' Protection Act, not the
25 federal Whistleblower Protection Act of 1989, as in the bill.
26 3. It replaces the provisions of the bill governing the process for enjoining violations
27 of the bill's provisions. Under the amendment, if the Attorney General believes that a
28 violation of the bill's provisions has occurred, the Attorney General must bring an action in
29 Superior Court to enjoin the violation. A person that violates the terms of an injunction
30 issued by the Superior Court is subject to a civil penalty of $500 for each day that a violation
31 occurs.
3322 4. It also adds an appropriations and allocations section.
