9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 583, L.D. 918, “An Act to Allow a
10 Qualifying Religious Organization to Self-insure for Automobile Insurance”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Allow a Qualifying Religious Organization Whose Religion Prohibits Using
13 Insurance to Self-insure for Automobile Insurance'
14 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 4 and inserting the following:
15 'Sec. 4. 29-A MRSA §1613 is enacted to read:
16 §1613. Self-insurance permitted for certain religious organizations
17 Notwithstanding any other provision of this Title to the contrary, the Secretary of State
18 may grant an exemption from any provision of this subchapter for any motor vehicle owned
19 or operated by a religious organization or its members subject to the following minimum
20 requirements.
21 1. Qualifying religious organization. In order to be eligible to self-insure under this
22 section, a religious organization must demonstrate with documentation to the satisfaction
23 of the Secretary of State that:
24 A. The religious organization has been recognized by the United States Social Security
25 Administration as an eligible religious group under Section 1402(g) of the United
26 States Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, and has filed Form 4029 or a
27 substantially similar form with the federal Internal Revenue Service;
28 B. The religious organization has established tenets and faith-based teachings and has
29 been in existence continuously since December 31, 1950;
30 C. The religious organization and its members operate 5 or more motor vehicles in this
31 State that are owned or leased by the religious organization or one of its members;
32 D. Each member of the religious organization that owns a motor vehicle has filed Form
33 4029 or a substantially similar form with the federal Internal Revenue Service;

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 583, L.D. 918
1 E. The religious organization and its members hold a common belief in mutual
2 financial assistance in time of need to the extent that they share in financial obligations
3 of members who would otherwise not be able to meet their obligations;
4 F. The religious organization and its members have met all of their responsibilities for
5 financial responsibility under this subchapter for the 2 years preceding its application
6 to self-insure under this section;
7 G. The religious organization is financially solvent and not subject to any actions of
8 bankruptcy, trusteeship, receivership or any other court proceedings in which the
9 financial solvency of the religious organization is at issue;
10 H. The religious organization and its members are not subject to any judgments arising
11 out of the operation, maintenance or use of a motor vehicle that have remained
12 unsatisfied for more than 30 days after final judgment was issued;
13 I. There are no factors causing the Secretary of State to believe that the religious
14 organization and its members do not have the financial ability to pay any future
15 judgments against them; and
16 J. The religious organization and its members meet any other requirements prescribed
17 in rules adopted in accordance with this section.
18 2. Requirements for self-insurance. Upon application under this section, the
19 religious organization shall file with the Treasurer of State satisfactory proof in the form of
20 an irrevocable letter of credit from a qualified financial institution valid for a period of 6
21 years, and updated annually, or the deposit of money and securities as provided in section
22 1605, subsection 3, paragraph B and section 1605, subsection 4 that the religious
23 organization meets the following minimum requirements:
24 A. For 5 to 10 motor vehicles, the amount of the irrevocable letter of credit or deposit
25 of money and securities must be equal to a combined single limit of $250,000; and
26 B. For more than 10 motor vehicles, the amount in paragraph A must be increased by
27 $5,000 per motor vehicle.
28 Upon application under this section, the religious organization shall submit to the Secretary
29 of State a receipt from the Treasurer of State showing the filing of an irrevocable letter of
30 credit or the deposit of money and securities in the amounts required by this subsection and
31 a list of the motor vehicles owned or operated by the religious organization and its
32 members, including information identifying for each motor vehicle the legal owner of the
33 motor vehicle; the vehicle identification number; and the make, model and year of the
34 motor vehicle.
35 Upon receiving an application in accordance with this subsection, the Secretary of State
36 may require a religious organization to submit a fee not to exceed $100 as established in
37 rules adopted by the Secretary of State pursuant to subsection 6.
38 3. Issuance of certificate of self-insurance. Upon demonstration that a religious
39 organization has met the requirements of subsections 1 and 2 and has paid any required
40 application fee, the Secretary of State shall issue a certificate of self-insurance to the
41 religious organization. The certificate of self-insurance serves as evidence of financial
42 responsibility required by section 1605, subsection 2. Any member of the religious
43 organization may provide the certificate of self-insurance as evidence of financial

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 583, L.D. 918
414 responsibility required by section 1605, subsection 2 to register a motor vehicle owned or
425 operated by that member.
3 4. Quarterly reporting. A religious organization granted a certificate of self-insurance
4 under this section shall report to the Secretary of State on a quarterly basis on any claims
5 made to the organization arising out of the operation, maintenance or use of a motor vehicle
6 owned or operated by the religious organization and its members, including, but not limited
7 to, the number of claims made during the reporting period; the number of claims still open
8 from a previous reporting period; the number of claims satisfied during the reporting
9 period; and for each claim made during the reporting period, the date the incident
10 underlying the claim occurred, the type of the claim, the amount paid, the date of any
11 payment made by the religious organization, the name of the individual paid and the name
12 of any insurance company or insurance adjuster involved in the claim. The religious
13 organization shall also provide on a quarterly basis an updated list of motor vehicles owned
14 or operated by the religious organization and its members as initially required upon
15 application in accordance with subsection 2 if any motor vehicle owner or operated by the
16 religious organization and its members is removed or added.
17 5. Notice of withdrawal. A religious organization granted a certificate of self-
18 insurance under this section shall notify the Secretary of State in writing if any member of
19 the religious organization ceases to be a member within 10 days of that member's departure
20 or removal from the religious organization.
21 6. Termination of self-insurance. The Secretary of State may:
22 A. At any time after granting a certificate of self-insurance, terminate the authorization
23 for self-insurance if the secretary determines that the religious organization no longer
24 meets the requirements for self-insurance under this section. Notice of the termination
25 must be provided to the religious organization in writing at least 30 days prior to the
26 termination of the authorization; and
27 B. Terminate the authorization for self-insurance if the religious organization or its
28 members are subject to a judgment arising out of the operation, maintenance or use of
29 a motor vehicle and that judgment has remained unsatisfied for more than 30 days after
30 the final judgment was issued. The Treasurer of State may hold deposited money or
31 securities until the judgment is satisfied.
32 7. Rules. The Secretary of State may adopt rules as necessary to implement the
33 requirements of this section. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical
34 rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
35 8. Applicability. A certificate of self-insurance granted by the Secretary of State
36 pursuant to this section does not apply to:
37 A. A motor vehicle with a registered gross weight of 26,001 pounds or more or a motor
38 vehicle requiring its operator to hold a commercial driver's license;
39 B. An emergency vehicle;
40 C. A for-hire transportation vehicle for transporting freight or merchandise;
41 D. A for-hire transportation vehicle for transporting passengers;
42 E. A school bus; or
