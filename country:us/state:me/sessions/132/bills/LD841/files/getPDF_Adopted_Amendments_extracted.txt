8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 374, L.D. 841, “Resolve, to Study the
10 Delivery of Emergency Medical Services to and Ferry Service Effects on Island
11 Communities in the State”
12 Amend the resolve by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, to Study the Delivery of Emergency Medical Services to and Ferry Service
14 Effects on Unbridged Island Communities in the State'
15 Amend the resolve by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
16 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
17 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
18 Whereas, emergency medical services are essential services and failing to provide
19 these essential services adversely affects public health; and
20 Whereas, addressing the delivery of emergency medical services to unbridged island
21 communities will require input and collaboration from several stakeholders; and
22 Whereas, this legislation must take effect before the expiration of the 90-day period
23 in order to address these issues; and
24 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
25 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
26 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
27 therefore, be it
28 Sec. 1. Department of Public Safety, Maine Emergency Medical Services
29 to convene working group. Resolved: That the Department of Public Safety, Maine
30 Emergency Medical Services shall convene a working group to study the delivery of
31 emergency medical services to and ferry service implications on unbridged island
32 communities in the State. The study must include an evaluation of the long-term
33 sustainability of emergency medical services to unbridged islands, legal and regulatory

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 374, L.D. 841
314 requirements related to such services and a cost comparison to emergency medical services
325 needs and costs statewide.
3 Sec. 2. Membership. Resolved: That the working group under section 1 consists
4 of the following members:
5 1. The Director of Maine Emergency Medical Services within the Department of
6 Public Safety, or the director's designee;
7 2. A representative of a municipal government of an unbridged island community that
8 is served by the Department of Transportation, Maine State Ferry Service;
9 3. A representative of a municipal government of an unbridged island community that
10 is not served by the Department of Transportation, Maine State Ferry Service;
11 4. A representative who provides emergency medical services on an unbridged island
12 community served by the Department of Transportation, Maine State Ferry Service;
13 5. A representative of the Department of Transportation, Maine State Ferry Service;
14 6. A representative of an organization with experience in providing air ambulance
15 critical care transport services in the State;
16 7. A representative of a statewide association representing ambulance services; and
17 8. A representative of a municipal fire department that provides marine emergency
18 medical services that are not primarily facilitated through a ferry service to an unbridged
19 island community.
20 Sec. 3. Report. Resolved: That, by January 1, 2026, the Department of Public
21 Safety, Maine Emergency Medical Services shall submit a report that includes the findings
22 and recommendations of the working group under section 1, including suggested
23 legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public
24 Safety and to the Joint Standing Committee on Transportation. Both committees may
25 submit legislation based on the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd
26 Legislature.
27 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
28 takes effect when approved.'
29 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
30 section number to read consecutively.
31 SUMMARY
3322 This amendment replaces the resolve and adds an emergency preamble and emergency
3333 clause. The amendment requires the working group to study the long-term sustainability
3344 of emergency medical services to unbridged island communities, legal and regulatory
3355 requirements related to such services and a cost comparison to emergency medical services
3366 needs and costs statewide.
37 The amendment changes the working group in the following ways.
38 1. The Department of Public Safety, Maine Emergency Medical Services is to convene
39 the working group instead of the Maine Office of Community Affairs.
Page 2 - 132LR2012(02)
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 374, L.D. 841
1 2. The resolve requires a representative of an unbridged island community that is
2 served by the Department of Transportation, Maine State Ferry Service and an unbridged
3 island community that is not. The amendment clarifies that these representatives represent
4 a municipal government on the unbridged island communities.
5 3. It requires, to serve as a member, the Director of Maine Emergency Medical Services
6 within the Department of Public Safety instead of the director of the Maine Office of
7 Community Affairs.
8 4. It removes a representative of emergency medical services of an island community
9 and replaces it with a representative providing emergency medical services on an unbridged
10 island community served by the Department of Transportation, Maine State Ferry Service.
11 5. It adds a representative of a statewide association representing ambulance services.
12 6. It adds a representative of a municipal fire department that provides marine
13 emergency medical services that are not primarily facilitated through a ferry service to an
14 unbridged island community.
15 The amendment requires that the report also be sent to the Joint Standing Committee
16 on Transportation in addition to the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and
17 Public Safety. Both committees have the authority to submit legislation based on the report
18 to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature. The amendment makes other
19 technical changes.
