9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 976, L.D. 1485, “An Act to Create a
10 Minimum Standard of Pathology for Children Under 3 Years of Age Who Die of Sudden
11 Infant Death Syndrome or an Unknown Cause”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
13 following:
14 'Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §3028, sub-§9-A is enacted to read:
15 9-A. Certain deaths of children under 3 years of age. If a medical examiner has
16 determined that a child under 3 years of age has died as a result of sudden unknown infant
17 death syndrome, suffocation or strangulation or the medical examiner is unable to
18 determine the cause of death of the child, the Chief Medical Examiner shall conduct:
19 A. A toxicology test that includes a complete blood count test and testing for c-reactive
20 protein, aluminum, arsenic, cadmium, hexavalent chromium, iron, lead, manganese,
21 mercury and branched and linear perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances; and
22 B. At the request of the parent or guardian of the child, any additional forensic
23 toxicology testing as long as the testing is readily available or accessible.
24 The Chief Medical Examiner shall obtain, preserve and retain sufficient tissue and fluid
25 samples and other evidence necessary to make a subsequent determination of the cause of
26 death for 2 years following the date of the death of the child. Such evidence must be made
27 available to an accredited or licensed testing facility at the request of the parent or guardian
28 of the child.
29 Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
30 allocations are made.
31 ATTORNEY GENERAL, DEPARTMENT OF THE
32 Chief Medical Examiner - Office of 0412
33 Initiative: Provides funding for additional toxicology testing of certain deaths of children
34 under 3 years of age.

8 SUMMARY
99 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, makes the following
1100 changes to the bill.
11 1. It replaces "sudden infant death" with the more current terminology of "sudden
12 unknown infant death."
13 2. It requires the Chief Medical Examiner to conduct a toxicology test for any child
14 under 3 years of age who died as a result of sudden unknown infant death syndrome,
15 suffocation or strangulation or whose death is unexplained. The test must include a
16 complete blood count test and testing for c-reactive protein, aluminum, arsenic, cadmium,
17 hexavalent chromium, iron, lead, manganese, mercury and branched and linear
18 perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances.
19 3. It requires the Chief Medical Examiner, at the request of the parent or guardian of
20 the child, to conduct any additional forensic toxicology testing as long as it is readily
21 available or accessible.
22 4. It requires the Chief Medical Examiner to obtain, preserve and retain sufficient tissue
23 and fluid samples for 2 years after the death of the child. The evidence must be made
24 available to a testing facility at the request of the parent or guardian of the child.
2255 5. It adds an appropriations and allocations section.
