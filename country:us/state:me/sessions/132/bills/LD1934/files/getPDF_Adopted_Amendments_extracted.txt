4 Amend the bill in section 1 in c. 21 in §481 by striking out all of subsection 4 (page 3,

6 '4. Rulemaking. The Department of Administrative and Financial Services may adopt
7 rules governing the implementation of this chapter and may establish appropriate
8 exemptions to the requirements of this chapter. The Department of Public Safety may
9 adopt rules establishing exemptions as described in subsection 3, paragraph B. The
10 Department of Transportation may adopt rules governing the lighting of roadways and
11 pedestrian facilities to ensure the safety and efficiency of the traveling public. Any
12 department proposing rules that affect municipalities shall confer with the Maine Office of
13 Community Affairs before proposing those rules. All rules adopted pursuant to this
14 subsection are major substantive rules as defined in chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.'
15 Amend the bill in section 1 in c. 21 in §481 by inserting after subsection 4 the
16 following:
17 '5. Local ordinances authorized. A municipality in the State may adopt a local
18 ordinance to promote compliance with this chapter and to extend its standards as
19 appropriate beyond public improvements governed by this chapter. No later than
20 September 30, 2026, the Maine Office of Community Affairs shall develop and share with
21 municipalities a model ordinance for these purposes.
22 This subsection may not be construed to prohibit a municipality from adopting lighting
23 ordinances stricter than those adopted by the State pursuant to this chapter, except that
24 municipalities may not adopt lighting ordinances that conflict with any of the provisions in
25 Title 23, section 708.'

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1295, L.D. 1934
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, amends the bill by
33 excluding ski facilities from the requirements applicable to outdoor sports lighting. It also
44 includes exemptions for lighting regulations, orders or guidance from the Federal Highway
55 Administration or other division of the United States Department of Transportation. The
66 amendment also provides exemptions for Department of Transportation roadway lighting
77 pursuant to Title 23, section 708 and pedestrian lighting for sidewalks and crosswalk
88 facilities and authorizes the Department of Administrative and Financial Services to
99 establish appropriate exemptions to the requirements of this legislation. It also provides
1100 that a municipality in the State may adopt local ordinances to promote compliance with this
1111 legislation and to extend its standards as appropriate beyond public improvements
1122 governed by this legislation and requires that, no later than September 30, 2026, the Maine
1133 Office of Community Affairs must develop and share with municipalities a model
1144 ordinance for these purposes.
