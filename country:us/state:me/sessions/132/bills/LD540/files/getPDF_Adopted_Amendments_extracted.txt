10 State's Unidentified Human Remains”
11 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following:
12 'Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §3028-A, as amended by PL 2025, c. 14, §1, is repealed and the
13 following enacted in its place:
14 §3028-A. Disposal of unidentified human remains and abandoned human remains;
15 identification
16 This section governs the storage, identification, disposition and expenses related to
17 human remains.
18 1. Storage and disposition of human remains. Whenever unidentified human
19 remains are recovered, the Chief Medical Examiner may store the remains, release them to
20 an educational institution, inter them in an appropriate resting place or have them cremated
21 in accordance with this section. Ashes of remains cremated may be disposed of in any
22 appropriate manner. Human remains uncovered in a cared-for cemetery are excluded from
23 the operation of this subsection. Human remains known to be Indian remains are excluded
24 from the operation of this subsection and must be handled in the manner required by section
25 2842-B.
26 The Chief Medical Examiner may assume responsibility for the disposal of identified
27 human remains of a deceased resident of this State that are the subject of a medical
28 examiner case if no one takes custody and control of the human remains for a period of 15
29 days after the Chief Medical Examiner has completed an autopsy or necessary examination
30 of the human remains, made reasonable inquiry under section 3028‑D, subsection 1 and
31 satisfied the requirements of subsection 2. Such abandoned remains may be interred or
32 cremated. The Chief Medical Examiner shall file or cause to be filed a certificate of
33 abandonment in the municipality where the human remains were recovered that indicates
34 the means of disposal.
35 In the absence of a responsible party, payment of expenses incurred by the Chief Medical
36 Examiner pursuant to this section must be made pursuant to section 3028‑D, subsection 2

3 2. Identification of human remains using DNA. After the Chief Medical Examiner
4 has had possession of unidentified human remains for 45 days, the Chief Medical Examiner
5 shall test the deoxyribonucleic acid, or DNA, of the unidentified human remains using
6 forensic genetic genealogy testing. For purposes of this subsection, "forensic genetic
7 genealogy testing" means a DNA analysis of a sample of biological material by a laboratory
8 to develop a profile and search for that profile in a database of personal genomics or using
9 a direct-to-consumer genetic genealogy service.'
