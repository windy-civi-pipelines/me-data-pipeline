Section 1:
1 L.D. 1264
2 Date: (Filing No. H- )
3 AGRICULTURE, CONSERVATION AND FORESTRY
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 839, L.D. 1264, “Resolve, Requiring the
10 Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands in the Department of Agriculture, Conservation
11 and Forestry to Modify the Deed That Conveyed Montpelier, the General Henry Knox
12 Museum in Thomaston, to the Friends of Montpelier”
13 Amend the resolve in section 1 in the last 5 lines (page 1, lines 18 to 22 in L.D.) by
14 striking out the following: "public education purposes and remove the reversion clause
15 specifying that, if the Friends of Montpelier do not continue to use the building and the
16 collections for public educational, historic or cultural purposes, the building and collections
17 will revert to the Bureau of Parks and Lands" and inserting the following: 'remove any
18 requirement that Montpelier be preserved, rehabilitated or restored in a manner as required
19 by the Maine Historic Preservation Commission'
20 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
21 section number to read consecutively.
22 SUMMARY
2233 This amendment directs the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry,
2244 Bureau of Parks and Lands to modify the deed that conveyed Montpelier, the General
2255 Henry Knox Museum in the Town of Thomaston, to the Friends of Montpelier to remove
2266 any requirement that Montpelier be preserved, rehabilitated or restored in a manner as
2277 required by the Maine Historic Preservation Commission.
28 The amendment removes from the resolve provisions that direct the bureau to modify
29 the deed to remove the requirement that Montpelier be used for public education purposes
30 and to remove the reversion clause specifying that if the Friends of Montpelier do not
31 continue to use the building and collections for public educational, historic or cultural
32 purposes, the building and collections will revert to the bureau.
