2 Date: (Filing No. S- )
3 INLAND FISHERIES AND WILDLIFE
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 75, L.D. 139, “Resolve, Directing the
10 Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to Establish a Pilot Program for the Electronic
11 Tagging of Deer”
12 Amend the resolve by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, Directing the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to Study
14 Establishing a Program for the Electronic Tagging of Deer'
15 Amend the resolve by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following:
16 'Sec. 1. Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to study establishing
17 program for electronic tagging of deer. Resolved: That the Department of Inland
18 Fisheries and Wildlife shall study and establish a plan for the implementation of a program
19 for the electronic tagging of harvested deer. The department shall submit a report, including
20 suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries and Wildlife no
21 later than January 14, 2026. The report must include information on the projected costs,
22 programming and staffing requirements of developing and implementing an electronic
23 tagging program and how the department would recoup lost money, if applicable. To the
24 extent practicable, the department shall consult with local business owners in the study who
25 contract as registered agents of the department for the tagging of deer to gauge how a
26 transition to an electronic tagging program may impact these agents, especially in more
27 rural areas of the State. The report must also include information related to anticipated
28 effects on biological data collection and relevant compliance and enforcement issues.
29 Finally, the report must outline a plan for program implementation, including suggested
30 time frames, and a plan for how the department would raise awareness of an electronic
31 tagging program across the State. The Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries and
32 Wildlife may report out a bill based on the report to the Second Regular Session of the
33 132nd Legislature.'
34 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
35 section number to read consecutively.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 75, L.D. 139
1 SUMMARY
22 This amendment replaces the resolve and changes the title. Whereas the resolve directs
33 the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to establish a program for the electronic
44 tagging of deer and report back to the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries and
55 Wildlife no later than December 3, 2025, the amendment directs the department to study
66 and establish a plan for such a program and report back to the committee no later than
77 January 14, 2026. The committee is authorized to report out a bill based on the report to
88 the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
