132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1581
H.P. 1039 House of Representatives, April 10, 2025
Certain Veterans
Reference to the Committee on Taxation suggested and ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative NUTTING of Oakland.
Cosponsored by Representative GREENWOOD of Wales,
Senator CYRWAY of Kennebec and
Representatives: BRIDGEO of Augusta, COLLINS of Sidney, PARRY of Arundel.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §1483, sub-§12, as amended by PL 2021, c. 240, §1, is further
3 amended to read:
4 12. Certain veterans. Automobiles owned by veterans who are granted free
5 registration of those vehicles by the Secretary of State under Title 29‑A, section 523,
6 subsection 1 or who are disabled by injury or disease incurred or aggravated during active
7 military service in the line of duty and are receiving any form of pension or compensation
8 from the United States Government for total, service-connected disability. In order for an
9 automobile to qualify for an exemption from the excise tax under this subsection, the name
10 of the qualifying veteran must appear on the automobile's certificate of title. If there are
11 multiple owners of an automobile, at least one of the owners whose name appears on the
12 certificate of title must meet the requirements of this subsection in order for the automobile
13 to qualify for the exemption;
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill requires that in order for an automobile to qualify for the excise tax exemption
1166 provided in current law for certain veterans who are disabled, the name of the qualifying
1177 veteran must appear on the automobile's certificate of title.
