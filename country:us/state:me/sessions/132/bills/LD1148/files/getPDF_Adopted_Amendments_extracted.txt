8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 753, L.D. 1148, “An Act to Improve
10 Access to Absentee Ballots for Municipal Elections for Uniformed Service Voters and
11 Overseas Voters”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, Directing the Secretary of State to Study Providing Access to Absentee
14 Ballots for Municipal Elections to Uniformed Service Voters or Overseas Voters in
15 the Same Manner as Absentee Ballots for Statewide Elections'
16 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
17 'Sec. 1. Department of Secretary of State to study providing access to
18 absentee ballots for municipal elections to uniformed service voters or overseas
19 voters in same manner as absentee ballots for statewide elections. Resolved:
20 That the Department of the Secretary of State shall study the feasibility of and methods for
21 providing digital access to and receipt of local absentee ballots for uniformed service voters
22 or overseas voters from a municipality holding an election on the same date as a statewide
23 election. In conducting this study, the Department of the Secretary of State may consult
24 with members of an association representing town and city clerks.
25 Sec. 2. Department of Secretary of State to update information provided to
26 uniformed service voters or overseas voters regarding access to and return of
27 local absentee ballots. Resolved: That the Department of the Secretary of State shall
28 update its publicly accessible website or the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee
29 Voting Act absentee ballot retrieval service portal, as applicable, to include clearly and
30 conspicuously that local absentee ballots must be obtained from the uniformed service
31 voter's or overseas voter's municipality of residence and that the process for applying for,
32 receiving and returning absentee ballots through the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens
33 Absentee Voting Act absentee ballot retrieval service portal applies only to statewide and
34 federal elections. The Secretary of State shall make easily accessible the contact
35 information for each municipality in the State.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 753, L.D. 1148
1 Sec. 3. Report. Resolved: That, no later than December 3, 2025, the Secretary of
2 State shall submit a report based upon the study in section 1, including an update on the
3 information required under section 2, along with any recommended legislation, to the Joint
4 Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs, which may report out legislation
5 related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.'
6 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
7 number to read consecutively.
8 SUMMARY
99 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve that directs the Department of the
1100 Secretary of State to study the feasibility of and methods for providing digital access to and
1111 receipt of local absentee ballots for uniformed service voters or overseas voters from a
1122 municipality holding an election on the same date as a statewide election. The department
1133 must also update its publicly accessible website and the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens
1144 Absentee Voting Act absentee ballot retrieval service portal to include information
1155 explaining that local absentee ballots are not included as a part of this service and that a
1166 uniformed service voter or overseas voter must obtain absentee ballots for local elections
1177 from the voter's municipality of residence. The department must provide contact
1188 information for each municipality on its website.
19 The Secretary of State shall submit a report of the findings, including recommended
20 legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs by December
21 3, 2025. The report must also include an update on the changes to the website and the portal.
22 The committee may report out legislation related to the report in the Second Regular
23 Session of the 132nd Legislature.
