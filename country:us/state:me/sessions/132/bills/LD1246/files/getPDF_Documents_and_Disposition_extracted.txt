Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1246
H.P. 821 House of Representatives, March 25, 2025
Development to Convene a Working Group to Review the Process of
Setting Impact Fees
Reference to the Committee on Housing and Economic Development suggested and
ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative GERE of Kennebunkport.
Cosponsored by Representatives: ANKELES of Brunswick, JULIA of Waterville, MALON of
Biddeford, ROEDER of Bangor, SACHS of Freeport.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Sec. 1. Department of Economic and Community Development to study
2 impact fees. Resolved: That the Department of Economic and Community
3 Development, in collaboration with the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future, shall
4 convene a working group to study the process by which municipalities impose impact fees
5 under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 4354. The working group must
6 include representatives of municipalities and developers associated with municipal
7 infrastructure improvements and others with relevant experience. The study must include
8 a review of the existing process by which municipalities establish and impose impact fees,
9 guidance related to impact fees provided to developers and documentation municipalities
10 use in establishing impact fees. For the purposes of this section, "impact fee" has the same
11 meaning as in Title 30-A, section 4301, subsection 6-A. By December 3, 2025, the
12 department shall submit a report based on the study, including recommendations and any
13 suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Housing and Economic
14 Development, and the committee may report out legislation related to the report to the
15 Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
16 SUMMARY
1177 This resolve requires the Department of Economic and Community Development, in
1188 collaboration with the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future, to convene a working
1199 group to study the process by which municipalities impose impact fees under the Maine
2200 Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 4354. The working group must include
2211 representatives of municipalities and developers associated with municipal infrastructure
2222 improvements and others with relevant experience. The department must submit a report
2233 based on the study, including recommendations and any suggested legislation, to the Joint
2244 Standing Committee on Housing and Economic Development, and the committee may
2255 report out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd
2266 Legislature.
