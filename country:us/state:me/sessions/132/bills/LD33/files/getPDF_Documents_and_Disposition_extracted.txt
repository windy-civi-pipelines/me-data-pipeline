132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 33
S.P. 51 In Senate, January 8, 2025
Submitted by the Department of Public Safety pursuant to Joint Rule 204.
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on January 6, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Veterans and Legal Affairs pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator HICKMAN of Kennebec.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 8 MRSA §1101, sub-§4, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 303, §2, is amended
3 to read:
4 A. One Six or more fantasy contestants pay an entry fee to participate;
5 Sec. 2. 8 MRSA §1101, sub-§9-A is enacted to read:
6 9-A. Proposition bet. "Proposition bet" means a wager made regarding the
7 occurrence or nonoccurrence of an event or circumstance during a sports event that is not
8 dependent on the final outcome of the sports event, including a contest in which a contestant
9 must choose, directly or indirectly, whether an individual athlete or a single team will
10 surpass an identified statistical achievement, such as points scored.
11 Sec. 3. 8 MRSA §1103, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 303, §2, is amended to
12 read:
13 1. License required. A person or fantasy contest operator may not offer a fantasy
14 contest in the State without first being licensed by the director, except during such time as
15 the person's or fantasy contest operator's application for a license is pending before the
16 director as provided in subsection 9.
17 Sec. 4. 8 MRSA §1103, sub-§3, ¶F, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 303, §2, is amended
18 to read:
19 F. The type and estimated number of fantasy contests to be conducted during the term
20 of the license and during any period of operation authorized under subsection 9;
21 Sec. 5. 8 MRSA §1103, sub-§6, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 303, §2, is amended to
22 read:
23 6. License fee; term. The initial and renewal fee for a license for a fantasy contest
24 operator that had gross fantasy contest revenues during the 12 months preceding
25 application equal to or greater than $100,000 is $2,500 $5,000. A fantasy contest operator
26 that had gross fantasy contest revenues during the 12 months preceding application of less
27 than $100,000 is not required to pay a license fee. Licenses must be renewed annually.
28 Sec. 6. 8 MRSA §1103, sub-§9, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 303, §2, is repealed.
29 Sec. 7. 8 MRSA §1104, sub-§2-A is enacted to read:
30 2-A. Proposition bets prohibited. A fantasy contest may not be based on a
31 proposition bet or a contest that has the effect of mimicking a proposition bet.
32 Sec. 8. 8 MRSA §1105, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 303, §2, is amended to
33 read:
34 2. Allocation of funds. The director shall collect for deposit to the General Fund 10%
35 of gross fantasy contest revenues, including any revenues earned while operating pending
36 approval of an application submitted to the director as described in section 1103, subsection
37 9, from a fantasy contest operator licensed under this chapter that has gross fantasy contest
38 revenues of $100,000 or greater.
Page 1 - 132LR0297(01)
1 SUMMARY
22 This bill updates the definition of "fantasy contest" to mean a contest in which 6 or
33 more contestants pay an entry fee to participate and adds a definition of "proposition bet."
44 It also updates the initial and renewal fee for a license for a fantasy contest operator to
55 $5,000 and repeals the law allowing a fantasy contest operator to operate a fantasy contest
66 while an initial license, license renewal or license transfer is pending. The bill also
77 prohibits fantasy contests based on proposition betting.
