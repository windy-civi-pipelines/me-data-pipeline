8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 801, L.D. 1226, “An Act to Protect
10 Consumers by Licensing Residential Building Contractors”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, Establishing a Working Group to Study the Regulation of Residential
13 Construction Contractors for the Protection of Consumers'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
15 'Sec. 1. Regulation of residential construction contractors for the
16 protection of consumers working group. Resolved: That the Office of Policy
17 Innovation and the Future shall convene a working group to review potential requirements
18 necessary for the regulation of residential construction contractors to effectively protect
19 consumers and develop recommendations.
20 1. The Director of the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future or the director's
21 designee shall participate in and chair the working group and shall invite the participation
22 in the working group of the following members: 3 members who are residential
23 construction contractors, including at least one who is a member of a construction trade
24 association; one member who is a municipal code enforcement officer; one member who
25 is an attorney with experience in residential construction and contract law; one member
26 who is a representative of the University of Maine System who oversees career and
27 technical education programs; one member who is a residential architect; and 2 members
28 of the public. Members of the working group serve without compensation. The Office of
29 Policy Innovation and the Future shall provide necessary staffing services to the working
30 group.
31 2. The working group shall review potential requirements necessary for and develop
32 recommendations regarding the regulation of residential construction contractors to
33 effectively protect consumers, including, but not limited to, consideration of residential
34 construction contractor licensing requirements and registration requirements. In
35 conducting its review, the working group may review the regulation of residential
36 construction contractors in other states.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 801, L.D. 1226
1 3. On or before December 3, 2025, the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future shall
2 submit to the Joint Standing Committee on Housing and Economic Development a report
3 containing the findings and recommendations of the working group under this section.
4 After reviewing the report, the committee may report out legislation relating to the report
5 to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.'
6 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
7 number to read consecutively.
8 SUMMARY
99 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill with
1100 a resolve. The amendment directs the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future to convene
1111 a working group to review potential requirements necessary for and develop
1122 recommendations regarding the regulation of residential construction contractors to
1133 effectively protect consumers, including, but not limited to, consideration of residential
1144 construction contractor licensing requirements and registration requirements. No later than
1155 December 3, 2025, the office must submit a report that includes its findings and
1166 recommendations to the Joint Standing Committee on Housing and Economic
1177 Development. After reviewing the report, the committee may report out legislation related
1188 to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
