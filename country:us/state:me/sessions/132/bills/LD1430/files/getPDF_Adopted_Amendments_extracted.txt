9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 939, L.D. 1430, “An Act to Promote the
10 Welfare of Disabled Veterans Through the Transfer of Moose Permits”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act Regarding the Transfer of Moose Permits to Disabled Veterans'
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
14 following:
15 'Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §11154, sub-§5, as repealed and replaced by PL 2017, c. 427,
16 §14 and affected by §19, is amended to read:
17 5. Eligibility. Except as provided in this subsection, a resident or nonresident who is
18 eligible to obtain a Maine hunting license or who will be eligible to obtain a Maine hunting
19 license by the opening day of the open moose season is eligible to apply for a moose
20 hunting permit. A person who has obtained a moose hunting permit is ineligible to obtain
21 another permit until the 4th calendar year after the issuance of the last permit. This
22 limitation does not apply to subpermittees under subsection 7 or transferors and transferees
23 under subsection 16. A person under 10 years of age on the opening day of the open moose
24 season is eligible to apply for a moose hunting permit and may accrue points under
25 subsection 8 but is ineligible to receive a moose hunting permit.
26 Sec. 2. 12 MRSA §11154, sub-§16, as enacted by PL 2019, c. 191, §1, is amended
27 to read:
28 16. Moose permit transfer; disabled veterans. A person who holds a valid moose
29 permit may transfer, in accordance with this subsection, that permit to a disabled veteran
30 who meets the eligibility and permit requirements under this section and section 10853,
31 subsection 4 and who is not otherwise prohibited from holding a moose permit. The
32 commissioner may authorize a permit holder to transfer the moose permit to a disabled
33 veteran identified by the permit holder or the permit holder may return the permit to the
34 department, which, in accordance with rules adopted by the commissioner, shall provide
35 for the transfer of the permit to a disabled veteran. A transferor and a transferee of the

316 permit are subject to the elimination of any accumulated points under subsection 8 and the
327 3-year ineligibility period under subsection 5. Rules adopted under this subsection are
338 routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.
4 Sec. 3. Effective date. This Act takes effect January 1, 2026.'
5 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
6 number to read consecutively.
7 SUMMARY
88 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment modifies a provision of law
99 governing moose hunting permits by removing language that provides that a disabled
1100 veteran who has obtained a moose hunting permit through transfer, as well as a person who
1111 has transferred a moose hunting permit to a disabled veteran, is ineligible to obtain another
1122 permit until the 4th calendar year after the issuance of the last permit and clarifying that
1133 this ineligibility period does not apply to those transferees and transferors. The amendment
1144 also adds an effective date of January 1, 2026.
