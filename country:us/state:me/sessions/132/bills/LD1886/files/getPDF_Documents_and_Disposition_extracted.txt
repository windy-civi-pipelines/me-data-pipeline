132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1886
H.P. 1257 House of Representatives, May 5, 2025
Distracted Driving When the Violation Results in the Death or
Significant Injury of Another Person
Received by the Clerk of the House on May 1, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Criminal Justice and Public Safety pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to
Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative LEMELIN of Chelsea.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 29-A MRSA ยง2118, sub-ยง3 is enacted to read:
3 3. Penalties. The following penalties apply to a violation of this section.
4 A. If a person violates this section and the State proves that the person's violation of
5 this section caused significant injury to another person, the violator's license may be
6 suspended for a period of 180 days. The court may order a suspension for a period that
7 is less than 180 days if the court finds that a shorter period of suspension is appropriate
8 due to extenuating circumstances.
9 B. If a person violates this section and the State proves that the person's violation of
10 this section caused the death of another person, the violator's license may be suspended
11 for a period of one to 3 years. The court may order a suspension for a period that is
12 less than one year if the court finds that a shorter period of suspension is appropriate
13 due to extenuating circumstances.
14 The court shall apply Title 17-A, section 33 in assessing causation under this subsection.
15 SUMMARY
1166 This bill amends provisions of law relating to distracted driving by establishing specific
1177 penalties for committing the traffic infraction of failure to maintain control of a motor
1188 vehicle. If the State proves that a driver's commission of the traffic infraction caused a
1199 significant injury to another person, the court may order a suspension of the driver's license
2200 for a period of 180 days. If the State proves that a driver's commission of the traffic
2211 infraction caused the death of another person, the court may order a suspension of the
2222 driver's license for a period of one to 3 years. In either case, the court may order a
2233 suspension for a shorter period if the court finds that a shorter suspension is appropriate
2244 due to extenuating circumstances.
