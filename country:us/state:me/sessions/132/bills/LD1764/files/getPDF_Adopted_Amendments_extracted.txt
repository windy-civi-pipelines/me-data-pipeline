9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1183, L.D. 1764, “An Act to Ensure
10 Equitable Staffing Ratios Across the State for Career and Technical Education Programs
11 in Areas with Smaller Student Populations”
12 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
13 following:
14 'Sec. 1. 20-A MRSA §15688-A, sub-§1, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 420, §8,
15 is amended by amending subparagraph (2) to read:
16 (2) Student-to-teacher ratios for each program. Ratios developed pursuant to this
17 subparagraph must provide for at least one full-time equivalent teacher allocation
18 for a program with a minimum of 4 students for a career and technical education
19 center or career and technical education region with fewer than 1,000 students
20 enrolled within the center's or region's geographic boundaries as described in
21 sections 8401 and 8451, respectively;
22 Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
23 allocations are made.
24 EDUCATION, DEPARTMENT OF
25 General Purpose Aid for Local Schools 0308
26 Initiative: Provides ongoing funds for the cost of lowering the student-to-teacher ratio for
27 programs at career and technical education centers or regions with fewer than 1,000
28 students enrolled within the center's or region's geographic boundaries from one full-time
29 equivalent teacher allocation for programs with a minimum of 8 students to one full-time
30 equivalent teacher allocation for programs with a minimum of 4 students.

3 SUMMARY
44 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment requires that student-to-teacher
55 ratios for a career and technical education center or region with fewer than 1,000 students
66 enrolled within the center's or region's geographic boundaries must provide for at least one
77 full-time equivalent teacher allocation for programs with a minimum of 4 students. The
88 amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section.
