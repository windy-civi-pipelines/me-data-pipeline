1 L.D. 872
2 Date: (Filing No. H- )
3 STATE AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 558, L.D. 872, “An Act to Ensure
10 Determinations Made by the State Are Free from Unethical, Unsafe or Illegal Interference
11 by Artificial Intelligence”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, Directing the Office of Information Technology to Study the Use of Artificial
14 Intelligence and Access to Ethical, Legal, Safe and Affordable Forms of Artificial
15 Intelligence'
16 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
17 'Sec. 1. Office to study use of artificial intelligence. Resolved: That the
18 Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Office of Information Technology
19 shall, in collaboration with the Federal Government, other states, municipalities, other
20 national governments as appropriate and other public and private organizations, study the
21 use of artificial intelligence and access to ethical, legal, safe and affordable forms of
22 artificial intelligence to allow the State and residents of the State to:
23 1. Effectively fulfill governmental and civic obligations;
24 2. Guarantee rights under the United States Constitution and the Constitution of Maine
25 and state and federal laws, rules and regulations; and
26 3. Advance economic, cultural and environmental well-being.
27 The Chief Information Officer shall establish ethical, legal and safety policy standards
28 pursuant to this section.
29 Sec. 2. Report to Legislature. Resolved: That, by December 3, 2025, the
30 Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Office of Information Technology
31 shall submit a report of the findings of the study under section 1, including any suggested
32 legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on State and Local Government. The
33 committee is authorized to report out a bill based upon the report to the Second Regular
34 Session of the 132nd Legislature.'

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 558, L.D. 872
1 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
2 number to read consecutively.
3 SUMMARY
44 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill with
55 a resolve. It directs the Office of Information Technology within the Department of
66 Administrative and Financial Services to study, in collaboration with the Federal
77 Government, other states, municipalities, other national governments as appropriate and
88 other public and private organizations, the use of artificial intelligence and access to ethical,
99 legal, safe and affordable forms of artificial intelligence and directs the Chief Information
1100 Officer to establish ethical, legal and safety policy standards. It also requires that, by
1111 December 3, 2025, the Office of Information Technology must submit a report to the Joint
1122 Standing Committee on State and Local Government, which is authorized to report out a
1133 bill based upon the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
