9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 454, L.D. 1069, “An Act to Require
10 Personal Finance to Be Taught as a Separate Course in Order for a Student to Obtain a
11 High School Diploma”
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
13 'Resolve, Directing the Department of Education to Review Personal Finance Course
14 Offerings in Maine Schools and Implement a Teacher Training Program'
15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
16 'Sec. 1. Personal finance; school survey. Resolved: That the Department of
17 Education shall administer a survey to all school administrative units in the State to collect
18 data and information on the status of personal finance education in schools. The survey
19 must include, but is not limited to:
20 1. Whether personal finance is taught as a discrete course or the personal finance
21 standards contained in the statewide system of learning results, as established in the Maine
22 Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 6209, are integrated into other social studies courses;
23 2. If personal finance is not taught as a discrete course, the name and curriculum of the
24 courses into which personal finance learning standards are integrated;
25 3. To which grade levels discrete personal finance courses are offered; and
26 4. The number of students enrolled in discrete personal finance courses and the number
27 of instructional hours those students receive in personal finance.
28 The Department of Education shall submit a report based on the results of the survey
29 to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs no later than January
30 15, 2026.
31 Sec. 2. Working group. Resolved: That, beginning in January 2026, the
32 Department of Education shall convene a working group of relevant stakeholders to
33 develop recommendations on increasing accessibility to personal finance education to
34 students in the State of all grade levels based on the findings of the report developed

326 curriculum resources, instructional time, staffing and any other supports the working group

359 to:
6 1. One member representing the Finance Authority of Maine;
7 2. One member representing the Maine Jump$tart Coalition for Personal Financial
8 Literacy;
9 3. At least 3 teachers employed in school administrative units in the State who teach
10 personal finance subject matter; and
11 4. One member representing the Maine Education Association.
12 The Department of Education shall submit a report based on the findings and
13 recommendations of the working group to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and
14 Cultural Affairs no later than March 1, 2026.
15 Sec. 3. Teacher training. Resolved: That, based on the findings of the reports
16 submitted pursuant to sections 1 and 2, the Department of Education shall organize a
17 summer teacher training opportunity for teachers in the State who teach or who wish to
18 teach personal finance with the intent of allowing teachers to share best practices, learn
19 from expert practitioners and network with one another. The teacher training opportunity
20 must take place during the summer months.

8 SUMMARY
99 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve. The amendment directs the
1100 Department of Education to survey school administrative units to collect data on the status
1111 of personal finance course offerings and integration into social studies curricula. The
1122 amendment also directs the department to convene a working group to develop
1133 recommendations on increasing accessibility to personal finance education for students in
1144 the State and directs the department to develop a summer teacher training opportunity
1155 related to personal finance instruction.
