7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 499, L.D. 1210, “An Act to Regarding
10 Renewable Electricity Generation by Hydropower Projects”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Provide for Evaluation of the Environmental, Energy and Public Benefits
13 of Hydropower Projects upon Relicensing or License Surrender'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
15 following:
16 'Sec. 1. 38 MRSA §635-C is enacted to read:
17 §635-C. Procedures regarding licensed hydropower projects; environmental, energy
18 and public benefits considerations
19 For a licensed hydropower project seeking relicensing or license surrender with the
20 Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, the department, based on information provided
21 by the owner or operator of the project and any other available information, shall review
22 the project to determine the environmental, energy and public benefits of the relicensing or
23 continued operation of the project based on the following considerations:
24 1. Environmental and energy benefits. Whether the relicensing or continued
25 operation of the hydropower project will result in significant environmental and energy
26 benefits to the public, including, but not limited to, public rights of access to and use of the
27 surface waters of the State for navigation, fishing, fowling, recreation and other lawful
28 public uses; flood control benefits or flood hazards; and hydroelectric energy benefits,
29 including the maintenance of or increase in generating capacity and annual energy output
30 resulting from the project; and
31 2. Public benefits. Whether the relicensing or continued operation of the hydropower
32 project will result in significant economic benefits to the public, including, but not limited
33 to, maintenance or creation of employment opportunities for residents of the State.
34 The department shall issue a written finding of fact with respect to the nature and
35 magnitude of the benefits of the relicensing or continued operation of a hydropower project

316 that addresses the considerations described in subsections 1 and 2 and shall provide a copy
327 of the written finding of fact to the owner or operator of the project and to the Federal
338 Energy Regulatory Commission.'
4 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
5 number to read consecutively.
6 SUMMARY
77 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
88 changes the title. It requires the Department of Environmental Protection, for a licensed
99 hydropower project seeking relicensing or license surrender with the Federal Energy
1100 Regulatory Commission, to review the project to determine the environmental, energy and
1111 public benefits of the relicensing or continued operation of the project based on certain
1122 considerations. After conducting the review, the department must issue a written finding
1133 of fact with respect to the nature and magnitude of the benefits of the relicensing or
1144 continued operation of the hydropower project addressing those environmental, energy and
1155 public benefits considerations and provide a copy of the written finding of fact to the owner
1166 or operator of the project and to the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission.
