9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1020, L.D. 1562, “An Act Regarding
10 Municipal Road Standards”
11 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following:
12 'Sec. 1. 23 MRSA §3651, as amended by PL 2019, c. 128, §1, is further amended
13 to read:
14 §3651. Failure to provide safety and convenience
15 Highways, town ways and streets legally established shall must be opened and kept in
16 repair so as to be safe and convenient for travelers with motor vehicles based on existing
17 roadway surface and the season of the year. A municipal officer or county commissioner
18 responsible for maintenance and repair of a town way must be notified by a resident in
19 writing of which way is not safe and convenient. Once notified, the municipal officer or
20 county commissioner may evaluate the risk to travelers based on existing roadway surface
21 and the season of the year and recommend a reasonably achievable repair. In default
22 thereof, those liable may be indicted, convicted and a reasonable fine imposed therefor If
23 a municipal officer or county commissioner unreasonably neglects to repair the defective
24 town way in a reasonable amount of time after receiving notice, a person who receives
25 bodily injury or suffers damage to the person's property through any defect or want of repair
26 on any town way may recover for the same in a civil action pursuant to section 3655.
27 1. Legal objects not defects. Trees, structures, utility poles and facilities and other
28 things that exist in accordance with municipal ordinances are not defects in a public way.
29 For the purposes of this subsection, "facilities" has the same meaning as in Title 35‑A,
30 section 2502, subsection 3.'
31 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 2 and inserting the following:
32 'Sec. 2. 23 MRSA §3652 is repealed and the following enacted in its place:
33 §3652. Notice of defect; optional local hearing on petition

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1020, L.D. 1562
1 1. Municipal board of appeals; authority to hear petition. The legislative body of
2 a municipality may appoint the municipal board of appeals established pursuant to Title
3 30-A, section 2691 to receive a petition of a defective town way to review.
4 2. Persons petitioning defective road. When a town liable to maintain a town way
5 unreasonably neglects to keep it in repair as provided in section 3651 after one of the
6 municipal officers has had 5 days' actual notice or knowledge of the defective condition,
7 any 3 or more responsible persons, setting forth those facts, may petition the municipal
8 board of appeals that has been granted authority by the legislative body pursuant to
9 subsection 1. The municipal board of appeals shall fix a time and place near the defective
10 town way for a hearing on the petition and cause such notice of the hearing to be given to
11 the town and petitioners as the board of appeals may prescribe. At the time appointed, the
12 board of appeals shall inspect the town way that is alleged to be out of repair and hear the
13 parties interested, and if the board of appeals adjudges the town way to be unsafe and
14 inconvenient for travelers, motor vehicles, horses, teams and carriages, the board of appeals
15 shall prescribe what repairs must be made, fix the time in which the town must undertake
16 the repairs and give notice of the repairs to the municipal officers. If the board of appeals
17 adjudges the town way to be safe and convenient based on existing roadway surface and
18 the season of the year, the board of appeals shall dismiss the petition.'
19 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
20 number to read consecutively.
21 SUMMARY
2222 This amendment does the following.
23 1. It requires a municipal officer or county commissioner responsible for maintenance
24 and repair of a town way to be notified by a resident in writing of a defect in the town way
25 that is not safe and convenient. The amendment specifies that safety is determined based
26 on existing roadway surface and the season of the year.
27 2. It allows the municipal officer or county commissioner, once notified, to evaluate
28 the risk based on existing roadway surface and the season of the year and recommend a
29 reasonably achievable repair.
30 3. It allows a person who receives bodily injury or suffers damage to the person's
31 property through any defect or want of repair on any town way to recover for the same in
32 a civil action.
33 4. It allows the legislative body of a municipality to appoint the municipal board of
34 appeals to receive a petition of a road defect to review.
35 5. It requires the municipal board of appeals to set a time and place near the defective
36 town way for a hearing on the petition. If the board of appeals adjudges the town way to be
37 safe and convenient based on existing roadway surface and the season of the year, the board
38 of appeals must dismiss the petition.
