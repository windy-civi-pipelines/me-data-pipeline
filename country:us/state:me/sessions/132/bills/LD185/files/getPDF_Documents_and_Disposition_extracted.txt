Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 185
An Act to Expand Opportunities to Invest Municipal Tax Increment
Financing Revenues
Reference to the Committee on Taxation suggested and ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative MASTRACCIO of Sanford.
Cosponsored by Senator HARRINGTON of York and
Representatives: ANKELES of Brunswick, BLIER of Buxton, BRIDGEO of Augusta,
FREDERICKS of Sanford, GATTINE of Westbrook, ROLLINS of Augusta, SALISBURY of
Westbrook, Senator: NANGLE of Cumberland.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 30-A MRSA §5225, sub-§1, ¶C, as amended by PL 2023, c. 142, §§1 to
3 3, is further amended by amending subparagraph (9) to read:
4 (9) Costs related to the construction, renovation or operation of municipal or
5 plantation public safety facilities, including facilities used as emergency shelters,
6 the need for which is related to general economic development within the
7 municipality or plantation, not to exceed 15% of the captured assessed value of the
8 development district;
9 Sec. 2. 30-A MRSA §5225, sub-§1, ¶C, as amended by PL 2023, c. 142, §§1 to
10 3, is further amended by amending subparagraph (12) to read:
11 (12) Up to 50% of the capital costs Costs related to the construction or renovation
12 of a municipality's or plantation's central administrative office facilities owned by
13 a municipality or plantation and used to provide municipal or plantation services,
14 the need for which is related to general economic development within the
15 municipality or plantation, not to exceed 15% of the captured assessed value of the
16 development district;
17 SUMMARY
1188 This bill maximizes the ability of a municipality or plantation to utilize tax increment
1199 financing revenue to renovate and construct municipal facilities that contribute to the
2200 community's economic development efforts, including facilities used as emergency
2211 shelters.
