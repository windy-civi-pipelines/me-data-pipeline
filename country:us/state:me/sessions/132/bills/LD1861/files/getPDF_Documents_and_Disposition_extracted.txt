Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1861
S.P. 731 In Senate, May 5, 2025
Barber Hair Stylists, Cosmetologists and Hair Designers
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on May 1, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Labor pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator TALBOT ROSS of Cumberland.
Cosponsored by Representative DHALAC of South Portland and
Senators: BAILEY of York, HAGGAN of Penobscot, HICKMAN of Kennebec,
Representatives: BABIN of Fort Fairfield, HENDERSON of Rumford, MATHIESON of
Kittery, RANA of Bangor, SATO of Gorham.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §14212-A, sub-§2, ¶B-1 is enacted to read:
3 B-1. Requirements that educational programs for aestheticians, barber hair stylists,
4 cosmetologists and hair designers include training on the care, styling and treatment of
5 textured hair. For the purposes of this paragraph, "textured hair" means hair that is
6 coiled, curly or wavy. At a minimum, the rules must require those educational
7 programs to provide training in:
8 (1) Techniques for the cutting and styling of and using chemical treatments for
9 textured hair;
10 (2) The use of products and tools specifically designed for textured hair;
11 (3) Best practices for hair health and scalp care for individuals with textured hair;
12 and
13 (4) The cultural and historical significance of textured hair in diverse communities;
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill requires the Director of the Office of Professional and Occupational
1166 Regulation within the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation to adopt rules
1177 that require educational programs for aestheticians, barber hair stylists, cosmetologists and
1188 hair designers to include training on the care, styling and treatment of coiled, curly or wavy
1199 hair, which is defined in the bill as "textured hair."
