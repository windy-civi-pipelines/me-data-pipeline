9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 485, L.D. 743, “An Act to Increase the
10 Availability and Affordability of Health Care by Eliminating Certificate of Need
11 Requirements”
12 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 11 and inserting the following:
13 'Sec. 11. 24-A MRSA §4303-F, sub-§1, ¶E, as amended by PL 2025, c. 34, §1
14 and affected by §2, is further amended to read:
15 E. A carrier may not require a ground ambulance service provider to obtain prior
16 authorization before transporting an enrollee to a hospital, between hospitals or from a
17 hospital to a nursing home, hospice care facility or other health care facility, as defined
18 in Title 22, section 328, subsection 8. A carrier may not require an air ambulance
19 service provider that is a nonprofit organization to obtain prior authorization before
20 transporting an enrollee to a hospital or between hospitals for urgent care. For the
21 purposes of this paragraph, the following terms have the following meanings.
22 (1) "Ambulatory surgical facility" means a facility, not part of a hospital, that
23 provides surgical treatment to patients not requiring hospitalization. "Ambulatory
24 surgical facility" does not include the offices of private physicians or dentists,
25 whether in individual or group practice.
26 (2) "Health care facility" means a hospital, psychiatric hospital, nursing facility,
27 kidney disease treatment center including a freestanding hemodialysis facility,
28 rehabilitation facility, ambulatory surgical facility, independent radiological
29 service center, independent cardiac catheterization center or cancer treatment
30 center. "Health care facility" does not include the office of a private health care
31 practitioner, as defined in Title 24, section 2502, subsection 1‑A, whether in
32 individual or group practice. In an ambulatory surgical facility that functions also
33 as the office of a health care practitioner, the following portions of the ambulatory
34 surgical facility are considered to be a health care facility:
35 (a) Operating rooms;
36 (b) Recovery rooms;

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 485, L.D. 743
1 (c) Waiting areas for ambulatory surgical facility patients;
2 (d) Any space with major medical equipment; and
3 (e) Any other space used primarily to support the activities of the ambulatory
4 surgical facility.
5 (3) "Health services" means clinically related services that are diagnostic,
6 treatment, rehabilitative services or nursing services provided by a nursing facility.
7 "Health services" includes alcohol or drug dependence, substance use disorder and
8 mental health services.
9 (4) "Hospital" means an institution that primarily provides to inpatients, by or
10 under the supervision of physicians, diagnostic services and therapeutic services
11 for medical diagnosis, treatment and care of persons who are injured, disabled or
12 sick or rehabilitation services for the rehabilitation of persons who are injured,
13 disabled or sick. "Hospital" also includes psychiatric and tuberculosis hospitals.
14 (5) "Major medical equipment" means a single unit of medical equipment or a
15 single system of components with related functions used to provide medical and
16 other health services that costs $3,200,000 or more. "Major medical equipment"
17 does not include medical equipment acquired by or on behalf of a clinical
18 laboratory to provide clinical laboratory services if the clinical laboratory is
19 independent of a physician's office and a hospital and has been determined to meet
20 the requirements of the United States Social Security Act, Title XVIII, Section
21 1861(s)(10 and 11). In determining whether medical equipment costs more than
22 the threshold provided in this subparagraph, the cost of studies, surveys, designs,
23 plans, working drawings, specifications and other activities essential to acquiring
24 the equipment must be included. If the equipment is acquired for less than fair
25 market value, the cost includes the fair market value. The threshold amount for
26 review must be updated by the commissioner to reflect the change in the Consumer
27 Price Index for medical care services as reported by the United States Department
28 of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics, with an effective date of January 1st each
29 year.
30 (6) "Nursing facility" means any facility defined under section 1812‑A.
31 (7) "Rehabilitation facility" means an inpatient facility that is operated for the
32 primary purpose of assisting in the rehabilitation of persons who are disabled
33 through an integrated program of medical services and other services that are
34 provided under competent professional supervision.'
