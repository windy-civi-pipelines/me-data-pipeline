Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 76
H.P. 40 House of Representatives, January 8, 2025
Compensate Hatchery Staff When Certain Operational Needs
Require Overtime
Submitted by the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife pursuant to Joint Rule 204.
Received by the Clerk of the House on January 6, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Inland Fisheries and Wildlife pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint
Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative THORNE of Carmel.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §10252, as affected by PL 2003, c. 614, §9 and amended by c.
3 655, Pt. B, §51 and affected by §422, is further amended to read:
4 §10252. Fish hatchery maintenance fund
5 The fish hatchery maintenance fund, referred to in this section as "the "fund," is
6 established in the department as a nonlapsing fund to be used by the commissioner to fund
7 or assist in funding engineering designs for the Embden Hatchery,; a statewide assessment
8 of all other hatchery facilities and; maintenance, repair and capital improvements at fish
9 hatcheries and feeding stations owned by the State; and overtime personnel services costs
10 when overtime for fish stocking and other hatchery-related work is operationally required.
11 The fund may not be used to fund personnel services costs or the general operating costs
12 of a fish hatchery or to fund personnel services costs other than those allowed pursuant to
13 this section. The commissioner may accept and deposit into the fund any monetary gifts,
14 donations or other contributions from public or private sources and must use that money
15 for the purposes specified in this section.
16 SUMMARY
1177 This bill expands the use of the fish hatchery maintenance fund to allow the funding of
1188 overtime personnel services costs when overtime for fish stocking and other hatchery-
1199 related work is operationally required.
