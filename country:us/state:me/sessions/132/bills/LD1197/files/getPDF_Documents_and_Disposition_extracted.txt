Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1197
S.P. 486 In Senate, March 20, 2025
Act by Removing Provisions of Law Regarding Unhosted Wallets
(EMERGENCY)
Reference to the Committee on Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services
suggested and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BALDACCI of Penobscot.
Cosponsored by Representative BOYER of Poland and
Representative: MOONEN of Portland.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, certain licensees under the Maine Money Transmission Modernization Act
4 are subject to the requirements for unhosted wallets upon renewal of their licenses
5 beginning January 1, 2025; and
6 Whereas, the lack of clarity regarding the regulation of unhosted wallets under the
7 Maine Money Transmission Modernization Act threatens marketplace stability for Maine's
8 digital asset businesses and consumers; and
9 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
10 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
11 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
12 therefore,
13 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
14 Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §6100-OO, sub-§8, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 662, §2, is
15 repealed.
16 Sec. 2. 32 MRSA §6100-OO, sub-§14, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 662, §2, is
17 repealed.
18 Sec. 3. 32 MRSA §6100-UU, as enacted by PL 2023, c. 662, §2, is repealed.
19 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
20 takes effect when approved.
21 SUMMARY
2222 This bill repeals provisions of law containing requirements for unhosted wallets in the
2233 Maine Money Transmission Modernization Act.
