11 Reestablish the Gagetown Harmful Chemical Study Commission”
12 Amend the bill in section 2 in subsection 5 in the first line (page 3, line 39 in L.D.) by
13 striking out the following: "shall" and inserting the following: 'may'
14 Amend the bill in section 2 in subsection 5 in the last 3 lines (page 3, lines 41 to 43 in
15 L.D.) by striking out the following: "The study commission shall provide medical testing
16 to individuals identified through information received pursuant to this subsection related to
17 the effects of exposure to harmful chemicals for purposes of completing the study."
18 Amend the bill by inserting after section 2 the following:
19 'Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
20 allocations are made.
21 DEFENSE, VETERANS AND EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT, DEPARTMENT
22 OF
23 Veterans Services 0110
24 Initiative: Provides one-time funding for consulting costs to create the Base Gagetown
25 Training Registry, which will collect and maintain data related to military service and
26 health conditions from individuals who report this data and trained at the Canadian military
27 support base in Gagetown, New Brunswick, Canada. The fund does not lapse but must be
28 carried forward to be used for the purpose for which the fund was appropriated.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 648, L.D. 1640
1 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
2 number to read consecutively.
3 SUMMARY
44 This amendment provides that the Gagetown Harmful Chemical Study Commission
55 reestablished in the bill may, rather than must, request information it determines necessary
66 to complete the study of the effects of exposure to harmful chemicals on individuals who
77 trained at the Canadian military support base in Gagetown, New Brunswick, Canada as a
88 member of the United States Armed Forces, as a member of the reserve component of the
99 United States Armed Forces or as a member of the National Guard of any state from the
1100 United States Department of Defense and other federal agencies. The amendment removes
1111 the provision in the bill directing the study commission to provide medical testing to
1122 identify effects of exposure to harmful chemicals to individuals identified through that
1133 information. The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section.
1144 FISCAL NOTE REQUIRED
1155 (See attached)
