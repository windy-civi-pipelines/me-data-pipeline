Section 1:
1 L.D. 238
2 Date: (Filing No. S- )
4 Reproduced and distributed under the direction of the Secretary of the Senate.
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 104, L.D. 238, “An Act to Protect
10 Emergency Medical Services Persons' Right to Work in Multiple Health Care Settings”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
12 following:
13 'Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §85, sub-§7, as amended by PL 2023, c. 132, §1, is repealed and
14 the following enacted in its place:
15 7. Delegation. A person acting under delegated authority pursuant to chapter 36 or 48
16 may not use or be governed by a license issued pursuant to this chapter while acting under
17 that delegated authority, and a person acting under a license issued pursuant to this chapter
18 may not be governed by delegated authority or engage in any activities delegated pursuant
19 to chapter 36 or 48 while acting under a license issued pursuant to this chapter.'
20 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
21 number to read consecutively.
22 SUMMARY
2233 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment clarifies the authority of a person
2244 licensed as an emergency medical services person to engage in activities delegated pursuant
2255 to existing law by a physician or physician assistant.
