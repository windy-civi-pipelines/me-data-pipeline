9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 415, L.D. 647, “An Act Regarding
10 Telephone and Video Call Access in Detention and Correctional Facilities and Jails”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'An Act to Expand County Jail and Correctional Facility Services to Indigent
13 Residents'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the
15 following:
16 'Sec. 1. 30-A MRSA §1561, sub-§1, ¶H, as enacted by PL 1995, c. 201, §1, is
17 amended to read:
18 H. Has less than $15 $100 in the prisoner's facility account and did not receive
19 additional money from any source for 6 months following the medical or dental service
20 or provision of the prescription, medication or prosthetic device.
21 Sec. 2. 34-A MRSA §3015, sub-§2, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 615, Pt. A, §1,
22 is amended to read:
23 A. The department shall provide a resident with a reasonable opportunity to make
24 telephone calls to relatives and friends, except that the department may restrict or
25 prohibit telephone calls when the restriction or prohibition is necessary for the security
26 of the department facility. The department shall provide to a resident who has less than
27 $10 $100 in the resident's facility account a free telephone call allowance for 30
28 minutes of telephone calls per week under this paragraph.
29 Sec. 3. 34-A MRSA §3015, sub-§2, ¶B, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 615, Pt. A, §1,
30 is amended to read:
31 B. The department shall provide a resident with a reasonable opportunity to make
32 telephone calls protected by the attorney-client privilege. The department shall provide
33 to a resident who has less than $10 $100 in the resident's facility account a free
34 telephone call allowance for 30 minutes of telephone calls per week under this
35 paragraph.

1 Sec. 4. 34-A MRSA §3031, sub-§2, ¶A, as amended by PL 2015, c. 291, §5, is
2 further amended by amending subparagraph (8) to read:
3 (8) Has less than $15 $100 in the client's facility account and did not receive
4 additional money from any source for 6 months following the medical or dental
5 service or provision of the prescription, medication or prosthetic device.
6 Sec. 5. 34-A MRSA §3039, sub-§7 is enacted to read:
7 7. Facility services charges. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a
8 correctional facility or detention facility may not charge a client who has less than $100 in
9 the client's facility account for any facility services. A correctional facility or detention
10 facility may not prevent a client from using any facility services based solely on the client's
11 inability to pay. If a chief administrative officer, or the chief administrative officer's
12 designee, determines that a client has manipulated funds in the client's facility account for
13 the purposes of obtaining free facility services, the chief administrative officer, or the chief
14 administrative officer's designee, may charge the client for facility services, even if the
15 client would otherwise qualify for free facility services, for up to 90 days. For the purposes
16 of this subsection, "facility services" includes, but is not limited to, telephone services,
17 medical services, dental services and mailing services. This subsection may not be
18 construed to apply to commissary sales under section 3012.
19 Sec. 6. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
20 allocations are made.

32 SUMMARY
3333 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill and
3344 changes the title. The amendment reduces the financial barrier regarding when a client in a
3355 correctional or detention facility is eligible for free medical, dental and telecommunications
3366 services and reduces the financial barrier regarding when a prisoner in a county jail is
3377 eligible for free medical or dental services. The amendment also prohibits a correctional
3388 facility or detention facility from charging a client for the use of facility services if the
3399 client has less than $100 in the client's facility account or preventing the client from using
4400 the facility services based solely on the client's inability to pay. If the chief administrative
4411 officer of a correctional facility or detention facility determines a client has manipulated
4422 the client's facility account funds for the purposes of obtaining free facility services, the
