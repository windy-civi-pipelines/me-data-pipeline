Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1025
S.P. 443 In Senate, March 13, 2025
Maine Public Employees Retirement System Benefits
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on March 12, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Labor pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator HARRINGTON of York. (BY REQUEST)
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §17759-A is enacted to read:
3 §17759-A. Federal employment service credit purchase
4 1. Service before becoming member. Notwithstanding section 17759, a member
5 who served as an employee of the federal employment service before becoming a member
6 is entitled under this subsection to purchase service credit for the period of time that the
7 member served as an employee of the federal employment service by complying with the
8 applicable provisions of subsection 2. Service credit purchased under this section is limited
9 to 5 years.
10 2. Members qualified for credit at actuarial cost. A member qualified under
11 subsection 1 to purchase service credit at the cost set forth in this subsection is entitled to
12 service credit if the member pays an amount that, together with regular interest on that
13 amount, is the actuarial equivalent, at the effective date of the retirement benefit, of the
14 portion of the retirement benefit based on the additional creditable service.
15 A. Payment must be made before any retirement benefit becomes effective for that
16 member.
17 B. Payment may be made to the retirement system by a single direct payment or by
18 annual direct payments in accordance with section 17701, subsection 4.
19 SUMMARY
2200 This bill allows a member of the Maine Public Employees Retirement System who
2211 served as an employee of the federal employment service before becoming a member to
2222 purchase service credit up to 5 years for the period of time that the member served as an
2233 employee of the federal employment service.
