9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 854, L.D. 1279, “An Act to Require the
10 Department of Marine Resources to Conduct Biotoxin Testing of Cultured Scallops”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, Establishing the Biotoxin Testing of Cultured Scallops Pilot Program'
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
14 'Sec. 1. Biotoxin Testing of Cultured Scallops Pilot Program established.
15 Resolved: That the Biotoxin Testing of Cultured Scallops Pilot Program, referred to in
16 this resolve as "the program," is established until December 31, 2027. The Department of
17 Marine Resources, referred to in this resolve as "the department," shall oversee the
18 program. The purpose of the program is to conduct biotoxin testing of whole cultured
19 scallops or the roe of cultured scallops in the same manner as the testing for other cultured
20 mollusks under the Marine Shellfish Toxins Monitoring Program, established in the Maine
21 Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 6076, including using high-performance liquid
22 chromatography testing. The department shall provide logistical support to a commercial
23 fisheries business engaged in scallop aquaculture in conducting the testing under this
24 section, including providing transportation of samples to be tested from a facility in the
25 municipality in which the grower is located to the testing site. For the purposes of this
26 section, "high-performance liquid chromatography testing" means a testing technique in
27 analytical chemistry that uses high-pressure pumps to separate, identify and quantify
28 specific components in mixtures. The program may not conduct testing on cultured
29 scallops or the roe of cultured scallops grown on limited-purpose aquaculture license sites.
30 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That the department shall submit a report on each year
31 of the program, by January 1, 2027 and January 1, 2028, respectively, detailing the number
32 of commercial fisheries businesses using the testing under section 1, the overall impact on
33 the scallop aquaculture industry, including economic growth and cultured scallop landings,
34 and recommendations for further improvements or expansions of the testing under section
35 1 to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over marine

16 SUMMARY
1177 This amendment replaces the bill and changes it to a resolve. The amendment
1188 establishes the Biotoxin Testing of Cultured Scallops Pilot Program and directs the
1199 Department of Marine Resources to oversee the program. The amendment specifies that
2200 the program operates for a 2-year period with the aim of conducting biotoxin testing of
2211 whole cultured scallops or the roe of cultured scallops in the same manner as the testing for
2222 other cultured mollusks under the Marine Shellfish Toxins Monitoring Program. The
2233 amendment requires the department to submit a report to the joint standing committee of
2244 the Legislature having jurisdiction over marine resources matters on each year of the
2255 program, by January 1, 2027 and January 1, 2028, detailing the number of commercial
2266 fisheries businesses using the testing offered by the pilot program, the overall impact on
2277 the scallop aquaculture industry, including economic growth and cultured scallop landings,
2288 and recommendations for further improvements or expansions of the testing. The
2299 amendment authorizes the committee to report out a bill based on the 2028 report to the
3300 Second Regular Session of the 133rd Legislature.
