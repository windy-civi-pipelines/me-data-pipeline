Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST SPECIAL SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 1545
S.P. 629 In Senate, April 10, 2025
Under the Maine Uniform Probate Code to Permit Collection of
Personal Property by Affidavit
Reference to the Committee on Judiciary suggested and ordered printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BAILEY of York.
Cosponsored by Senators: CARNEY of Cumberland, HAGGAN of Penobscot,
Representatives: HENDERSON of Rumford, KUHN of Falmouth, LEE of Auburn, POIRIER
of Skowhegan.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 18-C MRSA §3-1201, sub-§1, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2017, c. 402, Pt. A,
3 §2 and affected by PL 2019, c. 417, Pt. B, §14, is amended to read:
4 A. The value of the entire estate, wherever located, less liens and encumbrances, does
5 not exceed $40,000, adjusted for inflation pursuant to section 1-108;
6 Sec. 2. 18-C MRSA §3-1201, sub-§3 is enacted to read:
7 3. Publication. A county probate court shall annually publish on the court's publicly
8 accessible website the value described in subsection 1, paragraph A, including the
9 adjustment for inflation.
10 SUMMARY
1111 This bill amends the provision of law within the Maine Uniform Probate Code
1122 governing collection of personal property by use of an affidavit. This provision of law
1133 applies only to estates with values that do not exceed $40,000, adjusted for inflation
1144 pursuant to cost-of-living adjustment provisions within the Maine Uniform Probate Code.
1155 The bill adds a cross-reference to clarify that the $40,000 figure is adjusted for inflation.
1166 The bill also requires county probate courts to annually publish the value of this figure
1177 adjusted for inflation on the court's publicly accessible website.
