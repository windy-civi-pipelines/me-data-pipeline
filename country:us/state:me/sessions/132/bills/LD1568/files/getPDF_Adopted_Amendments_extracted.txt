9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 1026, L.D. 1568, “An Act to Allow the
10 Use of a Siren in a Vehicle Used by a Harbor Master or Deputy Harbor Master”
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the
12 emergency clause and inserting the following:
13 'Sec. 1. 29-A MRSA §2054, sub-§1, ¶B, as amended by PL 2019, c. 319, §1 and
14 c. 397, §25, is further amended by amending subparagraph (22) to read:
15 (22) A University of Maine System vehicle operated by a University of Maine
16 System police officer; and
17 Sec. 2. 29-A MRSA §2054, sub-§1, ¶B, as amended by PL 2019, c. 319, §1 and
18 c. 397, §25, is further amended by amending subparagraph (23) to read:
19 (23) A life support transport vehicle when parked on a Department of
20 Transportation ferry vessel and being used to transport a person who requires
21 constant medical support to survive.; and
22 Sec. 3. 29-A MRSA §2054, sub-§1, ¶B, as amended by PL 2019, c. 319, §1 and
23 c. 397, §25, is further amended by enacting a new subparagraph (24) to read:
24 (24) A municipal vehicle operated by a harbor master appointed by a municipal
25 officer pursuant to Title 38, section 1 or a deputy harbor master appointed pursuant
26 to Title 38, section 2.
27 Sec. 4. 29-A MRSA §2054, sub-§2, ¶F, as amended by PL 2023, c. 131, §1, is
28 further amended by amending subparagraph (5) to read:
29 (5) A municipality may authorize a harbor master or deputy harbor master
30 appointed under Title 38, section 1 or 2, respectively, to use one red or combination
31 red and white flashing auxiliary light mounted in the windshield or on the
32 dashboard at the front of a vehicle or 2 flashing red or combination red and white
33 auxiliary lights mounted on the front of the vehicle above the front bumper and
34 below the hood and one red auxiliary light mounted in the rear window area. The

315 light or lights may be displayed but may be used only while the harbor master or
326 deputy harbor master is responding to a watercraft emergency. A light mounted

348 does not interfere with the operator's vision. The authorization for the use of lights
359 or a siren may be revoked at any time by the municipality.
6 A vehicle described in this subparagraph may be operated only when exercising
7 emergency privileges by a person who has completed an emergency vehicle
8 operation course provided by the Department of Public Safety, Bureau of State
9 Police. A municipality may authorize an authorized emergency vehicle owned by
10 a municipality and operated by a harbor master or deputy harbor master as
11 described in subsection 1, paragraph B, subparagraph (24) to be equipped with a
12 siren in addition to authorized lights pursuant to this subparagraph.'
13 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
14 number to read consecutively.
15 SUMMARY
1166 This amendment replaces the bill but retains the emergency preamble and emergency
1177 clause. The amendment adds a municipal vehicle operated by a harbor master or deputy
1188 harbor master to the list of authorized emergency vehicles. The amendment also requires a
1199 municipality to require a person appointed or reappointed as a harbor master or deputy
2200 harbor master to have completed an emergency vehicle operation course provided by the
2211 Department of Public Safety, Bureau of State Police if operating a vehicle while exercising
2222 emergency privileges.
