Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 398
An Act to Amend the Definition of "Transient Occupancy" in the
Laws Governing the Maine Land Use Planning Commission
(EMERGENCY)
Reference to the Committee on Housing and Economic Development suggested and
ordered printed.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative GUERRETTE of Caribou.
Cosponsored by Representatives: ALBERT of Madawaska, BABIN of Fort Fairfield,
COOPER of Windham, DAIGLE of Fort Kent, DRINKWATER of Milford, FREDERICKS of
Sanford, HENDERSON of Rumford, THORNE of Carmel.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3 Whereas, the Legislature finds that it is desirable to extend principles of sound
4 planning, zoning and development to the unorganized and deorganized townships of the
5 State; and
6 Whereas, the Legislature desires to encourage appropriate use of these lands by the
7 residents of Maine and visitors in pursuit of outdoor recreation activities, including, but not
8 limited to, camping; and
9 Whereas, this legislation must take effect before the expiration of the 90-day period
10 since recreational camping season begins Memorial Day weekend in the month of May;
11 and
12 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
15 therefore,
16 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
17 Sec. 1. 12 MRSA §682, sub-§18, as amended by PL 2009, c. 16, §1, is further
18 amended to read:
19 18. Transient occupancy. "Transient occupancy" means occupancy that does not
20 exceed 120 150 days in a calendar year.
21 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
22 takes effect when approved.
23 SUMMARY
2244 This bill changes the definition of "transient occupancy" in the laws governing the
2255 Maine Land Use Planning Commission to mean occupancy that does not exceed 150 days,
2266 rather than 120 days as in current law, in a calendar year.
