Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §171, sub-§3, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 356, §1, is amended to
3 read:
4 3. Covered employer. "Covered employer" means a person or entity engaged in a
5 business, industry, profession, trade or other enterprise or that otherwise hires employees
6 in the State, whether for profit or not for profit, that has not offered to its employees,
7 effective in form or operation at any time within the current calendar year or 2 preceding
8 calendar years, a specified tax-favored retirement plan. "Covered employer" does not
9 include:
10 A. The Federal Government, the State or any other state, any county or municipal
11 corporation or any of the State's or any other state's units or instrumentalities; or
12 B. An employer that has not been in business during both the current calendar year
13 and the preceding calendar year.
14 If an employer does not maintain a specified tax-favored retirement plan for a portion of a
15 calendar year ending on or after the effective date of this chapter, but does adopt such a
16 plan for the remainder of that calendar year, the employer is not a covered employer for the
17 remainder of the year.
18 Sec. 2. 5 MRSA §173, sub-§3, as repealed and replaced by PL 2023, c. 167, §6, is
19 amended to read:
20 3. Implementation. The board may implement the program in stages, which may
21 include a pilot program and phasing in the program based on the size number of employers
22 a covered employer's employees, or other factors. A covered employer on the start date of
23 the program shall offer the program to its covered employees no later than December 31,
24 2024.
25 A covered employer with fewer than 5 3 employees is not required to offer the program to
26 its covered employees but may offer the program to its employees at the option of the
27 employer and in accordance with rules established by the board. An employer that becomes
28 a covered employer after the start date of the program shall offer the program to covered
29 employees within the time frame established by the board.
30 Sec. 3. 5 MRSA §173, sub-§4, ¶A, as amended by PL 2023, c. 167, §7, is further
31 amended by amending subparagraph (1) to read:
32 (1) From Starting July 1, 2025 to June 30, 2026, in the first year of the failure to
33 enroll, the maximum penalty per covered employee is $20;
34 Sec. 4. 5 MRSA §173, sub-§4, ¶A, as amended by PL 2023, c. 167, §7, is further
35 amended by amending subparagraph (2) to read:
36 (2) From Starting July 1, 2026 to June 30, 2027, in the 2nd year of the failure to
37 enroll, the maximum penalty per covered employee is $50; and
38 Sec. 5. 5 MRSA §173, sub-§4, ¶A, as amended by PL 2023, c. 167, §7, is further
39 amended by amending subparagraph (4) to read:
40 (4) On or after July 1, 2027, in the 3rd year of the failure to enroll, the maximum
41 penalty per covered employee is $100.

1 Sec. 6. 5 MRSA §179, sub-§2, as amended by PL 2023, c. 167, §13, is further
2 amended to read:
3 2. Submission of report. Beginning February July 1, 2026 and annually thereafter,
4 the board shall submit to the Governor, the Treasurer of State and the Legislature an audited
5 financial report, prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles,
6 detailing the activities, operations, receipts and expenditures of the program and board
7 during the preceding calendar year. The report must include the number of participants, the
8 investment options and their rates of return and other information regarding the program
9 and must also include projected activities of the program for the current calendar year.
10 Sec. 7. Transfers. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the State
11 Controller shall transfer from the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund to the Maine
12 Retirement Savings Program Enterprise Fund $350,000 on or before June 30, 2026 and
13 $275,000 on or before June 30, 2027.
14 Sec. 8. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and
15 allocations are made.
16 MAINE RETIREMENT SAVINGS BOARD
17 Maine Retirement Savings Program Z326
18 Initiative: Allocates funds to the Maine Retirement Savings Program Enterprise Fund to be
19 used in accordance with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 7-A.

21 ENTERPRISE FUND
22 All Other $350,000 $275,000

20 SUMMARY
2271 This bill makes the following changes to the Maine Retirement Savings Program.
28 1. It changes the definition of "covered employer" so that it includes all employers
29 who hire employees.
30 2. It allows covered employers who may have offered a workplace retirement plan
31 within the past 2 years to participate in the program.
32 3. It allows the Maine Retirement Savings Board to phase in the program based on the
33 number of employees rather than based on the size of employers as provided in current law.
34 4. For employers who become required to participate in the program after the start date
35 of the program, it clarifies that the employer is required to participate in the program within
36 a time frame established by the board.
37 5. It clarifies that covered employers that fail to enroll are subject to an annually
38 increasing penalty.
39 6. It changes when the Maine Retirement Savings Board is required to submit an
40 audited financial report to the Governor, the Treasurer of State and the Legislature from
Page 2 - 132LR0337(01)
411 beginning February 1, 2026 and annually thereafter to beginning July 1, 2026 and annually
422 thereafter.
3 7. It allocates funds to the Maine Retirement Savings Program Enterprise Fund.
