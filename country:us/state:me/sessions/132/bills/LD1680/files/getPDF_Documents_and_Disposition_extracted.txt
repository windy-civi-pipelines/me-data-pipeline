74 Sec. 2. 23 MRSA §4408 is enacted to read:
8 §4408. Ferry Oversight Committee
9 The Ferry Oversight Committee, established in Title 5, section 12004-J, subsection 22
10 and referred to in this section as "the committee," is an independent committee and shall
11 provide continuous oversight of ferry operations and serve as a legislative mechanism for
12 ensuring accountability and transparency around key decisions that affect unbridged island
13 communities.
14 1. Membership. The committee consists of 10 members appointed as follows:
15 A. One member of the Senate who serves on the joint standing committee of the
16 Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters, appointed by the President
17 of the Senate;
18 B. One member of the House of Representatives who serves on the joint standing
19 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters, appointed
20 by the Speaker of the House;
21 C. Two members of the Maine State Ferry Advisory Board under chapter 411, each of
22 whom represents an island municipality or plantation served by the Maine State Ferry
23 Service, appointed by the chair of the Maine State Ferry Advisory Board;
24 D. Two members who are not members of the Maine State Ferry Advisory Board
25 under chapter 411, each of whom represents an island municipality or plantation served
26 by the Maine State Ferry Service, appointed by the Governor; and
27 E. Four members with expertise in relevant infrastructure, emergency services,
28 economic development and education, appointed by the Governor.
29 2. Terms of appointment; vacancies. Members of the committee are appointed for
30 2-year terms. A member may serve multiple terms. Members of the committee may serve
31 after the expiration of their terms until their successors have taken office. A member
32 appointed to fill a vacancy prior to the expiration of the term for which the member's
33 predecessor was appointed serves only for the remainder of that term and must be appointed
34 by the same appointing authority. Legislative members serve terms commensurate with
35 their terms of office.
36 3. Chair. Annually, the committee shall elect a chair from among its members.
37 4. Meetings; quorum. The committee may meet as often as necessary but must meet
38 at least quarterly. A majority of the members constitutes a quorum.
39 5. Duties. The committee shall:
Page 1 - 132LR2406(01)
1 A. Review proposals submitted by the Department of Transportation related to ferry
2 operations, including berthing changes. The committee shall submit a report of a
3 review under this paragraph to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having
4 jurisdiction over transportation matters; and
5 B. Examine the effects of changes in ferry services on student access to education,
6 emergency service response, service reliability and the economic vitality of island
7 communities and submit a report by December 31st of each year to the joint standing
8 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters with
9 findings and recommendations.
10 Sec. 3. Staggered terms. Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 23,
11 section 4408, subsection 2, of the initial non-Legislator appointments to the Ferry
12 Oversight Committee, 4 members must be appointed to one-year terms and 4 members to
13 2-year terms.
14 SUMMARY
1155 This bill establishes the Ferry Oversight Committee, which is an independent
1166 committee providing continuous oversight of ferry operations and serving as a legislative
1177 mechanism for ensuring accountability and transparency around key decisions that affect
1188 unbridged island communities.
