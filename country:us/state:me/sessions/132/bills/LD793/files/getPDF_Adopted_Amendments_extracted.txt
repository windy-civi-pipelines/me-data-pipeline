1 L.D. 793
2 Date: (Filing No. S- )
3 VETERANS AND LEGAL AFFAIRS
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 358, L.D. 793, “An Act to Modernize
10 Gaming Revenue Allocations and Increase Efforts to Prevent Gambling Addiction”
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12 'Resolve, to Establish the Working Group to Review and Update the Laws Governing
13 Harness Racing'
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
15 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
16 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
17 Whereas, the effective date for acts and resolves of the Legislature is likely to be in
18 early October; and
19 Whereas, the report required by this legislation must be submitted by December 3,
20 2025; and
21 Whereas, the working group established by this legislation needs sufficient time in
22 which to complete its work; and
23 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
24 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
25 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
26 therefore, be it
27 Sec. 1. Working group established. Resolved: That the Working Group to
28 Review and Update the Laws Governing Harness Racing, referred to in this resolve as "the
29 working group," is established.
30 Sec. 2. Working group membership. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint
31 Rule 353, the working group consists of 12 members appointed as follows:
Page 1 - 132LR1395(02)
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 358, L.D. 793
1 1. Two members of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate, including a
2 member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature,
3 one of whom serves on the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs;
4 2. Two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the
5 House, including at least one member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number
6 of seats in the Legislature, one of whom serves on the Joint Standing Committee on
7 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry;
8 3. One member of the State Harness Racing Commission, appointed by the President
9 of the Senate;
10 4. One member representing a statewide association of horse owners, trainers and
11 drivers, appointed by the President of the Senate;
12 5. One member representing a statewide organization of Standardbred breeders,
13 appointed by the President of the Senate;
14 6. One member representing licensed commercial tracks, appointed by the President
15 of the Senate;
16 7. One member representing licensed off-track betting facilities, appointed by the
17 Speaker of the House;
18 8. One member representing agricultural fairs that conduct pari-mutuel wagering,
19 appointed by the Speaker of the House;
20 9. One member who is a veteran of the United States Armed Forces, appointed by the
21 Speaker of the House; and
22 10. One member of the public with experience in hospitality, tourism or advertising,
23 appointed by the Speaker of the House.
24 Sec. 3. Chairs. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the Senate
25 member who serves on the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs is the
26 Senate chair and the House of Representatives member who serves on the Joint Standing
27 Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry is the House chair of the working
28 group.
29 Sec. 4. Appointments; convening of working group. Resolved: That all
30 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this
31 resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative
32 Council once all appointments have been completed. After appointment of all members,
33 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the working group. If 30 days or more
34 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been
35 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for
36 the working group to meet and conduct its business.
37 Sec. 5. Duties. Resolved: That the working group shall review the Maine Revised
38 Statutes, Title 8, chapter 11 and prepare a partial or complete revision of that chapter and
39 any other sections of law reasonably related to harness racing in the State as may be
40 necessary to modernize and sustain the sport of harness racing and to provide support for
41 gambling addiction. The working group shall evaluate, but is not limited to evaluating, the
42 following:
Page 2 - 132LR1395(02)
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 358, L.D. 793
1 1. The availability of suitable facilities to conduct harness racing and how to encourage
2 development or preservation of those facilities;
3 2. Strategies to create a predictable annual schedule of races while maintaining the
4 quality of racing and promoting the health and safety of animal and human participants in
5 the sport;
6 3. Streamlining rules governing the licensing process and reducing complexity of those
7 rules;
8 4. The economic impact of the sport and the allocation of existing resources; and
9 5. Successful models for the promotion or sustainment of racing as may exist in other
10 jurisdictions.
11 Sec. 6. Staff assistance. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the
12 Legislative Council shall provide necessary staffing services to the working group, except
13 that Legislative Council staff support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular
14 or special session.
15 Sec. 7. Report. Resolved: That, no later than December 3, 2025, the working
16 group shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including
17 suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs and
18 the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry. The Joint
19 Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs and the Joint Standing Committee on
20 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry may each report out legislation related to the report
21 to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
22 Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
23 takes effect when approved.'
24 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
25 number to read consecutively.
26 SUMMARY
2277 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft, with a resolve. The
2288 amendment establishes a working group to review the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 8,
2299 chapter 11, which governs harness racing, and prepare a partial or complete revision of that
3300 chapter and any other sections of law reasonably related to harness racing in the State as
3311 may be necessary to modernize and sustain the sport of harness racing and to provide
3322 support for gambling addiction. The working group is required to submit a report, including
3333 its findings and recommendations, and any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing
3344 Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs and the Joint Standing Committee on
3355 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, both of which are authorized to report out
3366 legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
3377 The amendment includes an emergency preamble and emergency clause.
