8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 449, L.D. 710, “An Act to Expand
10 Access and Reduce Barriers to Access to Naloxone Hydrochloride and Other Opioid
11 Overdose-reversing Medications”
12 Amend the bill by inserting after the enacting clause the following:
13 'Sec. 1. 22 MRSA §2353, sub-§2, as amended by PL 2023, c. 161, §3, is further
14 amended to read:
15 2. Prescription; possession; administration. The prescription, possession and
16 administration of naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-reversing medication
17 is governed by this subsection.
18 A. A health care professional may directly or by standing order prescribe naloxone
19 hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-reversing medication to an individual at risk
20 of experiencing an opioid-related drug overdose. Over-the-counter naloxone
21 hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-reversing medication approved for over-the-
22 counter dispensation by the federal Food and Drug Administration or its successor
23 organization may be provided or administered with or without a prescription.
24 A-1. A pharmacist may prescribe and dispense naloxone hydrochloride or another
25 opioid overdose-reversing medication in accordance with protocols established under
26 Title 32, section 13815 to an individual of any age at risk of experiencing an opioid-
27 related drug overdose. Over-the-counter naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid
28 overdose-reversing medication approved for over-the-counter dispensation by the
29 federal Food and Drug Administration or its successor organization may be provided
30 or administered with or without a prescription.
31 B. An individual to whom naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-
32 reversing medication is prescribed or dispensed in accordance with paragraph A or A‑1
33 may provide the naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-reversing
34 medication so prescribed or dispensed to a member of that individual's immediate
35 family to possess and administer to the individual if the family member believes in
36 good faith that the individual is experiencing an opioid-related drug overdose.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 449, L.D. 710
1 C. A health care professional may directly or by standing order prescribe naloxone
2 hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-reversing medication to a member of an
3 individual's immediate family or a friend of the individual or to another person in a
4 position to assist the individual if the individual is at risk of experiencing an opioid-
5 related drug overdose. Over-the-counter naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid
6 overdose-reversing medication approved for over-the-counter dispensation by the
7 federal Food and Drug Administration or its successor organization may be provided
8 or administered with or without a prescription.
9 C-1. A pharmacist may prescribe and dispense naloxone hydrochloride or another
10 opioid overdose-reversing medication in accordance with protocols established under
11 Title 32, section 13815 to a person of any age who is a member of an individual's
12 immediate family or a friend of the individual or to another person in a position to assist
13 the individual if the individual is at risk of experiencing an opioid-related drug
14 overdose. Over-the-counter naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-
15 reversing medication approved for over-the-counter dispensation by the federal Food
16 and Drug Administration or its successor organization may be provided or
17 administered with or without a prescription.
18 D. If a member of an individual's immediate family, friend of the individual or other
19 person is prescribed or provided naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-
20 reversing medication in accordance with paragraph C or C‑1, that family member,
21 friend or other person may administer the naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid
22 overdose-reversing medication to the individual if the family member, friend or other
23 person believes in good faith that the individual is experiencing an opioid-related drug
24 overdose.
25 Nothing in this subsection affects the provisions of law relating to maintaining the
26 confidentiality of medical records.'
27 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
28 number to read consecutively.
29 SUMMARY
3300 This amendment adds language to the bill that clarifies the authority of health care
3311 professionals to administer naloxone hydrochloride or another opioid overdose-reversing
3322 medication approved for over-the-counter dispensation by the federal Food and Drug
3333 Administration or its successor organization with or without a prescription.
