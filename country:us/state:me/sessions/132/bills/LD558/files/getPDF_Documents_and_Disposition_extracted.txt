Section 1:
132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 558
S.P. 237 In Senate, February 25, 2025
Report of Medical Debt on Consumer Reports
Received by the Secretary of the Senate on February 19, 2025. Referred to the Committee
on Health Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered
printed.
DAREK M. GRANT
Secretary of the Senate
Presented by Senator BAILEY of York.
Cosponsored by Representative MATHIESON of Kittery and
Senators: President DAUGHTRY of Cumberland, HICKMAN of Kennebec, RENY of
Lincoln, TIPPING of Penobscot.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 10 MRSA §1310-H, sub-§4, as enacted by PL 2019, c. 77, §2, is amended
3 to read:
4 4. Reporting of medical expenses on a consumer report. Notwithstanding any
5 provision of federal law, a consumer reporting agency shall comply with the following
6 provisions with respect to the reporting of medical expenses on a consumer report.
7 A. A consumer reporting agency may not report debt from medical expenses on a
8 consumer's consumer report when the date of the first delinquency on the debt is less
9 than 180 days prior to the date that the debt is reported.
10 B. Upon the receipt of reasonable evidence from the consumer, creditor or debt
11 collector that a debt from medical expenses has been settled in full or paid in full, a
12 consumer reporting agency:
13 (1) May not report that debt from medical expenses; and
14 (2) Shall remove or suppress the report of that debt from medical expenses on the
15 consumer's consumer report.
16 C. As long as the consumer is making regular, scheduled periodic payments toward
17 the debt from medical expenses reported to the consumer reporting agency as agreed
18 upon by the consumer and medical provider, the consumer reporting agency shall
19 report that debt from medical expenses on the consumer's consumer report in the same
20 manner as debt related to a consumer credit transaction is reported.
21 SUMMARY
2222 This bill prohibits a consumer reporting agency from reporting debt from medical
2233 expenses on a consumer's consumer report.
