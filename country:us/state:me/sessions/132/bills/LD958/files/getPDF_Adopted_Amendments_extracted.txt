12 SUMMARY
1133 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, eliminates the
1144 provision of the Act to Implement the Maine Indian Claims Settlement imposing
1155 restrictions on the Passamaquoddy Tribe and the Penobscot Nation when they reinvest
1166 funds received as compensation for a taking of land within respective Indian territories for
1177 public uses under federal law.
18 The amendment also limits the applicability of the bill. Under the amendment, only
19 those trust lands of the Passamaquoddy Tribe, the Penobscot Nation, the Houlton Band of
20 Maliseet Indians and the Mi'kmaq Nation as of the date that the relevant tribal government
21 or governments certify approval of this legislation may not be taken for public uses under
22 the laws of this State.
