11 Amend the bill by striking out all of sections 4 and 5 and inserting the following:
12 'Sec. 4. 5 MRSA §13083-I, sub-§2, as enacted by PL 2005, c. 599, §1, is amended
13 to read:
14 2. Membership; appointment. The authority is governed by a board of trustees
15 composed of 11 12 voting members appointed pursuant to paragraphs C and D by the
16 Governor and subject to review by the joint standing committee of the Legislature having
17 jurisdiction over economic development matters and to confirmation by the Senate and one
18 additional trustee who serves as a voting member appointed pursuant to paragraph F by the
19 Governor.
20 A. Trustees Except for the trustee appointed pursuant to paragraph F, trustees are
21 appointed for 4-year terms, except that, for initial appointments, 3 trustees are
22 appointed to one-year terms, 3 trustees are appointed to 2-year terms, 2 trustees are
23 appointed to 3-year terms, 2 trustees are appointed to 4-year terms and the
24 commissioner commissioners designated pursuant to paragraph D serves serve at the
25 pleasure of the Governor. A vacancy must be filled in the same manner as the original
26 appointment for the balance of the unexpired term.
27 B. A trustee continues to hold office until a successor is appointed and qualified, but
28 the term of the successor is not altered from the original expiration date of the holdover
29 trustee's term.
30 C. The Governor shall make 10 appointments appoint 10 trustees, who serve as voting
31 members, of which no fewer than 7 must be from candidates who are residents of
32 Androscoggin County, Cumberland County and Sagadahoc County and are nominated
33 by the primary impact communities at least 3 are residents of Brunswick, Brunswick
34 town officials or Brunswick business owners and at least one is a resident of Topsham,
35 a staff member of the Topsham town administration or a Topsham business owner.

16 number to read consecutively.
17 SUMMARY
1188 This amendment amends the bill by requiring there to be at least one Topsham resident,
1199 Topsham town administration staff member or Topsham business owner on the board of
2200 trustees of the Midcoast Regional Redevelopment Authority and reduces to 3 the minimum
2211 number of trustees that are residents of Brunswick or Brunswick town officials or
2222 Brunswick business owners. The amendment also directs the Governor to appoint a trustee
2233 member, who serves a 4-year term, from a list of preapproved candidates provided by the
2244 Brunswick town council and removes the trustee appointed annually by the chair of the
2255 town council. The amendment removes the nonvoting member who is a Senator or
2266 Representative representing Brunswick or Topsham. The amendment maintains the
2277 inclusion of the authority under the Maine Tort Claims Act, which was repealed by the bill.
