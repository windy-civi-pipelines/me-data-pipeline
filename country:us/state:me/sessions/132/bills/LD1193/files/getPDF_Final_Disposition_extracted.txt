2 Date: (Filing No. S- )
3 STATE AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 SENATE
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 482, L.D. 1193, “An Act to Require the
10 Legislature to Elect Constitutional Officers and the State Auditor in Convention with an
11 Open Ballot System”
12 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 2 and inserting the following:
13 'Sec. 2. 5 MRSA §81, as amended by PL 1977, c. 674, §1, is further amended by
14 enacting at the end a new paragraph to read:
15 A convention that is held by the Legislature to elect the Secretary of State pursuant to
16 the Constitution of Maine, Article V, Part Second, Section 1 must be open to the public
17 and, notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the vote of each voting Senator
18 and Representative must be recorded and made available for public inspection in such a
19 manner that it is clear how each individual member voted.'
20 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 4 and inserting the following:
21 'Sec. 4. 5 MRSA §121, as amended by PL 2005, c. 683, Pt. C, §1, is further amended
22 by enacting at the end a new paragraph to read:
23 A convention that is held by the Legislature to elect the Treasurer of State pursuant to
24 the Constitution of Maine, Article V, Part Third, Section 1 must be open to the public and,
25 notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the vote of each voting Senator and
26 Representative must be recorded and made available for public inspection in such a manner
27 that it is clear how each individual member voted.'
28 Amend the bill in section 5 in §191-A by striking out all of the first indented paragraph
29 (page 1, lines 23 to 26 in L.D.) and inserting the following:
30 'A convention that is held by the Legislature to elect the Attorney General pursuant to
31 the Constitution of Maine, Article IX, Section 11 must be open to the public and,
32 notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the vote of each voting Senator and
33 Representative must be recorded and made available for public inspection in such a manner
34 that it is clear how each individual member voted.'

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to S.P. 482, L.D. 1193
1 Amend the bill in section 7 in the first indented paragraph in the 7th line (page 2, line
2 1 in L.D.) by striking out the following: "and the vote" and inserting the following: 'and,
3 notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the vote'
4 Amend the bill in section 7 in the first indented paragraph in the 8th line (page 2, line
5 2 in L.D.) by inserting after the following: "inspection" the following: 'in such a manner
6 that it is clear how each individual member voted'
7 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section
8 number to read consecutively.
9 SUMMARY
1100 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, amends the bill by
1111 specifying that conventions held by the Legislature to elect the Secretary of State, the
1122 Treasurer of State, the Attorney General and the State Auditor must be open to the public
1133 and, notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the vote of each voting Senator
1144 and Representative must be recorded and made available for public inspection in such a
1155 manner that it is clear how each individual member voted.
