1 L.D. 946
2 Date: (Filing No. H- )
3 ENERGY, UTILITIES AND TECHNOLOGY
5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 611, L.D. 946, “Resolve, to Increase
10 Access to Energy Efficiency Programs by Low-income and Moderate-income Residents”
11 Amend the resolve by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following:
12 'Sec. 1. Electric vehicle rebates. Resolved: That the Efficiency Maine Trust shall
13 ensure at least 1/2 of all funding available for electric vehicle rebates during the period of
14 the trust's 6th triennial plan developed by the trust in accordance with the Maine Revised
15 Statutes, Title 35-A, section 10104, subsection 4 is distributed to low-income and
16 moderate-income residents of this State.
17 Sec. 2. Energy efficiency programs. Resolved: That the Efficiency Maine Trust
18 shall ensure at least 1/2 of all funding available for incentives for weatherization and heat
19 pumps for dwellings in the State during the period of the trust's 6th triennial plan developed
20 by the trust in accordance with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 10104,
21 subsection 4 is distributed for low-income and moderate-income residents of this State.'
22 Amend the resolve by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or
23 section number to read consecutively.
24 SUMMARY
2255 The amendment replaces the resolve. It requires the Efficiency Maine Trust, during
2266 the period of the trust's 6th triennial plan, to ensure that at least 1/2 of all available funding
2277 for electric vehicle rebates is distributed to low-income and moderate-income residents of
2288 the State and at least 1/2 of all available funding for incentives for weatherization and heat
2299 pumps for dwellings in the State is distributed for low-income and moderate-income
3300 residents of this State.
