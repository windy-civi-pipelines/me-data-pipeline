2 Date: (Filing No. H- )

5 STATE OF MAINE
6 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
7 132ND LEGISLATURE
8 FIRST SPECIAL SESSION
9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT “ ” to H.P. 46, L.D. 82, “An Act to Amend the
10 Workers' Compensation Laws by Extending Indefinitely the Presumption Applying to Law
11 Enforcement Officers, Corrections Officers, E-9-1-1 Dispatchers, Firefighters and
12 Emergency Medical Services Persons Diagnosed with Post-traumatic Stress Disorder”
13 Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the following:
14 'Mandate preamble. This measure requires one or more local units of government
15 to expand or modify activities so as to necessitate additional expenditures from local
16 revenues but does not provide funding for at least 90% of those expenditures. Pursuant to
17 the Constitution of Maine, Article IX, Section 21, 2/3 of all of the members elected to each
18 House have determined it necessary to enact this measure.
19 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not
20 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
21 Whereas, the presumption applying to law enforcement officers, corrections officers,
22 E-9-1-1 dispatchers, firefighters and emergency medical services persons diagnosed with
23 post-traumatic stress disorder in the workers' compensation laws is necessary for the
24 recruitment and retainment of professionals employed in these career fields; and
25 Whereas, the presumption is set to expire on October 1, 2025, which may be before
26 the 90-day period has ended; and
27 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within
28 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as
29 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now,
30 therefore,'
31 Amend the bill by adding before the summary the following:
32 'Emergency clause. In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation
33 takes effect when approved.'
