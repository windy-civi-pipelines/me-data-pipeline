132nd MAINE LEGISLATURE
FIRST REGULAR SESSION-2025
Legislative Document No. 82
H.P. 46 House of Representatives, January 8, 2025
Indefinitely the Presumption Applying to Law Enforcement
Officers, Corrections Officers, E-9-1-1 Dispatchers, Firefighters and
Emergency Medical Services Persons Diagnosed with Post-
traumatic Stress Disorder
Received by the Clerk of the House on January 6, 2025. Referred to the Committee on
Labor pursuant to Joint Rule 308.2 and ordered printed pursuant to Joint Rule 401.
ROBERT B. HUNT
Clerk
Presented by Representative MATHIESON of Kittery.
Cosponsored by Senator LAWRENCE of York and
Representatives: Speaker FECTEAU of Biddeford, LAJOIE of Lewiston, MEYER of Eliot.
Printed on recycled paper
1 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2 Sec. 1. 39-A MRSA §201, sub-§3-A, ¶B, as amended by PL 2023, c. 405, Pt. A,
3 §143, is further amended by repealing the last blocked paragraph.
4 SUMMARY
55 Under current law, within the provisions governing entitlement to receive
66 compensation and services under the Maine Workers' Compensation Act of 1992, there is
77 a rebuttable presumption that the condition of post-traumatic stress disorder arose out of
88 and in the course of a worker's employment if that worker is a law enforcement officer,
99 corrections officer, E-9-1-1 dispatcher, firefighter or emergency medical services person
1100 diagnosed as having post-traumatic stress disorder that resulted from work stress, as long
1111 as certain specified conditions are met. This rebuttable presumption is scheduled to be
1122 repealed on October 1, 2025.
13 This bill repeals that repeal, continuing the rebuttable presumption indefinitely.
